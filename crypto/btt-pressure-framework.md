## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending **July 13 2026**, the framework reads **BTT at 0.00% net** — every sell row and every buy row is zero. The honest label here is a **static, fully-distributed token** whose supply does not move until the announced buyback-and-burn begins reporting.

## Sell pressure: where new BTT comes from

Nowhere — every sell row is zero, and structurally so. Sell #1, protocol inflation, is zero because BTT is **capped at 990T** and the full amount is already minted; there is no block reward in new BTT, no staking emission and no halving schedule, so the protocol cannot create a single additional token. Sell #2, vesting unlocks, is zero because the multi-year monthly airdrop that distributed BTT to TRON holders has **concluded** — no team, investor or airdrop cliff reaches the market in this window.

Sell #3, Foundation and unscheduled unlocks, is also zero for the window, though it is where the only overhang sits: about **2.96T BTT**, roughly **0.3% of the 990T cap**, remains outside circulation, held by the BitTorrent and TRON foundations. There is no dated release schedule for that reserve and the on-chain supply has held flat, so the framework books no net-new flow from it. Sell #4, long-term locked or bankruptcy, is zero: there is no bankruptcy estate and no court-ordered distribution attached to BTT.

## Buy pressure: where new BTT goes

Today the buy side is also all zeros, but this is the side that is about to change. Buy #1, programmatic buyback, is zero for now — yet BitTorrent has announced a long-term **buyback-and-burn program launching in Q3 2026** that directs **100% of the revenue** from its decentralized services to buy BTT on the market and send it to a burn address. Batch buybacks begin this quarter, but the first burn and its published size are not scheduled until **mid-October 2026** — just past this 90-day window — and no amount has been disclosed, so the framework books zero and keeps the row monitored rather than inventing a quantum.

Buy #2, protocol fee burn, is zero because BTT has no automatic base-fee burn; the token pays for gas on BitTorrent Chain, but those fees are not destroyed. Buy #3, Foundation buy, is zero — no foundation open-market buying has been observed, and the announced revenue buyback would be the first such flow once it begins. Buy #4, new long-term lock, is zero as a programmatic line: staking BTT on BitTorrent Chain for rewards keeps the tokens in circulating supply rather than removing them.

## Foundation and overhang

BTT carries one identified team-controlled overhang: the **~2.96T BTT** — about **0.3% of the 990T cap** — that is still outside circulation, held by the **BitTorrent and TRON foundations**. It sits on no published release schedule, and the monitor confirms the supply has held flat, so no scheduled flow is booked. Because circulating supply is already about 99.7% of the cap, this reserve is small relative to the float — a fully-distributed token with only a thin sliver still in foundation hands. If that reserve began moving BTT onto the market between refreshes, the outflow would enter Sell #3 at the next refresh. A second address to track from Q3 2026 onward is the buyback program's burn address, which will start receiving BTT once the first quarterly buyback completes.

## How BTT compares to other fixed-cap utility tokens

BTT belongs to the class of **fixed-cap utility tokens with no live emission** — the opposite structural shape to an uncapped, continuously-minting layer-1 like TRON's own TRX or Ethereum, which mint new coins to reward validators. BTT cannot mint at all: its 990T cap is fully distributed, so on the sell side it reads a clean zero where an uncapped chain reads a steady positive. That makes BTT structurally flatter than almost any proof-of-stake base layer, whose issuance keeps a small inflationary bias alive.

The closer analogue is an exchange or service token that **buys back and burns from revenue** — the model BNB pioneered with its quarterly burn. Until now BTT lacked that mechanism and simply sat flat; the **Q3 2026 buyback-and-burn** moves it toward the BNB-style deflationary shape, where operating revenue is converted into permanent supply reduction each quarter. The difference is scale and disclosure: BNB burns a large, scheduled amount, while BTT's burn is tied to decentralized-services revenue and has not yet published a size. Once the first burn lands in October 2026, BTT's reading should shift from flat toward mildly deflationary, in proportion to whatever revenue the program actually captures.
