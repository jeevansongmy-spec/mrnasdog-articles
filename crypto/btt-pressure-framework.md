BTT is a **static, fully-distributed** token.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 25 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **BTT at 0.00% net**: every sell row and every buy row is zero. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at about **+0.0002%** — effectively flat — so the gap between the two is roughly **zero percentage points** and **no monitor-gap chip** ships. There is no protocol inflation because BTT is not minted; there is no active unlock because the multi-year airdrop concluded years ago; and there is no buyback or material burn pulling coins back. BTT is a **fully-distributed, fixed-supply token whose float sits still**.

## Sell pressure: where new BTT comes from

There is no new BTT. Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**: BTT has a fixed cap of 990T and is not minted by the chain, so there is no block reward paid in fresh BTT. The chain's validators are paid from the existing supply, not from new issuance, which is the structural reason this row can never be positive without a tokenomics change.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero** in flow terms. The multi-year monthly airdrop to TRON holders ended back in 2020 after 16 rounds, so no scheduled cliff reaches the market in this window. A small partnership allocation vests slowly through 2028, but with roughly **99.7%** of supply already circulating, that residual is immaterial to the float. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero, with no dated discretionary release pending. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to BTT.

## Buy pressure: where new BTT goes

The buy side is equally quiet. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: there is no buyback program for BTT, and no protocol or Foundation buying is funded. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is also zero: a community burn design has been discussed on the governance forum, but it remains a roadmap proposal rather than a live, material on-chain reducer, so the framework carries it at zero and monitors it. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero, with no discretionary open-market buying and no new escrow announced in the window.

## Foundation and overhang

BTT has almost no live overhang. The token is roughly 99.7% circulating, so the classic risk — a large locked tranche cliff-unlocking into the market — has already largely played out. The one remaining scheduled item is a small partnership allocation that drips out through 2028; it is tiny relative to the 987T float and is monitored rather than booked as a flow. The framework books no discretionary release beyond that and re-checks the chain's supply and any governance burn proposals on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if a treasury balance falls faster than the schedule, or a burn goes live on-chain, the outflow enters Sell #3 — or the burn enters Buy #2 — at the next refresh.

## How BTT compares to other fixed-supply utility tokens

BTT belongs to the class of **fixed-supply, fully-distributed utility tokens** — closer to a hard-capped, no-emission token than to an inflationary proof-of-stake L1. Unlike an uncapped chain that mints a block reward every block, BitTorrent Chain pays its validators out of existing supply, so the cap of 990T is a true ceiling that the float is already pressed up against. There is no halving schedule to track, because there is nothing being issued to halve.

The contrast worth drawing is with the two more active token shapes. Against an emission chain, BTT has none of the steady dilution that a continuous mint produces — its float does not grow. Against an exchange token that buys back and burns aggressively, BTT has none of the structural deflation either — there is no revenue-funded burn pulling supply down. The result is a token that sits in the quiet middle: neither inflationary nor deflationary, just static.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the BitTorrent Chain governance forum for the burn-system proposal — if a material, recurring on-chain burn actually goes live, it would push the framework net negative and enter Buy #2. Watch the small partnership vesting tail through 2028, in case a tranche is ever large enough to register against the 987T float. Watch for any tokenomics change that would let the chain issue new BTT, which is the only path to a positive Sell #1. Absent any of those, expect the framework to keep reading flat and to stay in lock-step with our supply monitor.

## Summary

BTT is a fixed-supply utility and gas token whose 990T cap is already roughly 99.7% circulating. The chain mints no new BTT, the multi-year airdrop ended in 2020, and there is no buyback or material burn — leaving the framework at 0.00% net, in agreement with our supply monitor's near-zero read. The key risk is one-directional and low: a future on-chain burn could turn supply mildly deflationary, while there is no live mechanism that would make it inflationary. For now, BTT's supply simply sits still.
