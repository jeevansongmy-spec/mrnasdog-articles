## The verdict, in one paragraph

Over the last 90 days the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads SUI at **+2.44% net**: about **98.9M SUI** of new float against zero buy-side offset, on a circulating base of **4,052.33M SUI**. Our supply monitor reads the same trailing window at **+2.51%**, a gap of **0.07 percentage points** — comfortably inside tolerance, so this build ships **no monitor-gap chip** and needs no reconciliation walk. The two agree because Sui publishes its own month-by-month circulating-supply schedule and the framework books that schedule directly rather than inferring it. Looking forward, the framework reads **+1.63%** for the next 90 days. SUI is **structurally inflationary on the active float** — a hard-capped token whose supply cannot shrink, releasing a shrinking but still steady monthly tranche of previously locked coins.

## Sell pressure: where new SUI comes from

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the dominant force on Sui, at about **73.3M SUI** over the last 90 days and about **40.5M SUI** over the next. Sui does not release locked supply in lumpy annual cliffs; it steps circulating supply up once a month on a published calendar that runs to 2030, drawing from the community reserve, the Mysten Labs treasury, the early-contributor allocation and the Series A and Series B investor tranches. The single most important fact in this build is that the step got smaller: the monthly increase ran at roughly **52M SUI** through May 2026 and fell to roughly **22M SUI** from June 2026 onward, which is why the forward reading is barely two-thirds of the trailing one. One caution for readers comparing sources: public unlock trackers quote a much larger headline cliff for SUI — around **44M** for **Jul 1 2026** and again for **Aug 1 2026** — because they count the gross vesting-contract release event. Sui's own circulation schedule reports the net monthly increase at about **22M SUI**, and the framework follows the network's own number.

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the staking subsidy, and on Sui it is a finite pool rather than an open-ended mint. Reading the Sui mainnet system state directly on-chain, the network distributes about **282,430 SUI** per epoch, and one Sui epoch is exactly one day, which works out to about **25.7M SUI** over the last 90 days and about **25.4M SUI** over the next. The distribution amount steps down **1%** every 90 epochs by protocol rule, and the subsidy pool has only about **253M SUI** left in it — roughly two and a half more years at the current rate. This is the part of SUI inflation that is genuinely mechanical, genuinely predictable, and genuinely disappearing.

Sell #3 — foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is **zero** on this build, but the overhang behind it is not small. About **5.95B SUI** remains outside circulation. The framework books nothing here because every one of those buckets releases through the same published monthly calendar already counted in Sell #2, and no unscheduled foundation outflow was observed in the window. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is **zero** and structurally so: there is no bankruptcy estate, no trustee distribution schedule and no court-ordered release affecting SUI.

## Buy pressure: where new SUI goes

Every buy-side row on Sui is **zero**, and that is a structural property of the network rather than a gap in the research. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is zero because Sui operates no buyback of any kind: there is no protocol rule and no treasury programme that purchases SUI on the open market. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero because Sui explicitly does not burn. Computation gas is paid straight through to validators as staking rewards, and storage fees flow into a permanent storage fund whose principal is preserved and staked to pay for future storage. Sui's own tokenomics documentation states that SUI supply is non-decreasing and that tokens are never destroyed, which means the network has no mechanism analogous to an EIP-1559 base-fee burn.

Buy #3 — foundation buy — is zero: the Sui Foundation runs no open-market accumulation programme. A Nasdaq-listed corporate treasury has been buying and staking SUI in size, holding more than **108M SUI** as of its **May 4 2026** filing, but that is a third-party balance-sheet decision, not a project-side mechanism, so the framework does not book it as protocol buy pressure. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is zero for a subtler reason. The storage fund is the only mechanism on Sui that sets tokens aside indefinitely, and on-chain it holds only about **2.7M SUI** in total, well under **0.1%** of the float, part of which is refundable when on-chain objects are deleted. Staking is not a lock either: a Sui delegator can withdraw at the next daily epoch boundary, so staked SUI stays part of the circulating float.

## Foundation and overhang

The team-controlled overhang on Sui is large but unusually well documented. Roughly **5.95B SUI** — about **59.5%** of the fixed 10B total supply — sits outside circulation across five identifiable buckets: the community reserve at about **10.6%** of total supply, the stake-subsidy pool at about **9.5%**, the Series A and Series B investor allocations at about **7.1%** and **7.0%**, the early-contributor allocation at about **6.1%**, the community access programme at about **5.8%** and the Mysten Labs treasury at about **1.6%**, with the balance scheduled to release after 2030. The stake-subsidy pool is the one overhang readable directly on-chain: it holds about **253M SUI** and drains at a rule-fixed rate rather than a discretionary one.

How closely each bucket can be watched differs. The subsidy pool and the storage fund are read from the Sui mainnet system state at every rebuild, so any change is caught within a build cycle. The remaining allocations are tracked against the Sui Foundation's published circulation schedule, which is re-fetched on each rebuild and compared step by step against the previous read — that is how the June 2026 step-down from roughly 52M to roughly 22M a month was caught on this build. If any of these balances falls between refreshes faster than the published schedule allows, the excess outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh rather than being absorbed silently into the vesting row.

## How SUI compares to other uncapped-float layer-1 chains

SUI belongs to a specific structural class: the hard-capped, venture-funded layer-1 whose scarcity is real at the level of total supply but almost meaningless at the level of tradable float. Compared with a halving-model chain like Bitcoin, where the cap and the emission schedule are the same fact and the float converges on the cap, Sui's 10B cap tells you almost nothing about near-term supply — what matters is that only 40.5% of it is circulating and the rest arrives on a Foundation-published calendar. In that respect SUI reads much more like Aptos or Celestia than like Bitcoin or Litecoin: the dominant supply variable is a vesting calendar, not a block subsidy.

Against uncapped continuous-emission chains such as Solana or Cardano, the comparison inverts. Those networks mint genuinely new tokens every epoch, but they also anchor their inflation to a declining protocol curve that is fully predictable years out. Sui does have such a curve — the staking subsidy stepping down 1% every 90 epochs — but it is the smaller of Sui's two streams, roughly **25.4M SUI** of the **65.9M** forward total. The larger stream is discretionary in origin and only scheduled in execution, which makes the Sui Foundation's willingness to publish and adhere to its schedule the single most load-bearing governance fact about SUI supply.

The sharpest contrast is with fee-burn and buyback chains. Ethereum can post negative net issuance when base-fee burn exceeds validator rewards; BNB retires supply on a quarterly schedule. Sui has neither lever. Because computation gas goes to validators and storage fees are preserved rather than destroyed, SUI net supply cannot mathematically fall — the best case for SUI is a net figure approaching zero once the vesting calendar exhausts and the subsidy pool empties, not a deflationary print. That is the structural ceiling on how well SUI can ever score on this metric.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

The **Aug 1 2026** scheduled release adds about **22.2M SUI** to circulating supply, the **Sep 1 2026** step adds about **22.0M SUI**, and the **Oct 1 2026** step adds about **21.7M SUI** — three near-identical monthly steps with no cliff among them. Watch whether the realised circulating supply tracks those figures or overshoots them, because a divergence would mean the Foundation has amended its schedule. Watch the staking-subsidy step-down, which lands around **Oct 14 2026** when the distribution counter reaches its next 90-epoch boundary and the per-epoch amount falls another 1%. Watch for any Sui Improvement Proposal that would introduce a fee burn or alter the storage-fund rules, since that is the only route by which SUI could ever acquire a buy-side row. And watch the corporate-treasury bid: if a listed holder of more than 108M SUI began distributing rather than accumulating, it would not change the framework ledger but it would change the market that ledger describes.

## Summary

The MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads SUI as **structurally inflationary on the active float** at **+1.63% net** over the next 90 days, easing from **+2.44%** over the last 90 — a real improvement driven by Sui's monthly release step falling from about 52M to about 22M SUI in June 2026. The mechanism is entirely release-side: about **40.5M SUI** of scheduled unlocks plus about **25.4M SUI** of finite staking subsidy, against a buy ledger that is zero on every row because Sui neither buys back nor burns. The key risk is that **5.95B SUI** — nearly 60% of the fixed 10B cap — is still locked and arrives on a schedule the Sui Foundation itself can amend. The ceiling is equally clear: with no burn and no buyback anywhere in the protocol, SUI supply can flatten but can never shrink.
