## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **EDGE at −1.7% net** on the forward view — deflationary, because nothing unlocks while a daily buyback permanently burns EDGE. There is **no monitor cross-check** here: our supply monitor does not yet cover edgeX, so the end-check is deferred and the primary read is kept as-is. ## Sell pressure: where new EDGE comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**. EDGE has a fixed 1B max supply and no on-chain mint; staking and points rewards are paid out of pre-allocated buckets, not freshly created tokens. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is also **zero** for this window: the token generation event was March 31 2026, when 30% of supply (the Early Users cohort) unlocked, and every remaining cohort has its first cliff in 2027 or later.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a flow; the foundation allocation stays locked until its March 31 2027 cliff. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — carries zero flow but holds the real story: about **650M EDGE**, roughly two-thirds of supply, sits locked behind 2027 and 2028 cliffs, plus a team-pledged 140M one-year hold. None of it reaches the market before March 2027.

## Buy pressure: where new EDGE goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the active deflationary force, at about **6M EDGE** over 90 days. edgeX runs a daily buyback-and-**burn** funded by trading fees and net profit: repurchased EDGE is permanently destroyed, not parked in a treasury, with on-chain burn transactions published each day. Because the destination is a true burn, those coins are gone for good. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero: there is no separate gas or fee burn, since all fee-driven destruction runs through the daily buyback. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero; the 140M one-year team pledge made around the April 2026 airdrop is informational, not a new in-window lock event.

## Foundation and overhang

EDGE is a young, post-TGE token, and its overhang is large and dated. The locked cohorts are: Core Contributors at 25% (about **250M**, cliff around March 2028); Future Reserve at 30% (about **300M**, cliff around September 2027); Ecosystem & Community at 5% (about 50M, cliff around March 2027); Foundation at 5% (about 50M, cliff around March 2027); Liquidity at 5% (about 50M, terms undisclosed — an opacity flag); and Early Users at 30% (about 300M, already unlocked at TGE). The takeaway is blunt: roughly **65% of supply begins vesting in 2027**. For the next 90 days the framework books none of this as a flow, but the 2027 cliff wave is the dominant forward risk.

## How EDGE compares to other fee-burning exchange tokens

EDGE belongs to the class of **perp-DEX tokens with a fee-funded buyback-and-burn and a hard cap**. That sets it apart on two axes. First, contrast it with tokens whose buyback **stakes** the repurchased supply rather than burning it — there the coins survive and can re-enter the float later, while EDGE's burn is permanent destruction that genuinely removes supply. Second, contrast it with uncapped emission tokens that mint new supply continuously — EDGE has no mint at all, so its only supply moves are the burn down and the dated unlocks up.

The important caveat is maturity. EDGE is a young post-TGE token, so its current 90-day deflation sits in front of a heavy 2027 unlock schedule — a very different picture from a mature, fully-circulating burner whose float is stable. For an inflation lens, EDGE reads as deflationary right now precisely because the unlocks have not started yet; the mechanism is real, but the window is early.

And looking past 90 days, watch the first major cliff on March 31 2027, when roughly 65% of supply begins to vest — that is the event that flips the supply story from deflationary to a large unlock schedule.
