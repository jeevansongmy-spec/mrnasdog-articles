World (WLD) is a **fixed 10B-cap** token whose contract cannot mint or burn for its first 15 years, so all supply growth comes from immutable on-chain unlock contracts. Over the next 90 days those contracts release about **285.2M WLD** — community grants of **161.6M** plus team and investor vesting of **123.6M** — against **zero buyback and zero burn**, leaving the framework at about **+8.1% net**. Our supply monitor reads the last-90-day change at **+8.1%** realized, while our own scheduled-unlock math reads the last 90 days at **+13.0%** — a **4.9-percentage-point** gap that ships a warning chip.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending July 13 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **WLD at about +8.1% net** over the next 90 days — supply still growing fast. WLD has **no buyback and no fee burn**, so the only moving part is the unlock: immutable contracts release roughly **5.1M WLD a day** today, split into **3.2M** of community grants and **1.9M** of team-and-investor vesting. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day circulating change at **+8.1%** — about **264M** more WLD — whereas our own gross-unlock schedule for the same window reads **+13.0%**, about **459M**. That is a gap of roughly **4.9 percentage points**, so a **monitor-gap chip ships**: the daily unlock lands first in the community-grant pool and in held team wallets, which reach the tradable float more slowly than they leave the lock. Against a **3,531M** circulating base and a hard cap it cannot outrun on the buy side, WLD reads as **structurally inflationary, but cooling**.

## Sell pressure: where new WLD comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the community-grant unlock, about **161.6M WLD** over the next 90 days. WLD cannot be minted; its 10B supply was fixed at launch on July 24 2023. What looks like inflation is really the immutable on-chain unlock contract releasing pre-locked **Community tokens** — the network and user grants paid to verified humans — at about **3.2M WLD a day**, stepping down to **1.6M a day** on July 24 2026. This is the larger of the two supply drivers and the one closest to a classic emission.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the **Tools for Humanity team and investor** allocation, about **123.6M WLD** over the next 90 days. It vests from the same immutable contracts at about **1.9M WLD a day**, stepping down to **1.3M a day** on July 24 2026 after roughly **80%** of these tokens had their lock extended from three years to five years. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is **zero** as a flow: there is a large still-locked pool and a World Foundation grant reserve, but nothing releases on a discretionary schedule outside the fixed daily unlock already counted above. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is **zero**, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to WLD.

## Buy pressure: where new WLD goes

There is none. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: the WLD contract cannot mint or burn for its first 15 years, and no protocol revenue is routed into open-market WLD purchases. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is **zero** for the same structural reason: with burning disabled at the contract level, nothing on the buy side offsets the daily unlock. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is **zero**, with no observed open-market purchase by the World Foundation or Tools for Humanity. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is **zero** as an in-window event; the extension of ~80% of team and investor tokens from a three-year to a five-year lock is already in effect and shows up as the July 24 2026 unlock-rate cut, not as a fresh lock. The result is a one-sided ledger: WLD adds supply every day and removes none.

## Foundation and overhang

WLD's overhang is unusually large because roughly **65% of the 10B cap is still locked** — about **6.5B WLD** — and it all releases on the same immutable linear schedule counted in Sell #1 and Sell #2. Beyond the still-locked pool, the tracked team-controlled buckets are the **World Foundation community-grant and ecosystem pool** (unlocked community tokens that are only counted as circulating once verified users claim them), the **held Tools for Humanity team and investor tokens** that have unlocked but not yet reached the market, and the **~120M TFH reserve**. None of these carries a discretionary dated release in the window, so the framework books them at zero flow and enumerates them only as monitored scope. If any of these balances were to fall between refreshes, that outflow would enter Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How WLD compares to other fixed-cap unlock tokens

WLD belongs to the class of **fixed-cap tokens on a long linear unlock schedule with no buy-side sink** — the same structural family as many large infrastructure and identity tokens that pre-minted their full supply and now drip it out over years. What separates WLD from an exchange token like BNB or OKB is the buy side: those tokens pair emission or a fixed cap with a revenue-funded buyback-and-burn that removes supply, so their net can turn deflationary. WLD has **no such offset** — the contract is barred from burning for 15 years — so its unlock is pure one-way pressure until the schedule itself tapers.

The contrast worth drawing is with an uncapped proof-of-stake L1. A staking chain mints new coins every block with no ceiling, but often runs a fee burn that claws some back; WLD is the mirror image — it can **never exceed 10B**, but nothing is ever removed, so every unlocked token is permanent float. That makes WLD's inflation entirely front-loaded and predictable: it is highest now, at about **5.1M a day**, and mechanically declines as the linear schedule runs toward 2038. The July 24 2026 step-down — community grants halving to **1.6M a day** and team-investor vesting easing to **1.3M a day** — is the clearest example of that taper, and it is why the framework's next-90-day read (**+8.1%**) already sits well below its last-90-day read (**+13.0%**).

## What to watch in the next 90 days

The single dated event is the **−43% unlock-rate cut on July 24 2026**, when the total daily release drops from about **5.1M** to **2.9M WLD**. Watch how the market absorbs the first weeks at the lower rate — because there is no buyback, spot demand is the only thing that offsets the unlock, so a soft demand tape leaves the full unlock as net sell pressure. Watch the community-grant claim rate, which is what actually moves our supply monitor: the wider the gap between grants unlocking and grants being claimed, the more our scheduled-unlock read will run above the monitor. Watch the World Foundation for any change to the grant program or any new distribution from the reserve. And expect the monitor-gap chip to persist as long as unlocked tokens keep reaching the tradable float more slowly than they leave the immutable lock.
