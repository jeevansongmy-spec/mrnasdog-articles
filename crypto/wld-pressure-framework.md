## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 24 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **WLD at about +9.4% net** on the forward view — heavy, structural supply growth driven entirely by a daily unlock schedule with no offset. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+11.4%**, versus the framework's **+13.2%** gross-unlock read for the same window — a gap of about **1.85 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The gap is structural: the WLD contract cannot mint or burn for its first 15 years, so every new coin is a scheduled unlock, but part of each day's unlock lands in recipient and disclosed-holder wallets that the circulating classification absorbs more slowly than the gross unlock. WLD is **structurally inflationary by unlock schedule**, with no buy-side brake.

## Sell pressure: where new WLD comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the community grant stream, at about **288M WLD** over the last 90 days. World distributes new WLD to verified humans every day from the 75% community pool, at roughly **3.2M a day**. On **Jul 24 2026** that community rate is cut in half to 1.6M a day under the immutable on-chain unlock contracts, so the next 90 days adds only about **192M** from this row instead of 288M.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the investor and team stream, at about **171M WLD** over the last 90 days. The TFH investor and team allocations unlock linearly at roughly **1.9M a day**; the same Jul 24 2026 step cuts this rate 32% to 1.3M a day, so the next 90 days adds about **135M**. Together, Sell #1 and Sell #2 are the entire engine: about 459M unlocked over the last 90 days, easing to 327M over the next 90.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is booked at **zero** as a forward flow. The Foundation's issuance arm did a one-off OTC sale of about **239M WLD** ($65M) of already-unlocked reserve to four buyers, but that sits outside the daily schedule and has no published next-90-day timing, so the framework does not project it forward. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to WLD.

## Buy pressure: where new WLD goes

There is no buy pressure to report, and that is the headline. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: the WLD contract cannot mint or burn for its first 15 years, and World runs no program buying WLD back off the market. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is **zero**: World Chain fees do not destroy WLD, so no fee burn offsets the unlock. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is zero, and in fact the Foundation has been a net seller of reserve, not a buyer. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is zero; the Jul 24 2026 change slows unlocking but does not re-lock supply. With every buy row at zero, the unlock schedule passes straight through to net inflation.

## Foundation and overhang

About 65% of the 10B max supply is still locked, so the overhang is large by design. The two tracked discretionary overhangs are the **World Foundation / World Assets reserve**, which recently sold ~239M WLD OTC, and a disclosed **~283M WLD** holding by a public company — the largest publicly disclosed institutional holder, at roughly 8% of circulating supply. Neither carries a published forward schedule, so both are enumerated as watch items rather than projected as flow. The framework re-checks the unlock schedule and these balances on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if the Foundation reserve or a disclosed holder's balance falls between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How WLD compares to other uncapped-float unlock tokens

WLD belongs to the class of **fixed-cap tokens with a long, front-loaded unlock schedule** — closer to a multi-year vesting release than to a halving-model coin or a fee-burning exchange token. Like other large 2023–2024 launches with most supply locked at genesis, WLD's circulating float grows fast in the early years as the schedule releases community, investor and team allocations. Unlike a halving-model chain, there is no fixed cap on the rate that's already been reached; unlike an exchange token, there is no revenue-funded buyback to lean against the unlock.

The contrast worth drawing is with tokens that pair emission with a burn or buyback and can run net-flat or net-deflationary. WLD has neither, so the unlock is the whole story and net inflation stays firmly positive. What changes the trajectory is not a buy-side mechanism but the schedule itself: the Jul 24 2026 step-down cuts the aggregate unlock rate 43%, which is why the framework's forward read (+9.4%) sits below its trailing read (+13.2%). The direction is still up — just less steeply.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the World Foundation / World Assets reserve for another OTC or treasury sale like the recent ~239M block, which would push Sell #3 above zero. Watch the disclosed ~283M public-company holding for any change in position. And watch for any World ID revenue model that the project could one day route into a buyback or lock — today there is none, and until there is, the unlock schedule has no offset.

## Summary

WLD is a fixed 10B-supply token whose circulating float grows on a long unlock schedule, with no buyback and no burn to offset it. World unlocks about 327M WLD over the next 90 days — community grants plus investor and team vesting — leaving the framework at about +9.4% net new supply, against a realized +11.4% on our supply monitor. The key risk is the sheer pace of unlocking with no buy-side brake; the key relief is the Jul 24 2026 rate cut that lowers the aggregate unlock 43%. WLD stays heavily inflationary by structure, easing in degree but not in direction.
