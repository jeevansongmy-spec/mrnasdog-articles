## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 24 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **ATOM at about +2.5% net** on the forward view, driven entirely by protocol staking inflation with no buy-side offset of any kind. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+2.99%**, against the framework's **+2.89%** read for the same window — a gap of about **0.1 percentage points**, well inside tolerance, so **no monitor-gap chip** ships. The agreement holds because ATOM has no locked team or investor buckets, so total supply equals circulating supply, and every newly minted coin reaches the counted float. ATOM is **inflationary by continuous staking emission**, with no burn or buyback to brake it.

## Sell pressure: where new ATOM comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the whole story, at about **12.7M ATOM** over the next 90 days. The Cosmos Hub mints new ATOM every block as staking rewards; the on-chain mint module reports inflation at exactly **10%** a year, pinned at the ceiling that governance set in November 2023 when it cut the maximum rate from **14% to 10%**. The rate stays at that cap because the bonded ratio (~61% of supply staked) sits below the protocol's 67% target, so the dynamic band keeps issuance at the top. New ATOM is paid to validators and delegators, with a 2% community tax routed to the community pool — but all of it is newly issued supply, so it counts once, here.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero**: every genesis, foundation and early-backer allocation finished vesting years ago, so ATOM is fully circulating and no cliff hits the market. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also zero as a flow; the community pool accrues a small share of rewards but has no scheduled deployment that reaches the open market. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate applies and bonded ATOM is withdrawable after a 21-day unbonding period rather than locked long-term.

## Buy pressure: where new ATOM goes

The buy ledger is empty, and that is the defining feature of ATOM's inflation profile. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: staking rewards are paid from new emission, not by purchasing ATOM back from the market. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is also zero: the Cosmos Hub does not burn base fees, so transaction fees flow to validators and the community pool rather than being destroyed, and nothing is removed from supply. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero, with no discretionary open-market buying or new escrow announced in the window. With no offset at all, every minted ATOM is net new supply.

## Foundation and overhang

ATOM has no classic unlock overhang — the token is fully distributed, with total supply equal to circulating supply. The one pool worth naming is the community pool, which accrues the 2% community tax on staking rewards each block and can be deployed only by a passing governance vote. It is not a stockpile waiting to dump; deployments are infrequent, vote-gated, and historically modest. The framework books no discretionary release beyond protocol inflation and re-checks the on-chain mint rate and supply on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if the community pool balance falls faster than its accrual between refreshes, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How ATOM compares to other uncapped proof-of-stake chains

ATOM belongs to the class of **uncapped proof-of-stake L1s with continuous staking emission** — there is no hard cap and no halving schedule, just a dynamic inflation band that mints rewards to keep the chain secured. Among that class, ATOM sits at the higher-emission end: its 10% yearly rate is well above the low-single-digit emission of many newer L1s, and unlike chains that pair issuance with a base-fee burn, the Cosmos Hub destroys nothing. That makes ATOM a clean example of pure dilution — new supply enters, nothing leaves.

The contrast worth drawing is with capped, halving-model coins and with fee-burning chains that can go net-deflationary. ATOM does neither: it has no ceiling to approach and no burn to offset the mint. For an inflation lens specifically, that means ATOM reads as steadily, moderately inflationary — the staking emission is the only force on the page, and it points in one direction. The 2025-2026 community tokenomics overhaul is debating exactly this, but as of this read it is a proposal, not a protocol change.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the ATOM tokenomics overhaul — the community has been studying a fee-linked variable inflation model and lock-based staking multipliers, and any ratified proposal would lower the mint and could add the first buy-side row in ATOM's history. Watch the bonded ratio: if it climbs back above the 67% target, the dynamic band would start easing inflation below 10%, and if it falls further, the rate simply stays pinned at the cap. Watch the on-chain mint rate itself, the single number that decides Sell #1. And expect the framework to keep tracking our supply monitor closely, because ATOM's total supply equals its circulating supply — there is no hidden lockup to create a gap.
