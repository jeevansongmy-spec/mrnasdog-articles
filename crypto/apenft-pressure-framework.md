## How NFT compares to other fixed-supply tokens

NFT resembles a capped token more than an emitting network token. An uncapped proof-of-stake chain can have a standing issuance stream that needs to be measured every quarter, while NFT’s live contract begins with a fixed supply and has no observed inflation flow in this review. Bitcoin is also capped, but its remaining issuance follows a known halving schedule; NFT has no comparable scheduled block-reward stream to place into Sell #1.

It also differs from exchange tokens whose regular revenue-funded repurchases or quarterly burns produce a repeatable Buy #1 or Buy #2 entry. NFT’s public burn balance confirms that removal can happen, but the present evidence does not establish a continuous fee burn, a periodic buyback, or a dated future action. Structurally, that makes it neutral by default rather than predictably deflationary. The useful comparison is about mechanism, not price: fixed cap plus no scheduled flow is a different supply profile from ongoing issuance or recurring burns.
