## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window opening June 24 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **APT at about +4.7% net** on the forward view, driven by monthly vesting unlocks that dwarf the new, smaller staking emission. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+4.82%**, versus the framework's **+4.70%** read for the same window — a gap of only about **0.1 percentage points**, well within tolerance, so no monitor-gap chip is needed. The numbers agree because Aptos's vesting unlocks land directly in circulating supply and there is no buyback or material burn to absorb them. APT is **structurally inflationary at a mid-single-digit pace**, with the unlock cycle the single biggest force.

## Sell pressure: where new APT comes from

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the dominant force, at about **33.9M APT** over the next 90 days. Aptos releases monthly cliffs of roughly **11.3M APT** to early investors and core contributors on the 12th of each month, so three firings — July 12, August 12 and September 12 2026 — fall inside this window. This is the largest single driver of APT inflation, and it runs until the four-year unlock cycle ends in October 2026, after which monthly releases drop sharply.

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — adds about **5.3M APT** from staking rewards. Early-2026 governance cut the staking reward rate from 5.19% to **2.6%** a year, so the emission is now a fraction of the unlock flow; new APT mints each epoch and is paid to validators and delegators. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is **zero** as a flow: the Foundation permanently staked and locked 210M APT that can never be sold, and no other dated treasury release is scheduled. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where new APT goes

The buy side is essentially empty. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: Aptos runs no buyback contract or treasury buying APT off the market. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is carried at zero too: early-2026 governance raised gas fees about tenfold and now burns **100% of base fees**, but at current network volume the burned amount is tiny and not publicly quantified, so the framework does not estimate it. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero; the 210M APT Foundation lock already executed before this window. With nothing buying back, the full gross emission flows through to net.

## Foundation and overhang

The headline overhang is the **210M APT** the Foundation permanently staked and locked — about 18% of circulating supply — which is now removed from the float and can never be sold or distributed, so it counts as a reduction in the overhang rather than a future seller. Beyond that, the remaining investor and core-contributor allocations are the live overhang, releasing on the monthly schedule that already feeds Sell #2 through October 2026. The framework re-checks the unlock calendar and chain emission on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if a treasury wallet or vesting contract releases faster than the published schedule, the extra outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How APT compares to other uncapped proof-of-stake chains

APT belongs to the class of **continuous-emission proof-of-stake L1s** still working through a multi-year vesting tail — closer to a young, heavily-vesting chain than to a hard-capped, halving-model coin. What sets the current window apart is that staking emission is no longer the main story: the 2.6% reward rate is small, and the monthly unlocks are roughly six times larger. So unlike a chain whose inflation is purely protocol emission, APT's near-term dilution is dominated by the vesting calendar — a schedule that will fall off a cliff in October 2026.

Against chains that pair emission with a real fee burn or buyback, APT stands out for having almost no offset: the gas burn exists but is immaterial, and there is no buyback at all. That makes APT read as clearly inflationary today, where a fee-burning chain might net closer to flat. The structural case for the bull is forward-looking — the proposed 2.1B hard cap, the lower staking rate, and the October 2026 unlock cliff all point to far less new supply next year — but for this 90-day window the unlocks dominate and the framework reads growth.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the three monthly unlocks on July 12, August 12 and September 12 2026 — each adds about 11.3M APT and is the single biggest swing factor for the reading. Watch for the October 2026 end of the investor and core-contributor unlock cycle, which is expected to cut annualized releases by roughly 60% and would flip APT toward a much lower inflation profile. Watch whether the proposed 2.1B hard cap is formally ratified on-chain, which would lock the supply ceiling. And watch the gas-fee burn: if Aptos volume grows enough to make the burn material, the buy side could finally start offsetting emission.

## Summary

APT is an uncapped proof-of-stake token whose near-term supply growth is driven by monthly vesting unlocks, not by protocol emission. Over the next 90 days the chain adds about 5.3M APT of staking rewards at the reduced 2.6% rate plus roughly 33.9M from three monthly unlocks, with no buyback and only an immaterial gas burn to offset it — leaving the framework at about +4.7% net. Our supply monitor reads +4.82% realized, in close agreement. The key risk is the unlock schedule, and the key relief is its end in October 2026, paired with the proposed 2.1B hard cap.
