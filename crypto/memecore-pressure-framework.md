MemeCore's native token M mints **30 M per block** at 7-second blocks — about **33M M every 90 days** — with no quantifiable burn offsetting it. Framework reading: **+2.50% net** on a ~1.32B circulating base. Beyond the emission, roughly 40% of the 10B max supply sits in reserves with no disclosed vesting schedule, and a **$10M+ Foundation treasury buyback** announced July 2 2026 is still unexecuted.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending July 6 2026, the framework reads **M at +2.50% net inflation** — continuous block-reward emission with nothing structural absorbing it. The aggregator monitor reads **−24.98%** over the same window, a **27.5-percentage-point** gap that triggers the ⚠ chip. The deep walk resolved the contradiction: the monitor's 30-day reading is **positive (+0.44%)** — matching the block-reward pace — while its 90-day reading is deeply negative, a sign flip that pinpoints a one-time mid-window reclassification of ~438M out of the upstream circulating count, not a real burn or lock. M is structurally inflationary on the active float.

## Sell pressure: where new M comes from

Sell #1 (protocol inflation) booked **~33M M** this window: the proof-of-meme chain mints **30 M per block** at 7-second blocks — roughly 370K M per day — mining the supply from the 5B genesis (Sep 9 2025) toward the 10B max. The reward splits 75% to M stakers, 24% to meme-token delegators, and 1% to block proposers, with 10% routed to a Viral Grants Reserve. Sell #2 (vesting unlocks) is **0** with a major caveat: the Foundation (15%), Core Contributors (13%) and Investors (12%) allocations carry **no publicly disclosed vesting timelines** — the docs state this explicitly and no dated cliff falls in the next 90 days — so the row ships at zero as an opacity, not a verified absence. Sell #3 (Foundation and unscheduled unlocks) is **0** with the full reserve stack enumerated below. Sell #4 (bankruptcy) is **0**.

## Buy pressure: where new M goes

Almost nowhere that is confirmed. Buy #1 (programmatic buyback) is **0**; there is no protocol-encoded buyback. Buy #2 (protocol fee burn) is **0** as a quantified row: a portion of gas fees is burned, but the project does not disclose the fraction and recycles part of fees into reward pools — the burn base is tiny and unquantifiable. Buy #3 (Foundation buy) is where the newest event lands: on **July 2 2026** the Foundation announced a strategic treasury buyback of **at least $10M** after the late-June crash. It ships at **0** because the execution timing and method are deliberately undisclosed and no on-chain buyback is confirmed — a plan, not yet a flow — and because the repurchased M goes to the Foundation treasury wallet (accumulation, not burn), any executed buyback also enlarges the overhang rather than destroying supply. Buy #4 (new long-term lock) is **0** for M itself; new meme-token launches route a slice of their initial supply into a 1,000-day reserve vault, but that locks the new tokens, not M.

## Foundation and overhang

The overhang stack is the largest part of this token's risk profile. The tracked buckets, all without disclosed schedules: the **Foundation allocation (~1.5B M)**, the **Core Contributors allocation (~1.3B M)**, the **Investors allocation (~1.2B M)**, and the **Meme Treasury (~200M M)** plus a Viral Grants Reserve that accrues 10% of every epoch's rewards. Together that is roughly 4B M — three times the current circulating count — with no published release calendar. The **$10M+ treasury buyback** adds a new accumulation destination: repurchased M lands in the Foundation treasury, so it is a tracked overhang, not a burn. Each bucket is walked bi-weekly; if any balance falls between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

The reclassification episode is also a useful reminder of what circulating-supply numbers are and are not. Independent free-float analyses put MemeCore's genuinely tradeable supply far below the 1.32B headline count, because large staked and reserve balances sit between "technically transferable" and "actually trading," and on-chain investigators flagged concentrated insider supply during the June turmoil. The framework uses the standard circulating denominator so its percentages stay comparable across the catalog, but readers should know the effective float is thinner, which amplifies both the emission's and any future reserve release's market impact.

## How M compares to other uncapped-emission L1s

Mechanically, M behaves like a continuous-emission Layer 1 in its mining years — closer to a young proof-of-work chain than to its meme-token branding. Its ~2.5% per 90 days sits near Kaspa's pace and well above mature proof-of-stake chains like Cosmos Hub when normalised. The hard 10B cap puts a ceiling on the story — unlike ATOM's uncapped mint — but the cap is distant: roughly half the max remains to be mined.

The structural difference from fair-launch chains is the reserve stack. Litecoin or Kaspa carry zero team-controlled overhang; M carries ~4B in undisclosed-schedule allocations on top of its block-reward emission. Its buy side is also weaker than an exchange token's: where a Binance or OKX runs a disclosed, executed, burn-destination buyback every quarter, MemeCore's July 2026 buyback is undisclosed in timing and routes to treasury rather than a burn address — so it removes float without removing supply. The emission is predictable; the reserves and the buyback destination are not. That combination — coded mint plus opaque overhang — keeps this page's sell-side risk concentrated in the watch list rather than the ledger.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Five things move the framework reading. First, any **disclosure of vesting timelines** for the Foundation, Core Contributor or Investor buckets — a published schedule would move Sell #2 from opacity to a quantified row immediately. Second, **on-chain execution of the $10M+ buyback** — a verifiable repurchase would register as buy pressure, but because it accumulates in treasury it also becomes a Sell #3 overhang to watch. Third, any observed **outflow from the reserve buckets** — it enters Sell #3 at the next refresh. Fourth, a **burn-rate disclosure** — a quantified gas-burn fraction would activate Buy #2. Fifth, the upstream circulating count — this window's 27-point monitor gap came from a ~438M reclassification, and further re-tags would re-widen the chip; watch too whether the concentrated insider holdings flagged on-chain after the **~74% crash on Jun 25 2026** begin moving.

## Summary

M is the native asset of a meme-economy Layer 1 minting **30 M per block** toward a 10B max — about 33M per 90 days, a **+2.50% net** framework reading with no quantifiable offsetting flow. The larger story is what is not scheduled: roughly 4B M across Foundation, contributor, investor and treasury buckets with no disclosed vesting, three times the circulating count, plus a July 2026 treasury buyback that is announced but unexecuted and treasury-bound rather than burned. The emission is coded and predictable; the overhang is opaque and dominant. Until the project publishes schedules and executes on-chain, the framework reads the mint and watches the reserves.
