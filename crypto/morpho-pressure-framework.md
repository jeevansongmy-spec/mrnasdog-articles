## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 22 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **MORPHO at about +5.5% net** on the forward view, driven entirely by token vesting plus Morpho DAO treasury distributions, with no buy-side offset of any kind. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+17.67%** — roughly 97M new circulating MORPHO — versus the framework's **+5.6%** schedule-based read for the same window, a gap of about **12 percentage points** that ships a monitor-gap flag. The published vesting schedule explains about 26M of that 97M; the larger remainder is the Morpho DAO distributing its 35.4% governance treasury as grants and rewards, faster than the documented 4%-a-year floor we size it at. MORPHO is **structurally inflationary with no buyback to lean against**, at a mid-single-digit pace from the schedule and treasury alone.

## Sell pressure: where new MORPHO comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**. MORPHO is a fixed-supply governance token, not a network coin: there is no block reward and no staking emission, so the protocol mints nothing. Every new MORPHO that reaches the market was already minted at launch and is moving out of a locked or treasury bucket.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the first real flow, at about **26M MORPHO** over the next 90 days. Morpho's allocations vest linearly on a published schedule: user and launch-pool rewards, the strategic-partner cohorts, and early contributors all release gradually. The founders' 15.2% allocation is still inside its lockup until **May 17 2028**, so none of that large block reaches the market in this window — it stays a future overhang, not a current flow.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is the second flow, at about **10M MORPHO**. The Morpho DAO controls 35.4% of supply, roughly 354M MORPHO, and distributes it as grants and reward programs. Governance documents a rate above **4% of supply a year**, which is the number the framework books here. On-chain the DAO has actually been distributing faster than that floor, which is the source of the monitor gap below. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to MORPHO.

## Buy pressure: where new MORPHO goes

The buy side is empty. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: Morpho runs no buyback. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is also zero, because Morpho collects no protocol fee today. The protocol has a **fee switch** built into its contracts that governance could turn on, capped at 25% of the interest borrowers pay, but it is currently **off** — so there is no revenue stream to fund a buyback or a burn. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is zero, with no evidence of treasury open-market buying. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is zero in the window; a TradFi partner has been authorised to acquire up to **90M MORPHO** over four years, but no delivery is scheduled inside these 90 days. With every buy row at zero, nothing offsets the vesting and DAO distributions.

## Foundation and overhang

MORPHO carries one very large team-controlled overhang: the **Morpho DAO treasury**, about 354M MORPHO (35.4% of supply), which the DAO distributes through grants and reward programs. That is the source of Sell #3, and it is also the reason realized supply growth runs ahead of the published vesting schedule. Beyond it sits the **founders' 15.2% allocation**, locked until May 17 2028, and the remaining strategic-partner and contributor cohorts vesting through 2027. None of these are a fixed schedule the framework can fully predict, because the DAO distribution is discretionary. The framework re-checks the vesting schedule and DAO distribution on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if a treasury balance falls faster than the documented program rate, the extra outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How MORPHO compares to other governance tokens

MORPHO belongs to the class of **fixed-cap DeFi governance tokens** whose supply growth comes from vesting and treasury distribution rather than protocol emission. Unlike an uncapped proof-of-stake chain that mints block rewards forever, MORPHO has a hard 1B ceiling and no ongoing mint — so its inflation is finite and front-loaded into the unlock years. But unlike an exchange token that pairs distribution with an aggressive buyback-and-burn, MORPHO has **no buy-side mechanism at all** while its fee switch stays off. That combination is what makes the inflation read distinctly: the cap limits the total, but for now there is nothing pulling supply back, so every vested or distributed token is net new float.

The contrast worth drawing is with tokens that have flipped their fee switch on and route revenue into buybacks — those can go net-deflationary even while unlocks continue. MORPHO has the same structural option but has not used it, so its inflation lens is one-sided: the vesting schedule and the DAO's grant programs set the pace, and the only brake is the cap itself, years out. For an inflation read specifically, MORPHO is steadily inflationary until either the unlock schedule winds down or governance turns on a value-capture mechanism.
