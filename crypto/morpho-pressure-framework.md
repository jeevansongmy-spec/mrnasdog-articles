# MORPHO Inflation Analysis · July 2026 · Supply growing on unlocks and DAO grants

MORPHO is fixed at a **1B** hard cap with no mint function, so there is no protocol inflation — but the token is still deep in its distribution phase. Scheduled vesting adds about **25.8M MORPHO** to the float over the next 90 days and the Morpho DAO distributes roughly **9.9M** more as grants and rewards, and with **no buyback and no burn** to offset them, the framework reads a net of about **+5.5%**. Our supply monitor reads **+18.0%** over the last 90 days — a gap of about **12.4 percentage points** that raises a monitor-gap flag, because the DAO has been distributing its treasury faster than its documented rate.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window beginning July 8 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **MORPHO at about +5.5% net** on the forward view — roughly **25.8M MORPHO** of scheduled vesting plus about **9.9M** of Morpho DAO grant and reward distribution reaching the float, against nothing on the buy side. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+18.0%**, versus the framework's **+5.6%** read for the same window — a gap of about **12.4 percentage points** that **does raise a monitor-gap chip**. The published vesting schedule explains only about **26M** of the roughly **99M** the float actually grew; the larger remainder is the Morpho DAO handing out its ~354M governance treasury as grants and rewards faster than its documented 4%-a-year floor, which the monitor's data source counts as circulating. We size that distribution at the documented rate rather than the monitor figure, so the primary read stays. MORPHO is **structurally inflationary on the active float**, a capped token still working through a heavy distribution phase.

## Sell pressure: where new MORPHO comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**. MORPHO is fixed at a **1B** hard cap, its on-chain total supply already reads 1B, and there is no mint function above the cap and no staking emission, so the protocol issues nothing new. Every token that will ever exist already exists; the question is only how fast the locked portion reaches the market.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is about **25.8M MORPHO** over the next 90 days on the published linear schedule. The largest piece is users and distributors at about **16.2M**, followed by strategic partners at about **7.7M** and contributors at about **2M**. Earlier strategic cohorts are fully vested — Cohort 1 finished its three-year vest around December 2025 and the relocked Cohort 2 (16.8% of supply) completed on October 3 2025 — and the founder tranche of **152M** stays flat in this window, so scheduled vesting is actually the smaller of Morpho's two supply drivers.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is about **9.9M MORPHO**, and it is the more important driver. The Morpho DAO distributes its **~354M** governance treasury as grants and user rewards at a documented rate above **4%** of supply a year through two grant programs. We book that at the documented floor, about 9.9M over 90 days. On-chain the DAO has actually been distributing faster than the floor, which is the main reason the monitor reads more supply growth than the schedule alone implies. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is **zero**, because no bankruptcy estate or court-ordered distribution applies to MORPHO.

## Buy pressure: where new MORPHO goes

Every buy row is **zero** — this is the defining feature of MORPHO's inflation profile. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is zero: Morpho runs no token buyback, and protocol fees, when switched on, would accrue to the DAO treasury rather than buying MORPHO on the open market. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero: MORPHO has no fee-burn mechanism, and while governance can switch on a borrower-interest fee capped at 25%, that fee is off today and would route to the treasury, destroying no tokens.

Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is zero, with no treasury or Foundation open-market buying of MORPHO observed in the window. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is zero, with no new multi-year lock or escrow announced. With no buyback, no burn, and no new lock, there is nothing structural on the buy side to absorb the vesting and DAO distribution, so both flow straight through to net supply growth. A governance brief has flagged exactly this mismatch — token distribution above 4% a year while fee revenue stays inactive — as a sustainability concern.

## Foundation and overhang

Roughly **348M MORPHO** sits outside the circulating float, and most of it is team- or DAO-controlled. The largest bucket is the **Morpho DAO** governance treasury at about **354M** of the original allocation, the source of the grant and reward distribution in Sell #3. Alongside it are the **Morpho Association** reserve at about **63M** — which includes a 150M multi-year grant to the Association that only fully settles at the end of 2030 — the Reserve for Contributors at about **58M**, the Early Contributors allocation at about **49M**, and the still-locked founder tranche of **152M** that vests through 2028. These are the overhangs the framework watches on a roughly bi-weekly walk. If any of these balances falls between refreshes beyond the documented distribution rate, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How MORPHO compares to other DeFi governance tokens

MORPHO belongs to the class of **capped DeFi governance tokens whose supply growth is a distribution schedule, not an emission rate**. In that sense it resembles the early years of tokens like UNI or COMP — a fixed cap, a large share held by team, investors and a DAO treasury, and a multi-year unlock calendar that drives circulating supply upward long after launch. Where a continuous-emission Layer 1 mints new tokens forever, MORPHO can only release what already exists inside the 1B cap; the inflation is finite, but for now it is running fast because the treasury is being actively deployed.

The sharper contrast is with a capped DeFi token that runs a **buyback**, such as AAVE. AAVE is also capped and has finished vesting, and it uses protocol revenue to buy its token back off the market, so its net supply reads roughly flat to slightly deflationary. MORPHO is at the opposite point of the same cycle: its vesting is still live, its DAO is distributing treasury faster than the protocol earns fees, and it has no buyback or burn to offset either, so the net is firmly positive. The fee switch gives Morpho the option to turn revenue into an offset in future, but as configured today it routes to the treasury, not to reducing supply. Until either the distribution winds down or governance turns protocol revenue into a buyback or burn, MORPHO stays structurally inflationary while AAVE stays roughly steady — same capped-token category, opposite ends of the supply-pressure spectrum.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the DAO treasury distribution rate, the single biggest swing factor: the Morpho DAO's ~354M holding and its grant and reward programs decide how far above the documented 4%-a-year floor the real supply growth runs. Watch the scheduled vesting hold near 25.8M a quarter, led by the users-and-distributors and strategic-partner tranches. Watch the fee switch: a governance vote to turn on protocol fees, and especially any decision to route that revenue into a buyback or burn rather than the treasury, would be the first mechanism ever to push against MORPHO's supply growth. Watch the founder tranche of 152M, which stays flat for now but vests through May 2028. And watch whether the realized float growth stays near the recent few-million-a-month pace or reaccelerates the way it did in late April 2026.

## Summary

MORPHO is a capped 1B DeFi governance token on Ethereum with no mint, no buyback, and no burn — its supply growth is a published vesting schedule plus active DAO treasury distribution. Vesting adds about 25.8M MORPHO over the next 90 days and DAO grants and rewards about 9.9M, and with nothing on the buy side to offset them the framework reads about +5.5% net. Our monitor reads +18.0% realized over the last 90 days, because the DAO has been distributing its treasury faster than its documented floor; we size that flow at the documented rate and flag the gap. The key risk is a large team and DAO overhang deploying into a market with no structural buyer, and the one thing that could change the reading is a governance decision to route protocol revenue into a buyback or burn.
