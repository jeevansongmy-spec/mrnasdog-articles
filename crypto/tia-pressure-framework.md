## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **TIA at about +3.9% net** on the forward view. The surprise is what drives it: not the protocol mint, which governance has cut to roughly **2.5%** a year, but the daily release of early-backer and core-contributor tokens that started after the October 2024 cliff. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+4.28%**, versus the framework's **+3.9%** — a gap of about **0.3 percentage points**, inside tolerance, so no conflict flag ships. With **no buyback and no live fee burn** to offset, TIA is **solidly inflationary** for now — at a pace set mostly by the unlock schedule.

## Sell pressure: where new TIA comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the smaller of two forces, at about **5.7M TIA** over the next 90 days. Celestia mints new TIA every block as staking rewards. It launched at **8%** a year, and two governance upgrades have cut it sharply: the Lotus upgrade in mid-2025 dropped it toward **5%**, and the Matcha upgrade in late 2025 cut it again to roughly **2.5%**. The rate keeps easing each year toward a **1.5%** floor, so the mint is the part of TIA's supply story that is steadily shrinking.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the bigger force, at about **31M TIA** over 90 days. The seed, Series A&B and core-contributor allocations began unlocking after a one-year cliff in October 2024 and now release a little every day, roughly **10.3M TIA** a month in the current band, running through October 2027. That is more than five times the protocol mint, and it is the reason TIA still reads clearly positive even as the inflation rate falls. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero: the ecosystem and R&D buckets vest on the same published schedule rather than as ad-hoc drops. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where TIA goes

Buy pressure is the short part of this analysis, because there is none active. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: Celestia has floated a Proof-of-Governance plan that would pair a treasury-backed buyback-and-burn with a much lower mint near **0.25%**, but it still needs community approval and is not in effect in this window. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is also zero: data-availability fees are paid in TIA, but no recurring on-chain burn is live or quantified today. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero. The result is that every coin minted or unlocked reaches the float unopposed.

## Foundation and overhang

TIA's overhang is its vesting schedule, and it is large but transparent. The early-backer and core-contributor cohorts together hold a substantial share of supply that is still unlocking on a fixed, linear path through October 2027. Crucially, the heaviest year is already behind: the November 2024 to October 2025 band released around **30M TIA** a month, while the current band sits near **10.3M** and the final year steps down again toward **5M**. So the unlock pressure is real but decelerating. The framework books these as a continuous Sell #2 flow rather than a single cliff, and re-checks the monthly unlock pace and the chain's emission rate on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if a treasury balance falls faster than the schedule, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How TIA compares to other uncapped proof-of-stake chains

TIA belongs to the class of **uncapped proof-of-stake L1s** — no ceiling on supply, inflation funded by staking rewards. What sets it apart right now is the combination of a **falling mint** and a **still-heavy unlock schedule** stacked on top. Many comparable chains have either finished their investor vesting or never carried this much of it; TIA is in the middle of working through a venture-scale allocation, so its realized supply growth runs well above its nominal inflation rate. The two numbers will converge as vesting winds down toward late 2027.

The contrast worth drawing is with chains that pair emission with a burn. Some networks burn enough fees to go net-deflationary; TIA does not — its fee burn is not live, and its buyback is only a proposal. That is the key difference for an inflation lens: until Proof-of-Governance or a fee burn actually ships, TIA has no brake, so the unlock schedule sets the pace and the read stays firmly positive.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the monthly unlock pace — the ~10.3M TIA a month from vesting is the single biggest number, and it is the one that keeps net inflation near +3.9% rather than near the 2.5% mint. Watch the Proof-of-Governance vote, because if the community approves a treasury-backed buyback-and-burn alongside a sub-1% mint, TIA's entire supply picture flips from inflationary toward flat or deflationary. Note the emission rate's next step-down, which lowers the mint again on schedule. And watch for the unlock schedule's own deceleration into late 2026 and 2027, which gradually eases the dominant sell-side force without any governance action at all.
