Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+3.64%**, versus the framework's **+3.51%** read for the same window — a gap of about **0.13 percentage points**, inside the 0.5-point tolerance, so no monitor-gap chip is raised. AERO is **structurally inflationary on the active float**, at a steady low-single-digit pace.

## Sell pressure: where new AERO comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the whole story, at about **32.5M AERO** reaching the market over the next 90 days. Aerodrome runs the ve(3,3) model: new AERO is minted every weekly epoch as liquidity-gauge emissions, and veAERO voters decide which pools receive them. The gross mint is larger, but a large share is re-locked into veAERO and the weekly rebase is minted straight to lockers, so only the float slice counts here. The schedule decays roughly **1%** per epoch in the cruise phase, and the Aero Fed mechanism lets veAERO voters tune the rate by a small step each week — so the forward read sits just below the last-90-day pace.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero** as a discrete event: Aerodrome has no investor or team cliff hitting the market in this window. Its supply growth is the continuous gauge emission, not a scheduled unlock. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also zero as a separate flow; Foundation and team allocations accrue inside the weekly mint already captured in Sell #1. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to AERO.

## Buy pressure: where new AERO goes

Every buy row is **zero**, and that is the defining feature of AERO's inflation profile. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is zero: Aerodrome runs no token buyback. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero: there is no burn, because **100%** of trading fees are paid out to veAERO voters rather than used to retire supply. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is zero, with no discretionary open-market buying by the team or treasury in the window. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is zero as a booked offset: voters continuously re-lock AERO into veAERO, but that ongoing re-lock is already netted inside the Sell #1 float figure. With nothing on the buy side, the gauge emission flows straight through to net inflation.

## Foundation and overhang

AERO has no classic unlock overhang waiting to dump — its supply growth is the weekly emission itself. The structural overhang to watch is the **veAERO lock**: roughly 35–45% of circulating AERO is vote-escrow locked, and the weekly rebase mints fresh AERO straight to those lockers as anti-dilution. That locked pool is exactly why realized float grows slower than the gross mint — re-locked emissions and rebase mints never reach the market. The framework books no discretionary release beyond gauge emission and re-checks the weekly epoch emission and the veAERO lock ratio on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if locked AERO starts unwinding to the float faster than the schedule, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How AERO compares to other uncapped DEX tokens

AERO belongs to the class of **uncapped ve(3,3) DEX tokens** — the lineage that runs from Solidly through Velodrome on Optimism to Aerodrome on Base. These tokens share a defining trait: supply is uncapped and grows through continuous weekly gauge emissions, with no halving and no hard ceiling. Unlike an exchange token that funds quarterly buybacks from fee revenue, Aerodrome pays 100% of fees to veAERO voters and keeps zero for the protocol — so there is no buy-side mechanism to lean against the mint.

The contrast worth drawing is with fee-burning tokens that go net-deflationary. A chain with an active base-fee burn or a buyback-and-burn can offset or even reverse its issuance; AERO has neither, so its gauge emission flows straight to net inflation. What softens the picture is the decaying schedule and the deep veAERO lock: the emission rate falls about 1% an epoch, and the locked pool plus rebase keep most of the mint off the open market. For an inflation lens specifically, AERO reads as steadily, mildly inflationary — the emission is the dominant force and there is no offset, but the lock and the decay keep the realized pace in the low single digits.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the weekly epoch emission — each Wednesday epoch sets the gauge mint, and the cruise-phase decay of roughly 1% an epoch is what trims the forward read. Watch the veAERO lock ratio, since the share of AERO re-locked each week is the single lever that decides how much of the gross mint reaches the float. Watch the Aero Fed votes, where veAERO holders can nudge the emission rate up or down a small step each epoch. Note the predictive-allocation upgrade slated for July 2026, which replaces the weekly voting model for incentives. And watch for any move toward rebase elimination or deflationary mechanics tied to the planned protocol upgrades — a real burn or buyback would be the first buy-side offset AERO has ever had.
