## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 25 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **BONK at about −0.05% net** — a hair below flat. There is no protocol inflation and no vesting unlock, so the only moving part is a fee-funded buyback that burns roughly **472B BONK** per quarter. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+0.10%**, versus the framework's **−0.05%** — a gap of about **0.15 percentage points**, well inside tolerance, so **no monitor-gap chip ships**. Against an ~88-trillion-token float, the burn is real but tiny, so BONK reads as a **flat, fully-distributed memecoin with a mild deflationary tilt**.

## Sell pressure: where new BONK comes from

The honest answer is: nowhere. Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**, because BONK is a fixed-supply SPL token on Solana. There is no block reward, no staking emission, and no curve that mints new BONK over time; the supply that exists is the supply that will exist. That single fact is what separates BONK from an inflationary proof-of-stake coin — its dilution risk does not come from issuance.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is also **zero**. BONK launched with allocations to Solana NFT projects, early contributors, the BONK DAO, artists and developers, and an initial liquidity and marketing pool. Those schedules — including the three-year early-contributor linear unlock that began January 1 2023 — have all finished, so the token is **fully unlocked** and no cliff hits the market in this window. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a flow: the token is fully distributed, leaving no locked treasury reserve to release. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where new BONK goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the only active force, at about **472B BONK** over the last quarter. The Bonk ecosystem routes a share of its fee revenue into open-market BONK purchases that are then burned: the Bonk.fun (LetsBonk) launchpad directs roughly half of its platform fees to buyback-and-burn, and the BonkBot trading bot adds about a tenth of its fees. Because the destination is a burn address, those coins are gone for good, so the buyback counts cleanly on the buy side and is permanently deflationary.

Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero as a separate line: BONK has no base-fee burn of its own, since the burns already flow through the fee-funded buyback in Buy #1. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is zero in this window, but it carries a watch item: the BONK DAO has approved a one-time burn of **1T BONK** that triggers when the holder count crosses one million, and that milestone has not yet been reached. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is zero.

## Foundation and overhang

BONK has no classic unlock overhang — the token is fully distributed, with no team, seed or treasury reserve still vesting. What it does have is one community-controlled discretionary lever: the **BonkDAO**, which governs burn initiatives through on-chain voting. The DAO's standing item is the approved **1T-token burn** tied to the one-million-holder milestone — a balance-reducing event, not a supply-adding one, so if it executes it deepens the deflationary read rather than threatening it. The framework books no discretionary release beyond the ongoing fee burn and re-checks the burn flow and the holder milestone on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if a new DAO-approved distribution that adds supply were to appear, the outflow would enter Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How BONK compares to other fixed-supply memecoins

BONK belongs to the class of **fixed-supply memecoins** — closer to a hard-capped token than to a continuous-emission chain. Unlike an uncapped proof-of-stake L1, BONK has no mint and no inflation curve, so its supply ceiling was set at launch and never rises. Unlike a pure static-supply meme token, though, BONK pairs that fixed cap with an active, revenue-funded burn — which makes it structurally more like an exchange token that buys back and burns from fee income than like a passive memecoin that simply sits at its launch supply.

The contrast worth drawing is with the dilutive memecoins that keep printing or keep unlocking. BONK does neither: there is no issuance to outrun and no vesting tail to absorb, so the only direction supply can move is down, slowly, as the burn runs. The catch is scale — with about 88 trillion tokens outstanding, a 472B quarterly burn trims only a fraction of a percent, so BONK reads as flat with a deflationary lean rather than as aggressively shrinking. The burn is a genuine floor under supply, just a gentle one.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the fee-funded burn pace — the ~472B quarterly run-rate is the single number that decides whether the deflationary tilt holds or deepens, and it scales directly with Bonk.fun launchpad and BonkBot trading volume. Watch the one-million-holder count: crossing it triggers the DAO-approved 1T burn, a one-time event that would more than double a quarter's burn in a single transaction. Watch BonkDAO governance for any new burn proposal, since the burn program is community-steered rather than fixed by code. And expect the framework to keep reading within a fraction of a percent of our supply monitor, because with no minting and no unlocks there is no structural wedge between the two.
