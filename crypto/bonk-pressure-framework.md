## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending July 13 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **BONK at about −0.28% net**, edging down. BONK is the Solana community memecoin; its SPL mint authority is revoked, so the protocol can never issue another coin, and every launch allocation is fully unlocked with no vesting cliffs left. That makes all four sell rows zero. The only pressure is on the buy side: bonk.fun (LetsBonk) spends about half its platform revenue buying BONK, and a slice of that is burned outright — together about **250B BONK** removed over the window. Our supply monitor reads the same period at about **+0.12%**, a difference of about **0.41 percentage points** that sits inside the framework's 0.5pp tolerance, so no **monitor-gap** chip is raised — and because the mint is revoked, supply cannot actually grow, so the monitor's small positive read is mcap-and-price noise on a sub-cent token, not new issuance. BONK is best labelled a **quiet, deflationary-by-design token** whose burn is shrinking as launchpad revenue cools.

## Sell pressure: where new BONK comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**, and it is zero by cryptographic guarantee. BONK's mint authority on Solana has been revoked, which permanently disables minting; there is no staking reward, no block subsidy, no emission curve. Every BONK that will ever exist already exists, and on-chain the token supply can only move in one direction — down, through burns.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is also **zero**. BONK launched in December 2022 with its full 100 trillion supply, and the original allocations (Solana NFT communities, early contributors, the DAO, liquidity) have all finished releasing. Tokenomics trackers now show BONK as 100% unlocked with no cliffs scheduled for 2026 or beyond, so there is no unlock overhang feeding the float. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero for forward flow, though BONK does carry watched overhangs discussed below. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero; there is no estate or court distribution attached to BONK.

## Buy pressure: where new BONK goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the token's main mechanism, at about **180B BONK** over the next 90 days. The bonk.fun launchpad routes roughly **51%** of its platform revenue into open-market BONK purchases, which are now accumulated into the BNKK Digital Asset Treasury run by the public company Bonk, Inc. The important qualifier is that launchpad revenue has fallen hard: Bonk, Inc.'s first-quarter 2026 revenue was about **$4.34M**, but the platform's live dashboard shows only about **$104K** so far in July, a collapse that pulls the buyback well below the roughly **472B BONK** bought last quarter. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is about **70B BONK**: a portion of that fee-funded buying is sent to a dead address and destroyed outright, alongside community burns, and cumulative burns since launch now exceed **12 trillion BONK**, the reason supply has fallen from 100T to about 88T. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero; a DAO-pledged 1 trillion burn awaits BONK crossing one million holders, but that milestone has not been reached and no new lock fired in the window.

## Foundation and overhang

BONK is fully distributed, so its overhang is not locked team supply but a set of large, identifiable wallets. The first is the **BNKK Digital Asset Treasury** — Bonk, Inc. holds roughly **$25M** in BONK and has stated a target of owning about **5%** of circulating supply, funded from bonk.fun revenue; this is the buyback accumulation destination, trackable through the company's public filings and on-chain. The second is the **BonkDAO treasury**, the community war chest that governs burn initiatives. The third is newly relevant: a **governance exploit on July 6 2026** let an attacker spend about $4.4M to pass a malicious proposal and drain roughly **4.426 trillion BONK** (about $20M) from the BonkDAO treasury into an attacker-controlled wallet. That was a reallocation of already-circulating coins, not new issuance, so it does not change the supply count — but it converts DAO-held tokens into a large discretionary sell risk. None of these balances sits on a release schedule, so the framework books them at zero and watches them: if any of these balances falls between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How BONK compares to other fixed-supply memecoins

BONK belongs to the class of **fixed-supply memecoins with a revenue-funded burn** — closer in shape to a capped exchange token than to an inflating Layer 1. Unlike a proof-of-stake chain that mints new coins every block, BONK can never issue; unlike a passive fixed-supply token that just sits at its cap, BONK actively removes coins from revenue. The nearest analogue is its own launchpad rival's token, PUMP, which is likewise capped and runs a revenue buyback-burn — but PUMP still has huge investor unlock cliffs ahead, whereas BONK has none, so BONK's supply is the steadier of the two.

What separates BONK from a coin like DOGE — the archetypal memecoin — is the mint. DOGE has no cap and adds about 5 billion coins a year through a fixed tail emission, so its inflation is small but permanent and positive. BONK is the mirror image: no emission at all, a revoked mint, and a burn that nudges supply down. The catch is that BONK's deflation is only as strong as bonk.fun revenue, and that revenue is cyclical with memecoin speculation. When the launchpad is hot the burn is real; when volumes fade, as they have into mid-2026, the buyback shrinks and BONK drifts toward flat.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch **bonk.fun revenue** first, because the entire buyback is a fixed share of it — a rebound in launchpad volume would restore the burn, and the recent slide cuts it toward zero. Watch the **BNKK treasury** accumulation pace and disclosures, since a public company buying toward 5% of supply is the single largest structural bid on BONK. Watch the fallout from the **July 6 2026** governance exploit — whether the attacker's ~4.426T BONK is sold, returned, or frozen, and whether BonkDAO changes its voting rules. And watch the **one-million-holder** milestone, which triggers a pledged 1 trillion community burn that would meaningfully cut supply in a single event.

Our supply monitor reads about +0.12% for the same window, a 0.41pp gap inside tolerance and consistent with mcap-and-price noise on a token that cannot mint.
