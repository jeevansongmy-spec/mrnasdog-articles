KuCoin Token (KCS) has no mint and a profit-funded buyback-burn that has been dormant since December 2025. Over the last 90 days no KCS was created and none was burned, so the framework reads net **0.0%** — flat — on a **~134.7M** circulating base. Our supply monitor reads **+1.7%**, but that is the circulating count being re-counted upward, not new supply.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 24 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **KCS at 0.0% net** — flat. There is no protocol inflation, no vesting unlock, and the buyback-burn did not fire in the window, so both the sell ledger and the buy ledger are zero. Our supply monitor reads the realised last-90-day change at **+1.7%**, a gap of about **1.7 percentage points** that ships a ⚠ monitor-gap chip. The gap is not a real flow: the on-chain total supply has not moved since the December 2025 burn — no mint, no new burn — while the circulating figure rose because previously company-held KCS is being re-counted as circulating. KCS is a **fixed-supply exchange token with a paused burn**, deflationary by design but flat this quarter.

## Sell pressure: where new KCS comes from

Every sell row reads zero, because no mechanism adds KCS. Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **0**: KCS is an ERC-20 token with no mint function and no block emission, so the fixed 200M genesis can only shrink, never grow. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **0**: the founder and early-investor lockups from the 2017 launch expired years ago, and no vesting schedule remains, so no cliff reaches the market.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is **0** as a flow, with one caveat worth naming: about **7.5M KCS**, the gap between ~142.2M total supply and ~134.7M circulating, is held by the company outside circulation and carries no published release schedule. It is enumerated and monitored, not projected. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is **0**, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to KCS.

## Buy pressure: where new KCS goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — booked **0** this window, and that is the most important number on the page. KuCoin spends **10%** of profit buying KCS on the market and sending it to a black-hole burn address, working toward a long-term target of shrinking total supply from ~142.2M to **100M**. Those burns ran monthly through 2025 — the most recent published round was the December 2025 burn — but no burn has been announced so far in 2026, and the on-chain total supply has not moved since, so the framework books no burn for this window rather than estimating one. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is **0**: there is no continuous network fee burn, and the holder bonus that pays a share of fee revenue to KCS holders is a payout, not a supply change. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both **0**, with no discretionary accumulation or new escrow announced.

## Foundation and overhang

One overhang is tracked: the **corporate reserve of ~7.5M KCS** — the difference between total supply (~142.2M) and the circulating count (~134.7M). It carries no published schedule and no announced release in the trailing window, so it ships at zero under the framework's evidence rules and is re-checked on a roughly bi-weekly walk. This window's monitor reading suggests part of that reserve is already being re-counted as circulating upstream, which is exactly why the circulating figure rose while no new KCS was minted. If that reserve's balance actually moves to the market between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How KCS compares to other exchange tokens

KCS belongs to the class of **fixed-supply exchange tokens with a profit-funded buyback-burn** — the same structural family as the burn-model tokens of other large exchanges. Unlike an uncapped proof-of-stake chain, KCS has no emission curve and no validator rewards: nothing dilutes it, ever. Unlike a hard-capped coin that simply sits at its cap, KCS is meant to fall over time as the burn carries total supply toward 100M. The contrast worth drawing is between coins that burn on a fixed protocol schedule and coins like KCS that burn out of business profit.

That profit dependency is the whole story this quarter. When a burn is funded by a slice of exchange profit, the deflation switches off whenever the profit — or the will to spend it — pauses. KCS's burn ran monthly and small through 2025, then went quiet into 2026, so a token that markets itself as deflationary reads flat for the window: no new supply, but none removed either. For an inflation lens specifically, capped-but-not-currently-shrinking sits at neutral — the scarcity is real in the long run, but it is not happening right now.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Three things would move the framework reading. First, a **resumed burn** — the next published burn round would flip Buy #1 from zero back to a small deflationary flow; watch for an announcement and a matching drop in on-chain total supply. Second, the **~7.5M company reserve** — any announced deployment, or continued upstream re-counting of it into circulating, would either activate Sell #3 or extend the ⚠ monitor gap. Third, **KuCoin's profitability**, since the burn is funded by 10% of profit, so a stronger quarter is what restarts and sizes the burn.

## Summary

KuCoin Token is a fixed-supply exchange token with no mint and a profit-funded buyback-burn that has been dormant since December 2025. With no coins added and none removed in the window, the framework reads **0.0% net** — flat. The supply monitor's +1.7% reflects an upstream re-count of company-held KCS into the circulating figure, not a market flow, and ships under a ⚠ chip. KCS is deflationary by design but neutral this quarter; the key dependency is exchange profit, and the key watch item is when the burn resumes.
