## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 25 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **BEAT at about +15% net** on the forward view, driven by vesting unlocks that vastly out-issue the weekly buyback-burn. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+106%**, versus the framework's **+18.84%** documented-vesting read for the same window — a gap of about **87.7 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The gap is not noise: BEAT only launched on November 1 2025, so its 90-day-ago supply baseline sat at the launch-day unlock amount and only later stepped up to the real circulating count, and a large community "earn" reward stream is paying out faster than the vesting line with no disclosed schedule. BEAT is **steeply inflationary on a young float**, with documented unlocks as the floor and an unsized earn stream on top.

## Sell pressure: where new BEAT comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**. BEAT is a fixed 1B-supply BEP-20 token on BNB Chain, fully minted at launch, so the chain pays no block reward and creates no new coins; every increase in circulating supply is a locked allocation vesting, not issuance. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the whole story, at about **53.75M BEAT** over the next 90 days. The published schedule unlocks roughly **21.25M** a month: the Community pool (40% of supply, 8.33M a month over 48 months), the Foundation (15%, about 2.92M a month over 48 months), and a Marketing & Operations tranche (10% over 9 months, about 10M a month). The marketing tranche finishes around **Aug 1 2026**, so the September unlock drops to about **11.25M**.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is booked at **zero** as a flow, but it carries the most important caveat: a community "earn" reward stream is distributing BEAT faster than the linear vesting line implies, and there is no published next-90-day rate to size it, so the framework refuses to invent a number. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate applies to BEAT. The Team (8%), Advisors & Angels (~13%) and a further-users airdrop are all still inside a 12-month cliff that does not expire until around November 2026.

## Buy pressure: where new BEAT goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the only active offset, at about **9.5M BEAT** over 90 days. Each week Audiera spends part of its on-chain product revenue buying BEAT and burning it: the week of June 1–8 2026 burned **770,545 BEAT** against revenue worth roughly **$2.87M**, and the prior week burned **716,620 BEAT**, with more than **12.35M** destroyed since launch. Because the destination is a burn, those coins are gone for good. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is carried at zero, since there is no separate base-fee burn beyond that revenue buyback. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero. The burn is real, but at roughly a sixth of the vesting flow it slows dilution rather than reversing it.

## Foundation and overhang

The defining overhang is the Community allocation — 40% of the 1B supply, or 400M BEAT — which the published schedule releases over 48 months but which the live "earn" mechanism appears to be front-loading. That, plus the Foundation's 150M, is the team-controlled supply behind the monitor's doubling. These pools are not a silent stockpile; they are actively distributing to users, which is exactly why realized circulating supply has run far ahead of the orderly vesting line. The framework re-checks the burn reports, the unlock calendar and the chain's circulating count on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if a tracked balance falls faster than the schedule, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh — and the moment Audiera publishes an earn-emission rate, that figure becomes a real Sell #3 number instead of a flagged overhang.

## How BEAT compares to other young GameFi unlock tokens

BEAT belongs to the class of **young, fixed-cap GameFi and play-to-earn tokens** whose supply curve is dominated by post-launch vesting, not by protocol emission. Unlike an uncapped proof-of-stake chain that mints a steady percentage every year, BEAT can never exceed 1B coins — but in its first two years that cap is almost irrelevant, because the gap between circulating and fully diluted supply is enormous and unlocks at double-digit monthly rates. The dilution risk here is front-loaded, the opposite of a halving-model coin where new supply only shrinks over time.

The closer analogues are other dance-, move- and play-to-earn tokens that pair heavy community emissions with a revenue buyback-burn. In that cohort, the burn is a marketing-friendly counterweight, but it rarely outruns the unlock schedule in the early years; BEAT fits the pattern, with a real but modest burn against a vesting flow several times larger. The honest read for an inflation lens is that BEAT is steeply inflationary today, with the cap and the eventual end of the cliffs as the only brakes — both still a long way out.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the monthly unlocks on **Jul 1 2026** and **Aug 1 2026** (about 21.25M each) and the smaller **Sep 1 2026** unlock (about 11.25M) once the marketing tranche ends. Watch the weekly burn reports — the ~0.72M-to-0.77M BEAT pace is the only force pushing the other way, and it scales with product revenue. Most of all, watch for Audiera to publish a rate for the community "earn" distribution: that single disclosure would turn the largest part of the monitor gap into a sizeable, defensible Sell #3 figure. And note the Team, Advisor and airdrop cliffs near **Nov 1 2026**, which sit just past this window but will add a fresh wave of unlocks.
