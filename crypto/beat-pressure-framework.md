## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending July 13 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **BEAT at about +10.9% net** over the next 90 days — supply is still growing, and projected to keep growing. The one supply engine is the **vesting unlock**: roughly **43.75M BEAT** vests over the window against a weekly buyback-and-burn of about **10M BEAT**. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day circulating change at **+122%** — far above our own gross-schedule read of **+17.4%** for the same window — a gap of roughly **104.8 percentage points**, so a **monitor-gap chip ships**. That gap is a young-token artifact: the monitor divides a real **~170M** circulating rise by a starting float near **139M**, barely a third of today's **309M**, so the same tokens read as about **+55%** against today's base, and most of the rise was the front-loaded post-launch distribution that is now largely behind it. BEAT reads as **structurally inflationary on an early, still-unlocking float — but decelerating**.

## Sell pressure: where new BEAT comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**. BEAT cannot be minted: its **1B supply** was fixed at launch, and the on-chain total holds at exactly 1B. What looks like emission is really the release of pre-minted, pre-allocated buckets on a published **vesting** schedule. The in-game and creator rewards Audiera pays out are drawn from the community allocation, which is already counted as vesting below — so there is no separate protocol mint adding new coins on top.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the whole story, about **43.75M BEAT** over the next 90 days. Three monthly cliffs fall inside the window: **Aug 1 2026** releases about **21.25M BEAT** — community (8.33M), foundation (2.92M) and the final **10M marketing tranche** — then **Sep 1** and **Oct 1** each release about **11.25M** once the marketing bucket is exhausted. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is **zero** as a flow: the team allocation (8%, ~80M), advisors and angels (~13%, ~131M) and a 2% further-user airdrop (~20M) sit under a **12-month cliff** that only opens around **Nov 1 2026**, just after this window. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is **zero**, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to BEAT.

## Buy pressure: where new BEAT goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is about **10M BEAT** over the next 90 days, and it is the reason BEAT is not a one-sided ledger. Audiera routes platform revenue into a weekly **buyback-and-burn**: roughly **770K BEAT** bought back and destroyed each week, funded by around **$2.9M** of weekly revenue. The tokens are sent to a dead address and permanently removed — on-chain the burn wallet already holds about **16.24M BEAT**. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is **zero**: there is no automatic per-transaction burn, because the deflationary offset runs entirely through the revenue-funded buyback. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is **zero** beyond that programmatic buyback. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is **zero**; the existing 12-month cliffs are prior commitments, not fresh locks.

## Foundation and overhang

BEAT's overhang is large because only about **31% of the 1B supply is circulating** — roughly **690M BEAT** is still locked. The tracked team-controlled buckets are the **team allocation** (8%, ~80M), the **advisors and angels** allocation (~13%, ~131M) and a **further-user airdrop** (2%, ~20M) — about **230M BEAT** combined — all held under a 12-month cliff that opens around **Nov 1 2026**, plus the **foundation treasury**, which releases on the monthly schedule already counted in Sell #2. None carries a discretionary dated release inside this window, so the framework books them at zero flow and enumerates them only as monitored scope. The buyback destination is a burn — tokens gone, not held — so it adds no overhang. If any of these balances were to fall between refreshes, that outflow would enter Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How BEAT compares to other early game-token unlocks

BEAT belongs to the class of **early GameFi and app tokens with a fixed cap, heavy multi-year vesting, and a revenue-funded buyback-and-burn**. Structurally it is the mirror image of a fixed-cap infrastructure token like WLD, which unlocks on a long schedule with **no buy-side sink at all**: BEAT pairs its unlock with an actual burn, so part of every month's vesting is clawed back rather than left as pure one-way pressure. That is the same shape as an exchange token that runs a quarterly buyback — the difference is that BEAT's buyback is funded by **game revenue** rather than trading fees, and it burns weekly rather than quarterly.

The contrast worth drawing is with an uncapped proof-of-stake L1. A staking chain mints new coins every block with no ceiling; BEAT can **never exceed 1B** and is slowly removing supply through the burn — but it is very early in its unlock curve, so the scheduled vesting still dwarfs the buyback for now. That makes BEAT's inflation front-loaded and mechanically declining: the monthly unlock steps down after the **marketing bucket ends in August 2026**, and again once the burn grows relative to a maturing float. BEAT's headline supply history is dominated by the launch phase — which is exactly why our monitor's last-90-day read (**+122%**) sits so far above our forward read (**+10.9%**).

## What to watch in the next 90 days

The clearest dated event is the **Aug 1 2026** unlock of about **21.25M BEAT**, which includes the **final marketing tranche** — after it, the monthly unlock drops to about **11.25M** on Sep 1 and Oct 1. Watch the weekly **buyback-and-burn** rate, because it is revenue-driven: if platform revenue holds near **$2.9M** a week the burn keeps offsetting roughly a fifth of each month's unlock, but a revenue drop widens the net. Watch the approaching **12-month cliff around Nov 1 2026**, when the team, advisor and further-user airdrop buckets (~230M BEAT) begin to unlock — just outside this window, but the single largest change to the supply picture ahead. And expect the monitor-gap chip to persist until the launch-era base rolls out of the trailing 90-day window.
