## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 20 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **PI at about +6.5% net** on the forward view — supply growing, projected to keep growing. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+9.95%**, versus the framework's **+9.04%** read for the same window — a gap of about **0.9 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The gap is not a missing event: both numbers describe the same roughly **976M** new PI; the monitor divides by the smaller supply from 90 days ago, while the framework divides by today's larger circulating base. PI is **structurally inflationary by design** — its supply is engineered to keep rising as mining and migration continue.

## Sell pressure: where new PI comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the dominant force, at about **540M PI** over the next 90 days, down from roughly **810M** in the last 90 days. This is the heart of Pi Network: Pioneers who completed KYC migrate their mobile-mined Pi onto the mainnet, and fresh mining rewards keep being issued under a declining schedule. Every migrated and newly mined coin that reaches a mainnet wallet adds to the circulating float, and with more than 18 million verified users still moving balances across, this stream keeps the supply rising month after month. The pace is decelerating as the migration backlog clears and the mining rate steps down, which is why the forward number is lower than the trailing one.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — adds about **160M PI** over the next 90 days, down from roughly **166M** last quarter. On top of migration, locked balances release on a published monthly calendar that is tapering through 2026 — January 2026 was the year's largest tranche at about 134M, with later months stepping down. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a separate flow: the foundation and core-team allocations only unlock in proportion to community migration, so they are already counted inside protocol inflation rather than as a discretionary dump. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to PI; the roughly 83B of supply still off-market is gated behind future migration and lockups, not a forced sale.

## Buy pressure: where new PI goes

There is almost nothing on the buy side. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is zero: the core team has not confirmed any programme to buy PI on the open market, despite community calls for one. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is also carried at zero: a small transaction-fee burn does exist, but at only a few thousand PI a day it amounts to a rounding error against 90 days of new supply. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero, with no discretionary open-market buying and no new escrow announced in the window. With buy pressure this thin, the full weight of migration and lockups flows straight through to net inflation.

## Foundation and overhang

PI's real overhang is enormous but structurally gated. Of the 100B hard cap, roughly **83B** has not yet reached circulation, split across community mining rewards, a core-team allocation and a foundation reserve. Crucially, these buckets do not sit as a discretionary stockpile that can be dumped at will — under Pi's migration-locked model, each allocation unlocks only in proportion to how much of the community has migrated, so the off-market supply enters gradually and is captured inside protocol inflation, not as a separate Sell #3 cliff. The framework books no discretionary release beyond migration and the published lockup calendar, and re-checks the migration count and unlock schedule on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if the foundation or core-team balance fell outside that mechanism, the outflow would enter Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How PI compares to other uncapped-style mining tokens

PI is unusual. It has a hard cap of 100B like Bitcoin, but unlike a halving-model coin where new supply is small and predictable, only about a tenth of PI's cap is in circulation, so the relevant force is not the distant ceiling but the steep ramp of migration and unlocks happening now. In that sense PI behaves more like an early-stage token deep in its vesting schedule than like a mature, fully-distributed network — the cap is real but years away from binding.

The sharper contrast is with tokens that pair issuance with a burn or buyback to offset dilution. Exchange tokens and several fee-burning L1s remove supply as activity rises, sometimes turning net-deflationary. PI does the opposite: it issues heavily and burns almost nothing, so there is no structural brake on its inflation. For an inflation lens specifically, that makes PI one of the most clearly inflationary large-cap tokens we cover — the entire story is new supply arriving, with no meaningful counterweight.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the monthly migration count, published by the project — it is the single number that decides how fast Sell #1 runs, and a faster second-migration rollout would push net inflation back up. Watch the published lockup calendar for July, August and September 2026, since each monthly tranche lands as fresh sellable supply on roughly the 14th. Watch the mining-rate schedule, which steps down over time and slowly lowers new issuance. And watch for any official statement on a buyback or an expanded fee burn — the community has pushed for one, and any real buy-side programme would be the first thing capable of bending PI's inflation lower.

## Summary

PI is a mobile-mined, hard-capped token whose supply is engineered to keep rising as Pioneers migrate mined Pi to the mainnet and locked tranches release on schedule. The network adds about 700M PI over the next 90 days against effectively no buyback or burn, leaving the framework at roughly +6.5% net. Our supply monitor reads +9.95% realized over the last 90 days, with the small gap to the framework's +9.04% explained purely by which supply base each number divides by. The key risk is straightforward: with no buy-side mechanism, PI's inflation eases only as the migration pace slows — and the 100B cap is far too distant to matter yet.
