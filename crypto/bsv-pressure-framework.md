# BSV Inflation Analysis · July 2026 · Mixed flows, supply roughly steady

Bitcoin SV mints about **0.04M BSV** every 90 days from its fixed proof-of-work block reward of 3.125 BSV, with nothing offsetting it. Framework reading: **+0.20% net** on a ~20.06M circulating base against a 21M hard cap — about 95.5% mined, with the next halving due around 2028. The supply monitor reads **+0.18%**, a 0.02-point agreement.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the trailing 90-day window to July 13 2026, the framework reads **Bitcoin SV at +0.20% net inflation** — pure mining emission, no offsetting flow. The supply monitor reads **+0.18%**, a **0.02-percentage-point** gap, so no monitor-gap chip ships. Bitcoin SV is a quiet chain: a fair-launch proof-of-work fork that split off Bitcoin Cash in 2018, fully predictable emission, slowly approaching its 21M hard cap.

## Sell pressure: where new BSV comes from

One source only. Sell #1 (protocol inflation) booked **~0.04M BSV**: the Bitcoin SV block reward pays **3.125 BSV** per block since the April 2024 halving, at a ten-minute block target — roughly 144 blocks and about 450 BSV per day, or 40,500 BSV per 90 days. The subsidy halves every 210,000 blocks; the next halving to 1.5625 BSV lands around **2028** — well outside the next 90 days. Sell #2 (vesting unlocks) is **0** forever: Bitcoin SV forked fair off Bitcoin Cash in November 2018 with no premine, no ICO, and no investor vesting. Sell #3 (Foundation and unscheduled unlocks) is **0** — there is no protocol-level foundation treasury and no discretionary stockpile to release. Sell #4 (bankruptcy) is **0**.

The arithmetic is worth showing because so few coins allow it this cleanly. Bitcoin SV targets a ten-minute block, so the chain produces about 144 blocks a day; the live explorer showed **12,960 blocks** over the trailing ~90 days, right on that target. At **3.125 BSV** each, that is roughly 450 BSV per day — 40,500 BSV across the window, or 0.20% of the 20.06M circulating base. The monitor measured **+0.18%** over the same window from the supply data alone, independently of this derivation. When a protocol's entire supply policy fits in two lines of multiplication and the observed chain matches it to two hundredths of a percent, the framework has nothing left to investigate — which is precisely the point of owning an asset like this.

## Buy pressure: where new BSV goes

The buy ledger is entirely empty. Buy #1 (programmatic buyback) is **0**: Bitcoin SV has no treasury and no protocol revenue, so there is nothing to fund open-market buying. Buy #2 (protocol fee burn) is **0**: transaction fees flow to miners inside the coinbase — nothing is destroyed. Buy #3 (Foundation buy) is **0**; there is no accumulation programme. Buy #4 (new long-term lock) is **0**: Bitcoin SV is pure proof-of-work, with no staking and no lockup mechanism. New supply enters; nothing leaves the market.

## Foundation and overhang

There is no team-controlled overhang to enumerate — the framework records BSV as **fully circulating with no identified team-controlled wallets**. The fair-launch fork left no team allocation, and there is no foundation treasury, no investor unlock schedule, and no buyback accumulation destination that could surprise the market. This is one of the shortest overhang sections in the catalog, which is itself the finding: nothing is being watched because nothing exists to watch. If, against all precedent, a large dormant balance ever began moving toward the market, that outflow would enter Sell #3 at the next refresh; today there is nothing of the sort.

## How BSV compares to other halving-model PoW chains

Among hard-cap halving chains, Bitcoin SV sits exactly where its design puts it: the same emission model as Bitcoin — fixed reward, four-year halvings, a 21M hard cap — because BSV is a 2018 fork carrying Bitcoin's ledger and monetary policy through Bitcoin Cash. Bitcoin itself currently emits ~0.2% per 90 days after its 2024 halving, and BSV's ~0.20% is effectively identical, since the three share the same subsidy and the same cap. Its sibling fork Bitcoin Cash runs at the same 3.125-per-block pace for the same reason. Against Litecoin — another fair-launch halving chain at 4× the block speed and cap, emitting ~0.4% — BSV runs at roughly half the pace. Against smooth-decay chains like Kaspa, whose reward declines a few percent monthly instead of stepping every four years, BSV's emission is lumpier between halvings but identical in destination: a fixed ceiling, asymptotically approached.

The contrast with exchange tokens and proof-of-stake L1s is starker: BSV's scarcity is coded, not earned. An exchange token can go net-deflationary by burning revenue; an uncapped staking chain stays inflationary because it mints fresh rewards with no ceiling. Bitcoin SV does neither — no revenue dependence, no governance discretion, no treasury opacity, no burn to shrink supply, and no uncapped emission to inflate it. It will be mildly inflationary until the cap is reached, by design, with the rate stepping down every four years. The chain crossed **20M mined** around March 2026, so about **20.06M of 21M** now exists — leaving under a million coins ever to be issued, and the final million takes far longer to mine than the first twenty.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Genuinely little. The **2028** halving is the only scheduled supply event and it sits nearly two years away; the framework reading stays ~+0.20% until then. Hashrate swings can wobble block timing by a few percent, which is noise at this scale until the difficulty adjustment pulls it back. Watch the distance to the 21M cap, which only grows more binding as the subsidy shrinks and transaction fees become a larger share of miner revenue. The only structural watch line is protocol governance — any proposal touching the emission schedule — and none exists. The next material change to this page should be the halving itself.

## Summary

Bitcoin SV is a fair-launch, hard-cap proof-of-work chain emitting **~0.04M BSV per 90 days** at 3.125 BSV per block, with an empty buy ledger and no team-controlled overhang at all. The framework reads **+0.20% net**; the monitor agrees to within 0.02 points. The supply trajectory is fully coded: ~95.5% of the 21M cap is mined, the ~2028 halving cuts the pace in half, and nothing discretionary can move it. BSV is among the most predictable supply profiles in coverage.
