## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **STX at about +0.8% net** on the forward view. Two protocol mints add supply and nothing offsets them. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+0.54%**, versus the framework's **+0.79%** for the same window — a gap of about **0.25 percentage points**, inside the clean band, so no warning chip ships. STX is **mildly inflationary**, and the pace is now lower than it was a year ago because the miner reward halved in April.

## Sell pressure: where new STX comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the miner reward, at about **8.2M STX** over the next 90 days. Stacks anchors to Bitcoin: a new coinbase reward is minted for every Bitcoin tenure a miner wins. The first Stacks halving activated around April 2026, cutting that reward from 1,000 to **500 STX** per tenure and dropping the miner-issuance rate to roughly **1.8%** a year. The last-90-day window straddled the halving — full reward until April, then half — so realized issuance was close to the same ~8.5M.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled — is the second mint, at about **6.2M STX** over 90 days. A five-year growth program mints roughly 475 STX per tenure straight into a locked treasury contract. That treasury does not dump on the market: it sells through private deals with multi-month lockups, so the coins expand total supply but reach the open float slowly. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero**: the original 2019 genesis allocations ran out their multi-year unlock schedules, and circulating supply now equals total supply, so no dated cliff hits the window. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: what offsets new STX

Nothing does — every buy row is **zero**. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is not running: a buyback is named only as a future option, to be switched on if the token sharply overperforms, and that trigger has not fired. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero because STX network fees are paid to miners, not burned, so there is no fee sink. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero. With no offset, the full gross mint flows through to the net read.

## Foundation and overhang

STX has no classic unlock cliff left — the genesis distribution is effectively complete. What it has instead is the growth-treasury mint: a continuous, protocol-level issuance into a locked contract, governed and disclosed, with monthly public updates. Because those coins are sold only through locked private deals rather than on exchanges, they pressure supply gradually, which is exactly why realized float grows a touch slower than the gross mint. The framework books this issuance in Sell #3 and re-checks the treasury balance and the on-chain mint on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if treasury holdings fall faster than the schedule implies, the extra outflow stays in Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How STX compares to other Bitcoin-anchored chains

STX belongs to the class of **uncapped, Bitcoin-anchored layer-2 tokens** whose issuance halves on Bitcoin's schedule. Like Bitcoin, the miner reward steps down by half on a fixed timeline — that April 2026 halving is why the miner mint is now modest. Unlike Bitcoin, STX has no hard cap and layers a second, time-boxed growth emission on top, which lifts the near-term rate above where the halving alone would put it. The result is a chain that is mildly inflationary today but structurally trending down: the miner reward keeps halving, and the growth mint is scheduled to end in a few years.

The contrast worth drawing is with chains that pair issuance with a burn. STX has no burn at all — no fee burn, no active buyback — so the gross mint is the net mint. That makes the inflation read cleaner than most: there is no offsetting sink to model, just two mints and a denominator. For an inflation lens, STX reads as steadily, mildly inflationary, with the rate set to ease as the next halvings and the end of the growth program arrive.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the growth-treasury emission step-up: the per-tenure mint is scheduled to rise around late July 2026 as the program enters its second year, which lifts Sell #3. Watch the treasury's monthly updates for any shift from locked private sales toward open-market selling, which would move coins to the float faster. Note that no buyback or burn is active, so any net change comes entirely from the mint side. And expect the framework to keep reading slightly above our supply monitor for as long as treasury coins are minted into a locked contract rather than the open float — a small, structural gap, not a new unlock.
