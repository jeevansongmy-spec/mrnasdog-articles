Toncoin's own emission is gentle — about **0.6%** a year to validators, net of a fee burn — but an early-backer fund is unlocking on a fixed monthly schedule, adding roughly **110M TON** over the next 90 days. ## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 20 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **TON at +4.2% net**, driven almost entirely by the TON Believers Fund's monthly vesting rather than by protocol emission. Our supply monitor reads **+9.5%** — a gap of about **5.2 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The deep walk narrowed but could not fully close it: the published schedule (~110M vesting + ~4M emission ≈ 114M) accounts for about half of the monitor's ~233M, and the residual is an aggregator-side circulating reclassification (TON is mid-rebrand toward GRAM, with previously-frozen buckets re-tagged), not an observed primary outflow. TON is **structurally inflationary on the active float while the early-backer fund vests**.

## Sell pressure: where new TON comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the gentlest force, at about **4M TON** over 90 days. TON is an uncapped proof-of-stake chain, but its validator emission runs at only ~0.6% a year and part of each transaction fee is burned back, so net new issuance is small.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the real driver, at about **110M TON**. The TON Believers Fund holds 1,317M TON from early backers and releases one thirty-sixth of itself every month on the 11th, running through October 2028; three of those monthly cliffs — **Jul 11, Aug 11 and Sep 11 2026** — land in this window, moving roughly 110M coins from frozen to tradable. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled — is zero, with no dated discretionary release. Sell #4 — long-term locked — is zero as a flow: a large ~1,081M early-miner overhang stays frozen by a 2023 community vote until about February 2027.

## Buy pressure: where new TON goes

The buy ledger is empty. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is zero; there is no protocol buyback. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — exists but is small and already netted into the 0.6% emission figure, so it is not double-counted. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero. Nothing on the protocol side removes TON to offset the vesting.

## Foundation and overhang

TON's overhang is two-part: the unreleased remainder of the **Believers Fund** (releasing on its monthly schedule through 2028) and the **~1,081M frozen early-miner stack** (171 genesis accounts suspended by the February 2023 vote, unfreezing around February 2027). The framework books only the dated monthly fund cliffs and monitors both pools on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if a balance falls faster than its schedule, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How TON compares to other uncapped proof-of-stake chains

TON belongs to the class of **uncapped proof-of-stake L1s whose near-term supply curve is set by unlocks, not emission**. Unlike a high-inflation chain that mints heavily to validators, TON's emission is deliberately low; unlike a fully-distributed chain, it still has large early-backer and early-miner pools entering circulation on multi-year schedules. So its inflation reading is dominated by the vesting calendar — positive and lumpy around the monthly cliff dates — rather than by a steady mint.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the three Believers Fund cliffs on the 11th of July, August and September — each adds ~36.6M TON to the tradable float. Watch the GRAM rebrand and any supply-classification changes, since the monitor gap traces to that reclassification. And note the early-miner unfreeze around February 2027 — outside this window, but the single largest forward overhang.

TON is an uncapped proof-of-stake token with gentle emission but a heavy unlock schedule. About 4M TON is minted to validators over 90 days, while the TON Believers Fund vests roughly 110M across three monthly cliffs, leaving the framework at about +4.2% net with nothing on the buy side to offset it. Our monitor reads +9.5%, with the residual traced to an aggregator reclassification during the GRAM rebrand rather than a second unlock. TON stays inflationary on the active float while the early-backer fund runs.
