## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **PENGU at +3.4% net** on the forward view, driven entirely by scheduled vesting with no offsetting buy pressure. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at about **+0.03%**, versus the framework's **+2.2%** gross-unlock read for the same window — a gap of about **2.2 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The gap is a classification wedge: unlocked team and company tokens move into insider wallets, and the circulating count keeps treating insider-held coins as off-market until they are actually sold. PENGU is **structurally inflationary through scheduled vesting**, with no mechanism pulling supply back.

## Sell pressure: where new PENGU comes from

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the entire story, at about **2.11B PENGU** over the next 90 days. PENGU launched in December 2024 with most of its supply distributed up front, but two cohorts stayed locked: the team at **17.8%** of supply and the company at **11.48%**, each carrying a one-year cliff followed by a three-year linear vest. That cliff cleared at the end of 2025, so both cohorts now release a slice every month — roughly **703M PENGU** on the 17th. Three of those tranches land inside this window, on June 17, July 17 and August 17.

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**: PENGU is an ecosystem token, not a chain fuel token, so there is no block reward and no mint authority issuing new supply. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also zero as a flow; beyond the scheduled monthly vesting there is no dated discretionary release in the window. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where new PENGU goes

Every buy row is **zero**, and that is the defining feature of PENGU's inflation profile. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — does not exist: the project routes ecosystem fees into product and growth, not into buying PENGU off the open market. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero because PENGU has no burn mechanism. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is zero, with no disclosed discretionary accumulation, and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is zero. With nothing on the buy side, the scheduled unlocks meet no structural absorption.

## Foundation and overhang

The overhang here is the vesting schedule itself. The team and company cohorts together hold close to **29%** of supply, releasing on a fixed monthly schedule that runs into 2028 — a known, dated stream rather than a discretionary stockpile. Because the releases are scheduled, they are booked in Sell #2 rather than the discretionary Sell #3. The watch is on destination: the unlock pattern is claim-and-disperse, where tokens leave the vesting contract into multiple wallets, and the moment those holders begin selling, the circulating count catches up to the framework read. If a treasury balance falls faster than the published schedule, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How PENGU compares to other ecosystem and memecoin tokens

PENGU belongs to the class of **fixed-supply ecosystem tokens with no protocol mint and no burn** — closer to a memecoin or a brand token than to a fee-burning chain asset. Unlike an uncapped proof-of-stake chain, it issues nothing per block; unlike an exchange token, it runs no revenue-funded buyback. Its only supply dynamic is the release of pre-allocated tokens out of vesting, so for an inflation lens PENGU reads as purely supply-driven: dilution comes entirely from the unlock calendar, with no offsetting deflationary force at all.

The contrast worth drawing is with tokens that pair a fixed cap with an active burn or buyback. Those assets can hold supply flat or even shrink it as activity grows; PENGU cannot, because the only lever in motion adds supply. A strong business — merchandise, licensing, a debit card, ecosystem fees — drives real revenue, but none of it is wired to the token, so growth does not translate into buy pressure. That makes PENGU mildly-to-moderately inflationary on a schedule that is fully predictable.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the three unlock dates — June 17, July 17 and August 17 2026 — each releasing about 703M PENGU; they are the supply events that define the window. Watch whether any value-accrual mechanism is announced: a buyback or a burn funded by ecosystem revenue would be the single change that turns the net read down. Watch the claim-and-disperse wallets after each unlock, since that decides how fast the monitor catches up to the framework. And expect the framework to keep reading well above our supply monitor for as long as unlocked tokens sit in insider wallets the circulating count treats as off-market — that gap is a classification wedge, not a missing flow.
