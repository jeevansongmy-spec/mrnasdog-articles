## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **ZBCN at about 0.0% net**: there is no protocol inflation against the hard **100B** cap, the vesting schedule completed before the window, and the revenue-linked buyback has no published in-window token quantum to book on the buy side. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **−0.06%** — a near-flat, fractional shrink — versus the framework's **0.0%**, a gap of roughly **0.06 percentage points**. That is comfortably inside tolerance, so **no monitor-gap flag** is raised. ZBCN is best characterised as a **quiet, fully-distributed token with a flat float** — the unlock chapter is closed and the only forward dynamic is a buyback whose size is not yet disclosed.

## Sell pressure: where new ZBCN comes from

The honest answer is that no new ZBCN comes from anywhere this window. Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**: ZBCN is hard-capped at **100B** coins, all already minted against the cap, so the protocol issues no block reward and adds no new supply. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is also **zero**, and this is the key structural fact for ZBCN: the final scheduled unlock fired around **March 16 2026**, four years after launch and two days before this window opened. Aggregators now read the token as fully unlocked, so no cliff reaches the market in the next 90 days.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is **zero** as a flow: with the schedule complete there is no pending dated release, and there is no public evidence of a discretionary treasury or team distribution in the window. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is **zero**, because no bankruptcy estate or court-ordered distribution applies to ZBCN. The entire sell ledger nets to nothing.

## Buy pressure: where new ZBCN goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the part of ZBCN's story that is real but not measurable on the page. Zebec runs a revenue-linked buyback funded by **payroll processing, Zebec Card fees and partner contracts**, and reports that buyback volume has grown at an annualized rate of over **70%** since the program launched in late 2023. The difficulty is that no token quantity is published for this specific 90-day window, and the disposition — whether bought-back coins are burned or held — is not clearly stated. Following the framework's anti-fabrication rule, the buyback is carried at **zero** and monitored rather than estimated from the supply change.

Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is also **zero**: the January 2026 tokenomics narrative describes a portion of transaction fees being removed from circulation, but the amount is not publicly quantified, so it is not booked. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both **zero**, with no discretionary open-market buying outside the revenue program and no new escrow announced. The buy ledger, like the sell ledger, nets to nothing on the figures the framework can defend.

## Foundation and overhang

ZBCN has essentially no remaining unlock overhang — that is the whole point of where the token now sits. With circulating supply at roughly **97.95B** of the **100B** cap, about **98%** of all ZBCN is already distributed and the final scheduled unlock has executed. The small residual between circulating and total supply is the last of the schedule rather than a discrete cliff waiting to drop. The treasury and contributor allocations that once formed an overhang have vested out, so there is no Foundation balance whose drawdown would feed Sell #3. The framework books no discretionary release and re-checks the unlock surface and the buyback disclosures on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if a treasury balance is shown falling between refreshes, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How ZBCN compares to other capped payment tokens

ZBCN belongs to the class of **hard-capped, fully-distributed utility tokens** tied to a real payments business — closer in structure to a fixed-supply exchange or payment token than to an uncapped, continuously-emitting Layer 1. Unlike a continuous-emission chain, ZBCN has no block reward and no inflation curve to decay; unlike a halving-model coin, it has no scheduled future issuance at all. The only forward force on its supply is the revenue-linked buyback — the same shape as exchange tokens that buy and burn from product revenue.

The difference from those exchange tokens is disclosure, not mechanism. A buyback that publishes its token quantity each quarter can be booked on the buy side and can push a token net-deflationary; ZBCN's buyback is described and growing, but without an in-window token figure it cannot be quantified, so the framework reads the token as flat rather than shrinking. For an inflation lens specifically, that makes ZBCN a clean zero today: no dilution from new supply, and a deflationary tilt that is plausible but not yet measurable.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch for a quantified buyback disclosure — a quarterly or monthly token figure would let the framework move Buy #1 off zero and read ZBCN as actively shrinking. Watch Zebec Card and payroll revenue, since those flows fund the buyback, so a strong product quarter scales the contraction. Watch the supply monitor: its fractional **−0.06%** drift is the only current signal that open-market buying is happening at all, and a widening shrink would confirm the buyback is biting. And confirm the unlock schedule stays closed — with the last cliff already past, any new dated unlock would be a material change to this reading.

## Summary

ZBCN is a hard-capped, almost fully-distributed payments token whose unlock chapter closed in March 2026. The protocol mints no new supply, no cliff reaches the market this window, and the revenue-linked buyback has no published in-window token quantum, so the framework reads about 0.0% net. Our supply monitor agrees at −0.06%, a near-flat shrink, with no monitor-gap flag. The key risk-and-opportunity is disclosure: a quantified buyback would turn ZBCN from a flat zero into a measurable, deflationary read, all of it absorbed under the unchanged 100B cap.
