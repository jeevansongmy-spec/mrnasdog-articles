LayerZero has a fixed one-billion-token cap and mints nothing, yet three monthly vesting cliffs release about **77M ZRO** over the next 90 days while a revenue-funded buyback adds back only about **0.4M**. That leaves the Pressure Framework near **+30% net**. Our supply monitor reads **+24.2%** realized — the gap is unlocked coins that recipients keep staked or unsold.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **ZRO at about +30% net** on the forward view — one of the heaviest unlock windows on the board. There is no protocol inflation to speak of: ZRO is hard-capped at one billion, and the network mints nothing. The entire move is **vesting** — investor and team coins coming unlocked on a monthly schedule — set against a buyback so small it barely registers. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+24.2%**, versus the framework's **+30.4%** gross-unlock read for the same window — a gap of about **6.2 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. ZRO is **steeply dilutive right now**, with the unlock pace, not any mint, doing all the work.

## Sell pressure: where new ZRO comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**. ZRO has a fixed one-billion-token maximum and no block reward, so unlike a proof-of-stake chain it issues no new coins. Every bit of supply growth comes from tokens that already exist but are locked, vesting into circulation on a published schedule.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the whole story, at about **77M ZRO** over the next 90 days. Two big cohorts drive it: strategic partners, who hold about 32% of supply, and core contributors — the team — who hold about 26%. Both were locked for a year after the June 2024 launch, then began unlocking **monthly** over the following two years. The cliff lands on the 20th of each month at roughly **25.7M ZRO** — split, on the March 2026 example, into 13.4M to strategic partners, 10.6M to core contributors and 1.7M from repurchases. Three of these cliffs fall inside the window: **June 20, July 20 and August 20**, for about 77M in total. The next one, September 20, lands a week after the window closes.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a flow: there is no dated discretionary treasury release pending beyond the monthly vesting cliffs already counted above. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where ZRO goes off the market

Buy #1 — buyback — is the only offset, and it is tiny: about **0.4M ZRO** over 90 days. The Foundation buys ZRO on the open market using protocol revenue and holds it in a community-pledged bucket. The pace has cooled sharply through 2026, down to roughly **0.1M ZRO a month** in the spring — a rounding error against the ~25.7M unlocking each month. Buy #2 — fee burn — is carried at zero: the protocol fee switch, which would route network fees into buying and burning ZRO, is **currently switched off**, so no fee burn is active. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero.

## Foundation and overhang

ZRO's overhang is large and dated, not discretionary. About a quarter of the supply is fully unlocked today; the rest sits in scheduled vesting, with strategic partners and core contributors releasing on the same monthly track for roughly another year. That is the opposite of a fully distributed token — the supply curve still climbs steeply month after month. The buyback is the only counterweight, and at its current pace it removes far less than a single month's unlock. The framework books no discretionary Foundation release beyond the scheduled cliffs and re-checks the unlock calendar and buyback ledger on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if the Foundation steps up open-market buying or the fee switch is turned on, the offset enters Buy #1 or Buy #2 at the next refresh.

## How ZRO compares to other fixed-cap unlock tokens

ZRO belongs to the class of **hard-capped tokens still early in their vesting schedule** — the same shape as many recent infrastructure launches. Unlike an uncapped proof-of-stake chain, it cannot mint, so its inflation is entirely a function of the unlock calendar rather than a block reward. That makes the inflation lens unusually clean to read: the gross supply added each quarter is just the sum of the dated cliffs, and the only thing that can pull it back is a buyback or a burn.

The contrast worth drawing is with tokens whose buyback-and-burn is large enough to absorb their unlocks. ZRO's is not — the buyback is real but small, and the fee burn that could matter is currently inactive. So while a chain like an exchange token can turn net-deflationary even while issuing, ZRO reads as **steeply, mechanically dilutive** for as long as the monthly cliffs run ahead of any offset. The pressure eases only when the vesting schedule tapers — not before.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the monthly unlock dates — June 20, July 20 and August 20 — each adding roughly 25.7M ZRO; they are the single biggest force on the token. Watch whether the Foundation accelerates its buyback or, more decisively, whether the next fee-switch vote turns the protocol fee burn on, which would be the first real offset to the unlocks. And expect the framework to keep reading above our supply monitor for as long as unlocked coins stay staked or unsold in recipient wallets rather than reaching the open float — that gap is a timing wedge, not a missing unlock.
