## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **LUNC at +1.7% net**, driven by a fixed 7% staking emission that vastly out-issues the burn tax. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+1.0%**, versus the framework's **+1.7%** gross-mint read for the same window — a gap of about **0.7 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The gap is structural: Terra Classic mints new LUNC against the full supply but pays it only to the roughly **14%** of LUNC that is bonded, and those rewards largely auto-restake rather than reaching the float. LUNC is **structurally inflationary on the active float**, despite one of the most aggressive burn narratives in crypto.

## Sell pressure: where new LUNC comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the whole story, at about **111B LUNC** over the next 90 days. The Terra Classic mint module issues new LUNC every block at a fixed **7%** annual rate and routes it to bonded stakers as block rewards. Because the supply is uncapped, this issuance has no ceiling; it is the single largest force on the chart, dwarfing the burn. Most of it lands on coins that are already staked and auto-restake, which is why realized float grows slower than the headline mint — but as new supply, the full mint counts once, here.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero**: Terra Classic has no vesting schedule, because every original team, seed and treasury allocation was distributed at the 2022 fork. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also zero as a flow; LUNC is community-governed with no foundation holding a dated discretionary unlock, and the on-chain community pool has no scheduled release in the window. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where new LUNC goes

Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is the only active offset, at about **15B LUNC** over 90 days. A **0.5%** tax on every on-chain transaction is burned to a dead address — roughly **110-130M LUNC** a day — and exchanges such as Binance add monthly burns funded by trading fees, like the **923M** burned in May and over **2B** in June. Cumulative burns passed **451B LUNC** by mid-June 2026. Because the destination is a burn, those coins are gone for good. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is zero: there is no protocol buyback, since LUNC removes supply by a tax, not by buying coins back. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero.

## Foundation and overhang

LUNC has no classic unlock overhang — the token has been fully distributed since the 2022 fork, with no team, seed or foundation cliff left to release. The one structural overhang is the on-chain community pool, which is funded continuously by a slice of the staking emission and the burn-tax split, and is governed entirely by token-holder votes rather than a foundation. It is not a stockpile waiting to dump; spends require a passed proposal. The framework books no discretionary release beyond protocol inflation and re-checks the burn dashboards on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if the community-pool balance falls faster than its on-chain spending schedule, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How LUNC compares to other uncapped proof-of-stake chains

LUNC belongs to the class of **uncapped Cosmos-SDK proof-of-stake chains with a fixed staking emission** — closer to a continuous-emission inflation chain than to a hard-capped, halving-model coin. Unlike a fixed-supply token, LUNC has no ceiling; unlike chains that pair issuance with an EIP-1559 base-fee burn that scales with usage, LUNC's burn is a flat 0.5% transaction tax topped up by voluntary exchange burns. The result is that the mint dominates: at 7% a year against a 5.5-trillion supply, gross issuance is enormous, while the burn — for all its prominence — removes only about a seventh of it.

The contrast worth drawing is with exchange tokens and base-fee-burn L1s that go net-deflationary when activity is high. LUNC cannot reach that point at the current rate: the burn would have to multiply several times over, or the emission would have to be cut by governance, before net supply turns down. For an inflation lens, that means LUNC reads as steadily inflationary on the active float — the fixed 7% mint is the dominant force, and the famous burn is a partial brake.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch for any governance proposal to cut the 7% mint rate — that single parameter decides whether LUNC stays inflationary or flips toward neutral, and the community has repeatedly debated lowering it. Watch the monthly exchange burns, since a large Binance burn is the biggest discrete event that can dent the net. Watch the on-chain burn-tax pace — the 110-130M LUNC daily run-rate scales with network activity. And expect the framework to keep reading above our supply monitor for as long as staking rewards auto-restake rather than reach the float — that gap is structural, not a new unlock.

## Summary

LUNC is an uncapped proof-of-stake coin whose supply grows on a fixed 7% staking emission. Terra Classic mints about 111B LUNC over 90 days at that rate, while the 0.5% burn tax and exchange burns remove roughly 15B back out, leaving the framework at about +1.7% net. Our supply monitor reads +1.0% realized, with the gap explained by staking rewards auto-restaking instead of reaching the open market. LUNC stays inflationary on the active float despite its high-profile burn, and only a governance cut to the mint or a far larger burn would turn net supply down.
