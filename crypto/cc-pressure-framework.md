Canton Coin is an **uncapped burn-and-mint token**: the Canton Network minted **1.46B CC** over the last 90 days to reward network activity, and destroyed **1.19B CC** paying network fees. That leaves the framework at **+0.70% net**, easing to **+0.40%** over the next 90 days. Canton Coin is **approaching burn-mint equilibrium** — the Canton Network now collects more fee revenue than any other chain, and the destruction that revenue causes has nearly caught the mint.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending Jul 21 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **Canton Coin at +0.70% net** — **1.46B CC** minted against **1.19B CC** burned, roughly **+275M CC** of net new supply on a **39.1B** circulating base. Our independent supply monitor reads the same window at **+1.95%**, a gap of **1.25 percentage points**, which is outside tolerance, so a monitor-gap flag ships on the overview and the measured primary read is kept. The direction of that gap is a supply-classification difference, not a missing flow: a live block explorer counts **39,806,532,992 CC** circulating on Jul 21 2026 where the monitor counts **39,106,207,518** — a 700M classification difference larger than the unexplained residual itself. Canton Coin is **mildly inflationary and converging on equilibrium**.

## Sell pressure: where new CC comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the entire sell side, at **1.46B CC** over the last 90 days. The Canton Network mints fresh CC in discrete mining rounds roughly every ten minutes and pays it to the participants doing measurable work: application providers, who take **62%** of the reward pool, Super Validators at **20%**, and validators at **18%**. The protocol's live issuance curve steps down over the network's life — **40B CC** a year at genesis, **20B** from the six-month mark, **10B** from the eighteen-month mark, **5B** from year five and a flat **2.5B** from year ten. The Canton Network is now about two years old, so the active allowance is **10B CC** a year, which would be **2.47B CC** across a 90-day window. Only **1.46B** was actually minted — about **59%** of the allowance — and that shortfall is by design rather than a data gap: per-transaction reward caps mean the budget only mints when someone earns it, and unclaimed reward coupons expire after **36 hours**. Canton Coin's minting is therefore a usage meter, not a schedule.

The mint is also shrinking. **CIP-0096** zeroed passive validator liveness rewards on **Apr 30 2026**, so simply keeping a node online no longer mints CC. The effect is visible in the chain's own daily series: the Canton Network was minting **19.6M CC** a day before May and mints about **13.7M CC** a day now. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero** and structurally so: Canton Coin had a fair launch with no pre-mine, no venture allocation and no team or seed schedule, so no cliff exists to unlock. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is **zero**, because there was no token sale to build a foundation stockpile from; the protocol development fund is switched off in the live rules, and its on-chain manager party holds just **12,120 CC** with no movement since Apr 30 2026. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is **zero**, because no bankruptcy estate or court-ordered distribution applies to Canton Coin.

## Buy pressure: where new CC goes

Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is the whole buy side, at **1.19B CC** over 90 days. Every fee on the Canton Network's Global Synchronizer is priced in dollars but settled by destroying CC at the on-chain rate, so activity converts directly into supply destruction. Traffic purchases, preapproval burns, setup burns, dust expiry, holding fees and sender-change fees are all destroyed rather than collected — there is no fee recipient. Over the window that came to **$179.6M** of fees, which at each day's CC price is **1.19B CC** destroyed, and the current rate is around **15M CC** a day. The scale is the story: Canton's **$55.3M** of 30-day chain fees is the highest of any chain, ahead of Tron at **$25.9M**, Solana at **$14.5M** and Ethereum at **$7.6M**. Canton Coin has the largest fee-driven burn in the industry, and that burn now offsets **81%** of its own mint.

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: no treasury bids for Canton Coin on the open market, because the model destroys fees outright instead of recycling them into buying. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is **zero**, with no discretionary open-market buying observed. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is **zero** in the ledger despite two live locking programmes, and this is the most important thing to watch. **CIP-0105** makes Super Validators lock **70%** of their lifetime earned rewards to keep full governance weight, and **CIP-0116**, which activated **May 20 2026**, requires featured apps to lock CC to keep earning, calling for **620M CC** in total across 120 apps and one issuer. Neither is readable on chain yet: the Super Validator lock wallets are disclosed privately to the Canton Foundation rather than mapped on chain, and every one of the 120 featured apps still reads as pending with nothing recorded as locked. Without a defensible quantum, the framework books nothing and monitors instead.

## Foundation and overhang

Canton Coin's team-controlled overhang is unusual because there is no allocation to overhang from. The largest identified concentration is the **Super Validator balance**: the top 13 Super Validators hold roughly **20.2B CC** between them, of which about **14.5B CC** is committed under the CIP-0105 locking framework. That commitment is irreversible by design and exists to buy governance weight, so it is a lock rather than a queue of sell pressure — but it is disclosed off chain, so it is refreshed by hand on a bi-weekly walk rather than read from a contract. The second identified overhang is the **protocol development fund**, whose on-chain manager party is live but whose emission share currently reads as unset in every tranche of the issuance curve, leaving a balance of **12,120 CC** and no activity since Apr 30 2026. That corrects a prior reading of this fund as taking 5% of all new emissions; the live protocol rules do not support it. A third watch line is the **featured-app lock pool** under CIP-0116, sized at **620M CC** but showing zero locked coverage today. If any of these balances falls between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How CC compares to other burn-and-mint chains

Canton Coin belongs to the small class of chains where issuance and destruction are both first-class protocol mechanisms rather than one being an afterthought. Ethereum is the closest structural cousin: both mint to reward the participants securing and using the network, and both destroy a usage-denominated fee. The difference is the denominator. Ethereum's base fee burn is priced in ETH and collapses when blockspace is cheap, which is why Ethereum's burn has fallen to about **$7.6M** a month. Canton's fee is priced in dollars, so the CC destroyed scales with settlement value and rises when the token price falls — a burn that is anti-fragile to the token's own drawdowns.

Against hard-capped chains like Bitcoin, Canton Coin looks worse on paper and better in trend. Bitcoin's supply growth is fixed and falling on a halving schedule nobody can change, while Canton Coin is uncapped and could in principle mint 10B CC a year forever. But Canton Coin's issuance curve steps down on its own schedule to **2.5B** a year by year ten, and its realised mint already runs **41%** below the allowance because the protocol only creates coins that were earned. Meanwhile the burn scales with institutional settlement volume that Bitcoin does not compete for. Compared with exchange tokens that buy back and burn from profits, Canton Coin's destruction is not discretionary — no committee votes on the quarterly quantum, and no treasury can pause it. Compared with uncapped continuous-emission L1s that have no burn at all, Canton Coin has already offset four-fifths of its issuance. The honest framing is that Canton Coin is **the only chain whose burn is funded by the largest fee revenue in the industry**, and that is what makes an uncapped supply defensible here where it would not be elsewhere.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

First, the crossover: at the current mint of **13.7M CC** a day against a burn near **15M CC** a day, Canton Coin can print a negative quarter for the first time — watch whether the daily burn holds above the mint rather than spiking on single settlement days. Second, **CIP-0104**, approved **Feb 12 2026**, shifts app rewards from marker-based tracking to traffic-based measurement; it is running in dry-run mode inside the live protocol rules today, and its switch-over will reset how much of the 62% app share is actually claimed. Third, the CIP-0105 locking framework moves to its second phase when the on-chain locking contracts deploy, at which point the roughly 14.5B CC of Super Validator commitment becomes readable and the framework can book Buy #4 for the first time. Fourth, CIP-0116 featured-app locking should begin reporting real locked balances against its **620M CC** requirement now that the rule has been live since May 20 2026. Fifth, institutional settlement flow — the DTCC processed its first live production tokenized stock and Treasury trades on **Jul 15 2026** — is the single biggest input into the burn side.

## Summary

The MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads Canton Coin at **+0.70%** net supply growth over the last 90 days and **+0.40%** over the next 90, on a **39.1B CC** circulating base. The mechanism is a genuine burn-and-mint equilibrium in which the Canton Network minted **1.46B CC** to reward measured activity and destroyed **1.19B CC** paying dollar-priced network fees — a burn funded by the highest fee revenue of any chain, **$55.3M** over 30 days. The key risk is that Canton Coin has **no supply cap**: the equilibrium holds only while institutional settlement volume keeps rising, and a fall in usage cuts the burn faster than it cuts the mint, because unearned issuance simply does not get minted. The ceiling is the protocol's own issuance curve, which allows **10B CC** a year today and steps down to **2.5B** by year ten.
