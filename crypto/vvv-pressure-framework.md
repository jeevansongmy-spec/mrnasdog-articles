Venice Token (VVV) is an **uncapped, emission-based** token: new VVV is minted to stakers every day. After a cut to **3M VVV a year** on Jul 1 2026, that emission adds about **0.74M VVV** over the next 90 days, against a revenue-funded buy-and-burn that destroys only about **0.06M** — a framework net of about **+1.4%**. Our supply monitor reads the trailing 90 days at **+3.48%**, so VVV is genuinely inflationary today but cooling as each emission cut lands.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the window opening Jul 7 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **VVV at about +1.4% net** supply growth over the next 90 days — **0.74M VVV** of new staking emission against a **0.06M** revenue buy-and-burn. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+3.48%**, a gap of about **0.9 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. That gap is structural: the framework counts only the live staking emission as market pressure, while the monitor's upstream also reclassifies some Venice company and team tokens into circulating as they vest — supply Venice has publicly said it will not sell. VVV is a **structurally inflationary AI token on a steep emission-decay curve**, softened but not reversed by a small buy-and-burn.

## Sell pressure: where new VVV comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the dominant and defining force, at about **0.74M VVV** over 90 days. Venice mints new VVV to stakers every day, with **100% of emissions** paid out as staking yield. The rate decays on a published schedule: it began at **14M VVV a year** at the January 2025 launch, stepped down to **6M** in February 2026 and **5M** in May 2026, and was cut again to **3M VVV a year** effective Jul 1 2026. At that rate the emission stream is the entire engine of new supply, and its trajectory is downward.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero** inside the window: no third-party seed or investor cliff hits the market. The only allocation still unlocking is the team's **7.5M** tranche, vesting linearly at roughly **312.5K a month** to January 2027, but it releases into the Venice company treasury rather than to outside holders, so it is tracked as overhang, not as a vesting sell. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also **zero** in booked value: the Venice company treasury of roughly **30M VVV**, about 37% of total supply, is the standing overhang, but the company has publicly said it does not plan to sell it, and no discretionary market outflow has been observed. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate applies to VVV.

## Buy pressure: where new VVV goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the only active offset, at about **0.06M VVV** over 90 days. Venice runs a revenue-funded buy-and-burn: since late 2025 it has used a share of platform subscription revenue to buy VVV on the open market and send it to the **null address**, permanently burning it. Recent months have destroyed around **$234K** worth — roughly **21K VVV a month** at current prices — and a cumulative **33.8M VVV** has been removed, most of it in an earlier strategic airdrop burn. Because the destination is a burn, every VVV bought is gone for good; the constraint is that the monthly amount sits well below the emission it is meant to counter. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero: there is no per-transaction fee burn, since Venice destroys tokens through the revenue buy-and-burn rather than by burning a slice of each fee. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero, with no discretionary open-market buying beyond the programme and no new escrow announced.

## Foundation and overhang

VVV's team-controlled overhang is concentrated in the **Venice company treasury**, roughly **30M VVV** — about 37% of total supply — which the project has publicly stated it will not sell. Alongside it sits the team's remaining **~2.2M** of tokens still vesting linearly to January 2027 and the incentive fund. None of these has a dated market release inside the window, and the framework books no discretionary sell from them; they are surfaced as watched overhang and re-checked on a roughly bi-weekly walk. If the treasury or a team wallet's balance falls between refreshes through a real market sale, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How VVV compares to other uncapped emission tokens

VVV belongs to the class of **uncapped, continuous-emission tokens** — closer to an inflationary proof-of-stake L1 than to a hard-capped governance token like a fixed-supply DeFi asset. Where a capped token simply cannot dilute, VVV mints new supply to stakers every day, so its inflation is a live variable set by the emission schedule. What separates VVV from a plain inflationary chain is the aggressive decay curve: emission has fallen from 14M to 3M a year in eighteen months, so the dilution rate is a fraction of what it was at launch and still dropping.

The sharper comparison is with exchange and AI tokens that buy and burn. Some tokens burn enough revenue to shrink total supply outright and read as clean deflation. Venice runs the same style of revenue buy-and-burn — buying VVV and sending it to the null address — but the monthly burn is smaller than the daily mint, so the two do not net to deflation yet. VVV therefore reads as an emission token racing its own buy-and-burn: the mint is winning today, but the gap has narrowed with every emission cut, and if platform revenue keeps the burn rising while emission holds near 3M a year, the lines can cross.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the emission schedule: at 3M VVV a year the mint is the single number that sets inflation, and any further scheduled cut would move the reading down. Watch the monthly buy-and-burn amount — recently around $234K — since that is the only force pulling supply back, and Venice doubled the per-subscription burn on Apr 27 2026, so subscription growth feeds it directly. Watch Venice platform revenue, which funds the burn entirely. Watch the company treasury of roughly 30M VVV for any change in its stated no-sell stance, which is the largest latent overhang. And expect the framework to keep reading below our supply monitor while treasury and team tokens vest into the circulating count without being sold.
