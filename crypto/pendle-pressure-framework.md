Pendle has minted **zero PENDLE** in more than twelve months — total supply read exactly **281,527,448.4585** on-chain at both ends of the window and at seven dates in between. Every token that reached the market came out of storage: **1.70M** paid from the incentive reserve and **2.81M** escaping the retired vePENDLE lock contract, **4.51M** of sell pressure against **3.66M** of buy pressure from a fee-funded buyback and fresh sPENDLE staking. That is **+0.50% net** over 90 days against our supply monitor at **+3.08%**, a **2.59 point** gap we explain rather than adopt. Both sell rows are fading, so the forward reading is **-1.31%**.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending July 20 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **PENDLE at +0.50% net**. Sell pressure totals **4.51M PENDLE** and buy pressure **3.66M PENDLE**, against a circulating base of **171.63M PENDLE**. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+3.08%**, a gap of about **2.59 percentage points**, which is over tolerance and ships a monitor-gap flag on the Pendle overview. Most of that gap is mechanism rather than error: the monitor tracks the **gross** release of **4.51M** against its own **+5.13M**, while the framework nets off the **3.66M** that the buyback and the sPENDLE staking contract absorbed, because the absorbing contract is not held as non-circulating upstream. Pendle is best characterized as a **fixed-supply token whose float is governed entirely by staking behaviour**, not by issuance.

## Sell pressure: where new PENDLE comes from

The honest answer is that no new PENDLE comes from anywhere. Sell #1, protocol inflation, is **1.70M PENDLE** over 90 days, and none of it is minted. The PENDLE ERC-20 contract reported the identical total supply at the April 21 2026 window open and the July 20 2026 close, and at every interior date checked back to July 2025 — no mint function fired and no token was burned. What Pendle actually does is pay weekly liquidity incentives out of a pre-minted reserve held in a governance multisig, and that multisig's balance fell from **22.55M** to **20.85M** across the window in four round transfers. The documented terminal rate of **2% a year** on total supply would imply **1.39M**, so the reserve ran slightly ahead of schedule this quarter; the on-chain figure is the one that ships, and the trailing twelve-month drain of **6.28M** confirms the 2% rate is real.

Sell #5, legacy lock expiries, is the row most readers will not have on their ledger, and at **2.81M PENDLE** it is the larger of the two live sell rows. When Pendle retired vePENDLE for sPENDLE in January 2026, the multi-year vote-escrow locks already in place were not cancelled — they simply run to term. The deprecated vePENDLE contract fell from **67.14M** to **64.33M** PENDLE as those locks expired and holders withdrew, and that is genuine supply arriving on the float. It is also nearly over: the first two weeks of the window released **2.11M** of the total, while the last 60 days released only **0.33M**. Sell #2, vesting unlocks, is **zero** — Pendle team, investor and advisor allocations completed vesting in September 2024. Sell #3, Foundation and unscheduled unlocks, is **zero**: the Pendle protocol treasury took tokens in rather than sending any out, and the developer multisig held nothing at all at both ends. Sell #4, long-term locked or bankruptcy, is zero — there is no Pendle estate and no trustee distribution.

## Buy pressure: where new PENDLE goes

Buy #1, the programmatic buyback, is **1.27M PENDLE** and it is the cleanest number on the page. Pendle routes **80%** of its V2 yield and swap fees into a buyback contract, harvested every two weeks and executed over the following week on a one-hourly time-weighted average. Reading the PENDLE transfer log directly, that contract took in **1,266,162.6 PENDLE** across **583** separate fills during the window and pushed **1,279,765.3** back out in seven deliveries, which reconciles to the wei against its opening balance and its closing balance of zero. It accumulates nothing. Sizing the same flow independently from protocol revenue of roughly **$3.37M** in the window at the 80% share lands near **1.35M PENDLE**, close enough to confirm the on-chain read.

Where those tokens go matters, and it is the question most Pendle write-ups get wrong. The bought PENDLE is **not burned**. It is delivered into the sPENDLE staking contract and distributed to active stakers in staked form. That is still a float sink, because Pendle's own supply definition excludes the sPENDLE contract, the vePENDLE contract, the ecosystem fund and the project multisigs from circulating supply — and exit is not instant, requiring a **14-day** withdrawal or a **5%** instant-redemption fee. Buy #4, new long-term lock, is **2.39M PENDLE** and is the single largest buy-side row. The sPENDLE contract grew by **3.67M** over 90 days, but **1.28M** of that was the buyback delivery already counted in Buy #1, so the organic figure — tokens holders staked with their own PENDLE — is **2.39M**. Buy #2, protocol fee burn, is **zero**: Pendle destroys no PENDLE at all, which is the structural reason this token can never go deflationary through burning. Buy #3, Foundation buy, is zero, with no discretionary Pendle treasury purchase disclosed.

## Foundation and overhang

Pendle's team-controlled overhang is small and readable, which is unusual. The largest piece is the **incentive reserve multisig**, holding **20.85M PENDLE** — about 7.4% of total supply — and it is the wallet that funds Sell #1. At the observed drain rate it holds roughly a decade of incentives, and because Pendle never mints, this reserve is the entire supply of future emissions. The **protocol treasury** holds only **78,377 PENDLE** and gained tokens during the window rather than losing them, while the **developer multisig** holds nothing whatsoever. Two further contracts hold PENDLE but are not team-controlled: the sPENDLE staking contract at **36.87M**, which is staker-owned and is where the Pendle buyback destination lands, and the retired vePENDLE contract at **64.33M**, which is user-owned and draining through Sell #5. There is no bankruptcy residual. Every one of these balances is re-read on each rebuild; if any of them falls between refreshes, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How PENDLE compares to other DeFi fee tokens

The natural comparison class is fee-sharing DeFi governance tokens that run a vote-escrow or staking model — the Curve lineage and its descendants. Most of that class shares one structural weakness: they keep minting. A Curve-style token pays liquidity incentives out of fresh issuance, so locking merely slows a supply curve that never stops rising. Pendle has already stepped off that curve. Its total supply has been fixed at **281.53M** for over a year, and the weekly incentive it pays is a transfer out of a finite pre-minted reserve, not a mint. That makes the Pendle supply question a distribution question with a hard ceiling rather than an issuance question with none — a materially better structural position than an uncapped emitter, and one no amount of vote-escrow lockup can replicate on a token that still mints.

Against exchange tokens that run quarterly buyback-and-burn, Pendle looks weaker on one axis and stronger on another. It is weaker because Pendle destroys nothing: a burn is permanent, whereas the tokens the Pendle buyback removes sit in the sPENDLE contract and can return to the float after a 14-day withdrawal. A burn-based token's deflation is irreversible; Pendle's is a behavioural equilibrium that unwinds the moment stakers leave. It is stronger because the Pendle buyback is continuous and mechanically tied to revenue at a documented **80%** share rather than announced quarterly at management discretion, and because the offsetting staking flow is large — over **101M PENDLE**, roughly 36% of total supply, now sits in the two lock contracts combined, up from about 20% under the old vePENDLE model. The comparison that matters most, though, is with itself a year ago: the switch to sPENDLE plus an algorithmic incentive module that cut emissions by about **71%** has turned a token that leaked supply into one whose float is shrinking.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the incentive reserve multisig. Its balance is the entire forward Sell #1 row, and after the algorithmic incentive module cut emissions the drain fell from **1.1M** in the window's first 30 days to **0.6M** across the last 60 — if it re-accelerates, the forward reading of **-1.31%** weakens immediately. Watch the retired vePENDLE contract drain to zero: only about **0.5M** of expiry is projected for the next 90 days, and once those legacy locks finish unwinding the largest sell row on this page disappears permanently. Watch Pendle protocol revenue, which is the whole of Buy #1 — TVL fell from about **$1.44B** in May 2026 to about **$1.02B** by late June 2026, and the buyback shrinks one-for-one with fees. Watch net sPENDLE staking flow, the largest and least predictable row at **2.39M**, since it is holder behaviour rather than a protocol rule and could reverse. And watch for any governance proposal touching the terminal **2%** incentive rate, which is the only lever that could restart meaningful supply growth.

## Summary

Pendle is a yield-tokenization protocol whose token has not been minted once in over twelve months: total supply is fixed at 281.53M PENDLE, and all supply movement is redistribution between a finite incentive reserve, a retired lock contract, and the sPENDLE staking contract. The framework reads +0.50% net for the last 90 days — 4.51M released against 3.66M absorbed by a fee-funded buyback and fresh staking — and projects -1.31% forward as the legacy vePENDLE unwind exhausts and the algorithmic incentive module holds emissions down. The key risk is that the deflation is behavioural rather than structural: Pendle burns nothing, so the 2.39M of new staking and the 36.87M already staked can leave after a 14-day withdrawal, and the buyback shrinks with a revenue line that has been falling since May 2026. The key structural fact is the ceiling — with the mint dormant, the 20.85M PENDLE left in the incentive reserve is the entire supply of future Pendle emissions.
