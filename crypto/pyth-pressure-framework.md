## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **PYTH at about −0.1% net** on the forward view: no scheduled unlock, no protocol inflation, and a programmatic buyback that quietly removes supply. Our supply monitor reads the realized trailing-90-day change at **+36.97%** — versus the framework's **+26.90%** read for the same window — a gap of about **10 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. That gap is not a disagreement about events: both reads count the identical **2.13B** unlock that fired May 19 2026. The only difference is the denominator — the monitor divides the jump by the smaller pre-unlock supply, the framework by today's larger circulating supply. Looking forward, PYTH is **structurally flat with a mild deflationary tilt**: a fixed cap, no emission, and a buyback as the only active flow.

## Sell pressure: where new PYTH comes from

For the next 90 days, every sell row is **zero**. Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is zero because Pyth Network has no perpetual emission: the 10B max supply is fixed, and new PYTH enters the market only at scheduled unlock dates, never block by block the way a proof-of-stake chain mints rewards. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the row that matters for PYTH, and it is zero in this window. Pyth launched in November 2023 with 85% of supply locked, released in four equal cliffs at six, eighteen, thirty, and forty-two months. The thirty-month cliff — about **2.13B PYTH** — fired on **May 19 2026**, inside the prior window, lifting circulating supply from roughly 5.75B to 7.87B. The final forty-two-month cliff does not arrive until **May 19 2027**, so no scheduled tokens reach the market before September 14 2026.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also zero as a flow. The DAO treasury now holds the ecosystem-growth and publisher-reward allocations that the May 19 2026 cliff unlocked, but those are programmatic buckets with no dated discretionary release pending in this window. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate or court-ordered distribution applies to PYTH.

## Buy pressure: where new PYTH goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the only active flow in either direction, at about **7.5M PYTH** over 90 days. Under a DAO-approved program that began in early 2026, the Pythian Council spends roughly a third of monthly DAO-treasury revenue buying PYTH on the open market; the bought tokens are returned to the **Pyth DAO Treasury** and held there. Recent monthly buys confirm the run-rate — about **2.47M PYTH** in April 2026 and **2.57M PYTH** in May 2026 — so roughly 2.5M a month, or about 7.5M across the window. Because the destination is the treasury rather than a burn address, this is buyback-and-hold: it removes PYTH from the freely-trading float, but the coins are not destroyed.

Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero: Pyth Network runs no on-chain fee burn, so nothing is permanently destroyed. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero, with no discretionary open-market buying outside the published buyback and no new escrow announced in the window.

## Foundation and overhang

PYTH's overhang is concentrated in the **Pyth DAO Treasury**, which now controls the ecosystem-growth, publisher-reward, protocol-development and private-sale allocations released by the May 19 2026 cliff, plus the PYTH steadily accumulated by the monthly buyback. These are non-circulating, on-chain, and trackable, but they sit under DAO governance rather than a fixed release schedule — capacity exists, timing is set by vote. The framework books no discretionary release beyond the buyback flow and re-checks the monthly purchase reports and the unlock calendar on a roughly bi-weekly walk. If a treasury balance falls between refreshes — for example, if the DAO votes to deploy an ecosystem allocation into the market — that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How PYTH compares to other fixed-cap unlock-driven tokens

PYTH belongs to the class of **fixed-cap, cliff-unlock tokens** — closer to a venture-style vesting schedule than to a continuous-emission chain. Unlike an uncapped proof-of-stake L1 that mints rewards every block, PYTH has a hard 10B ceiling and zero perpetual inflation; its supply curve is a staircase, flat between unlock dates and stepping up sharply on each cliff. That makes the framework read swing hard with the calendar: the window that contained May 19 2026 looked intensely inflationary, while the very next window — with no cliff — reads flat.

The contrast worth drawing is with tokens that burn fees or buy back and destroy, which trend net-deflationary on a continuous basis. PYTH's buyback is real but modest and goes to the treasury rather than a burn, so it slows float growth between cliffs without reversing the long-run dilution that the remaining unlock will add in May 2027. For an inflation lens, that means PYTH reads as **step-function supply**: long flat stretches punctuated by large scheduled jumps, with a small buyback nibbling at the float in between.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the monthly PYTH purchase reports — the ~2.5M-per-month buyback pace is the single number that decides whether the window stays mildly deflationary or drifts toward flat. Watch DAO governance, since the treasury now holds the freshly unlocked ecosystem and publisher allocations, and any vote to deploy them into the market would add a Sell #3 flow. Watch network revenue, because the buyback is funded by a third of treasury income, so growth in Pyth's data-product revenue scales the buy side. And keep the calendar in view: the final unlock cliff arrives **May 19 2027**, well outside this window, but it is the next large supply event on the horizon.
