DoubleZero's 2Z token has been one of the quietest supply stories in crypto for nine months — and is about to become one of the loudest. Only **30.0M 2Z** reached the market over the last 90 days, for a framework reading of **+0.85% net**, because every locked wallet on the Solana chain sat completely untouched. On **Oct 2 2026**, the one-year cliff of DoubleZero's four-year Standard Lockup releases roughly **1,620M 2Z** across six investor and team buckets, taking the forward reading to **+47.54% net** against a circulating base of just **3,469.7M 2Z**. The 10B cap on 2Z is real, but it constrains nothing that matters in the next 90 days.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

Over the last 90 days the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads DoubleZero at **+0.85% net**: **30.0M 2Z** of sell pressure against **0.5M 2Z** of buy-side offset, on a circulating base of **3,469.7M 2Z**. Our supply monitor reads the same trailing window at **-0.05%** — a gap of **0.90 percentage points**, which is over the 0.5pp tolerance and ships a **monitor-gap warning**. The reconciliation is a classification difference that DoubleZero documents itself: the 30.0M that left the DoubleZero Foundation vault on **Jul 17 2026** had already been counted as circulating while it sat unmoved, so the monitor sees no change where the framework sees supply reaching the market. Forward, the framework reads **+47.54%**. 2Z is **a frozen float facing a single-day cliff** — a token whose supply risk is not a rate at all, but a date.

## Sell pressure: where new 2Z comes from

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the entire story of DoubleZero, and this build books it at **zero** for the trailing window and roughly **1,620M 2Z** for the forward one. The reason for the zero matters. DoubleZero's own tokenomics disclosure schedules the Validators allocation remainder — about **30M 2Z** — for release one year from purchase date, in April or May 2026, squarely inside the trailing window. Reading the Solana chain directly, none of it moved. Every large 2Z wallet outside the DoubleZero Foundation is untouched: the biggest, holding **1,750M 2Z**, has not transacted since September 2024; a **299.4M** wallet has been static since the token generation event on **Oct 2 2025**; a **326.4M** wallet last moved in December 2025. Tokens that vest on paper but never leave the wallet are not sell pressure, so the framework books the realised outflow rather than the calendar entitlement, and the undrawn tranche is carried as overhang instead.

The forward figure is a different kind of number. DoubleZero's Standard Lockup is a four-year release running from launch on Oct 2 2025 to October 2029, and its first anniversary falls inside the next 90-day window. Two independent unlock trackers price that event within 2.5% of each other — one itemises it bucket by bucket as Jump Crypto **574M**, Malbec Labs **350M**, Institutions **300M**, Team **250M**, Contributors **100M** and Builders **50M**, totalling **1,620M 2Z**; the other publishes a single headline release of **1.66B 2Z** on the same date. The itemised split is exactly one quarter of each locked bucket, which is precisely what the first year of a four-year vest should look like, so the framework ships the more conservative **1,620M**.

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**, and unusually for a young network it is zero on measured evidence rather than assumption. All **10,000M 2Z** were minted at launch. Reading the SPL mint on Solana this session returns a total supply of **9,998.3M 2Z** — roughly **1.7M** below the launch mint. The mint authority is still live and DoubleZero's white paper does describe minting new 2Z as rewards for computational resources, but that mechanism belongs to a staking and security layer the project describes as a future capability. Until it switches on, the only force acting on the DoubleZero mint is the burn, and 2Z supply has moved in one direction only: down. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is **zero** and structurally so, with no estate, trustee schedule or court-ordered distribution anywhere near 2Z.

That leaves Sell #3 — foundation and unscheduled unlocks — carrying the whole trailing window at **30.0M 2Z**. The DoubleZero Foundation vault was funded with **1,650M 2Z** in September 2024 and holds **1,269.6M** today. Its complete outflow history is short enough to read in full: **287.5M** and **37.5M** in December 2025, **11.8M** and **13.6M** in February 2026, then nothing until **30.0M** left in two transfers on **Jul 17 2026**. Stripping out the launch-distribution phase, the cadence since February runs at roughly **30M** a quarter, which is what the framework projects forward alongside the cliff.

## Buy pressure: where new 2Z goes

Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is the only buy row DoubleZero actually fills, at about **0.5M 2Z** per 90 days. DoubleZero burns 2Z for integrity: partly to keep the distribution between contributing parties equitable, and partly to make it expensive for a network contributor to push inorganic traffic through the fibre in order to inflate its share of rewards. Because minting is dormant, that burn is directly measurable from the chain — **1.7M 2Z** destroyed net across the **292 days** since launch, a rate near **6,000 2Z** a day. It is a genuine deflationary mechanism and it is also, against a 1,620M cliff, arithmetically irrelevant.

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is carried at **zero**, but not because DoubleZero lacks one. On **Apr 16 2026** the project launched Edge, a subscription service that sells real-time Solana shred data to trading firms over the DoubleZero fibre network. Revenue arrives in dollars, is converted into open-market 2Z purchases, and a tenth of every purchase is burned permanently. Structurally this is exactly the mechanism the Pressure Framework wants to see. The problem is scale and verification: only a single public estimate carries a quantum, putting current Edge revenue near **$205K** annualised while the platform runs on a handful of seats, and no buyback wallet has been disclosed for on-chain confirmation. One unconfirmed estimate does not clear the framework's triangulation bar, so Buy #1 ships at zero and stays under watch.

Buy #3 — foundation buy — is **zero**: the DoubleZero Foundation vault has only ever sent 2Z out, and nothing has flowed back in since it was funded. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is **zero** too, and for a simple reason: 2Z staking does not exist yet. DoubleZero's disclosure describes staking as something the token will eventually support, so there is currently no contract on Solana capable of taking 2Z off the market for a fixed term. The net effect is that DoubleZero enters its largest supply event with a buy ledger of **0.5M 2Z** — roughly three hundredths of one percent of what is scheduled to arrive.

## Foundation and overhang

The team-controlled overhang on DoubleZero is enormous and, unusually, fully readable on Solana. The DoubleZero Foundation and Ecosystem allocation is **29%** of the 10B supply, and its vault holds **1,269.6M 2Z** with no published release schedule at all — the largest genuinely discretionary pool in the ledger, and the only one that has demonstrated a willingness to move. Behind it sit the Standard Lockup buckets: Jump Crypto at **28%**, Malbec Labs at **14%**, Institutions at **12%**, the DoubleZero team at **10%**, Contributors at **4%** and Builders at **2%**, together roughly **5.2B 2Z** still outside circulation. A separate **1,750M 2Z** wallet, untouched since September 2024, is the single largest identified balance on the chain. Finally there is the roughly **30M** Validators tranche that vested on paper in April or May 2026 and never left its wallet — a small overhang, but a live demonstration that DoubleZero's calendar and its chain do not move together.

Every one of these balances is a token account readable directly on Solana, so each is re-read at every rebuild rather than tracked through disclosure. That is what caught the Jul 17 2026 foundation outflow within days of it happening. It is also what keeps the forward number honest: DoubleZero's lockups appear to be contractual rather than enforced by an escrow contract, which means nothing releases mechanically on Oct 2 2026 — the holders simply become free to move, and the framework will see it when they do. If any of these balances falls between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh rather than being absorbed quietly into the vesting row.

## How 2Z compares to other young capped-supply DePIN tokens

DoubleZero belongs to the class of capped-supply infrastructure tokens launched with a small float and a long insider vest — the same structural family as most recent DePIN and infrastructure launches, where the interesting number is never the cap. 2Z is capped at 10B and, unlike an uncapped continuous-emission layer-1 such as Solana or Cardano, it genuinely cannot inflate through a block subsidy today. But only **34.7%** of that cap is circulating, and the remaining **65%** is governed by a vesting calendar rather than a protocol curve. Against a halving-model chain like Bitcoin, where the cap and the emission schedule are the same fact and the float converges smoothly on the cap, DoubleZero's supply arrives in a lump on a legal anniversary. Predictability of date is not the same thing as gentleness of impact.

The comparison that flatters DoubleZero is with exchange tokens that run quarterly buybacks funded by real revenue. DoubleZero has built the same shape of mechanism — Edge revenue converts to open-market 2Z buys with a mandatory burn — and it is a better mechanism than most DePIN networks manage, because it is tied to a service institutional traders actually pay for rather than to token emissions circling back on themselves. The difference is maturity: an established exchange token burns a meaningful fraction of float every quarter, while DoubleZero's burn currently runs at **0.5M 2Z** against a float of **3,469.7M**. The buyback is the right long-run answer to the unlock problem; it is simply nowhere near large enough yet to be one.

Compared with peers whose one-year cliffs have already passed, DoubleZero's position is unusual in one respect worth naming: its trailing 90 days show essentially nothing moving, which makes the trailing reading a poor guide to the forward one. A reader who anchors on the last quarter — a monitor reading of **-0.05%** and a framework reading of **+0.85%** — will badly misjudge what the next quarter holds at **+47.54%**. Frozen floats are not stable floats; they are floats whose supply event has not happened yet.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

**Oct 2 2026** is the date that decides this reading — the one-year anniversary of the token generation event, when roughly **1,620M 2Z** across six buckets becomes free to move. Because DoubleZero's lockups look contractual rather than escrow-enforced, watch the wallets rather than the calendar: whether Jump Crypto's and Malbec Labs' balances actually fall in the days after the anniversary is the difference between a paper unlock and a real one. Second, watch the DoubleZero Foundation vault at **1,269.6M 2Z**, which fired **30.0M** on **Jul 17 2026** with no schedule behind it and could fire again at any size. Third, watch DoubleZero Edge seat growth: at current revenue the buyback is immaterial, but the mechanism scales linearly with subscriptions, and a move from a handful of test seats toward the published tier capacity would put a genuine bid under the token for the first time. Fourth, watch whether 2Z staking ships — activating it would switch on Sell #1 as real minted inflation while simultaneously creating the first mechanism capable of locking 2Z away in Buy #4.

## Summary

The MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads DoubleZero (2Z) at **+0.85% net** over the last 90 days and **+47.54% net** over the next, against a circulating base of **3,469.7M 2Z** and a hard cap of 10B. The structural mechanism is not inflation at all — 2Z has never minted a token on net and its on-chain supply has fallen by **1.7M** since launch — but a four-year insider lockup whose first quarter releases about **1,620M 2Z** on **Oct 2 2026**. The key risk is that DoubleZero's buy side, a **0.5M** quarterly integrity burn plus an unverified revenue-funded buyback, absorbs a fraction of a percent of that release. The ceiling is the 10B cap, which is real and permanent, and which by 2029 will have been fully distributed — meaning the constraint that eventually protects 2Z holders is the same schedule that dilutes them first.
