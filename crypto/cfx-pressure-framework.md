# CFX Inflation Analysis · July 2026 · Mixed flows, supply roughly steady

Conflux adds about **22.3M CFX** to the market every 90 days, and every single coin of it is freshly minted by the protocol: Conflux runs proof-of-work and proof-of-stake at the same time, so miners and stakers are both paid in new CFX. Against that, buy pressure is **zero** — Conflux operates no buyback, and its base-fee and storage burns are real but far too small to register at ledger scale. That leaves the framework at **+0.43% net**, against our supply monitor at **+0.30%**. CFX vesting finished in 2024, so nothing on a calendar can surprise this reading; the only thing that moves it is a governance vote on the mint rate.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending July 20 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **CFX at +0.43% net**. Sell pressure totals **22.3M CFX** and buy pressure **zero**, against a circulating base of **5,213.89M CFX**. Our supply monitor reads the realised last-90-day change at **+0.30%**, a gap of about **0.13 percentage points** — comfortably inside tolerance, so no monitor-gap chip ships on the Conflux overview. The reconciliation is unusually clean for a hybrid chain because the number was measured directly rather than modelled: the Conflux total-issued counter read **5,775.87M CFX** on April 21 2026 and **5,798.29M CFX** on July 20 2026, and the chain's own reward parameters were byte-identical at both ends of that window. Conflux is best characterised as a **quiet, uncapped chain with a slow two-engine mint and no offset at all**.

## Sell pressure: where new CFX comes from

Sell #1, protocol inflation, is **22.3M CFX** over 90 days, and it is the entire sell ledger. Conflux is unusual in minting from two engines simultaneously. The proof-of-work engine pays a block reward of **0.40 CFX** to every block, and Conflux closes a block roughly every half second, which works out to about **6.24M CFX** across the window. The proof-of-stake engine pays interest to everyone who locks CFX into staking, at an annual yield the protocol computes from the ratio of circulating supply to staked supply — about **8.4%** on the **882.37M CFX** currently staked, or roughly **18M CFX** per 90 days. Both streams are newly created coins, not transfers. Conflux has no supply cap and no halving schedule, so nothing puts an end to this emission — only a governance vote can slow it, and one did: a community parameter vote halved the block reward from 0.8 to 0.4 CFX, effective **Apr 7 2026**, two weeks before this window opened.

The other three sell rows are all **zero**, and each for a different structural reason. Sell #2, vesting unlocks, is zero because Conflux finished distributing its founding allocations in 2024 — the chain still publishes a two-year and a four-year unlock balance, and both read exactly zero, which is as clean a proof of an expired schedule as a token can offer. Sell #3, Foundation and unscheduled unlocks, is zero because no project-controlled pool moved in the window. Sell #4, long-term locked or bankruptcy, is zero because Conflux has no estate and no court-ordered distribution. That concentration matters for a reader: with vesting genuinely spent, Conflux inflation is a single mechanism, not a stack of them, and it is fully described by two numbers that a governance vote controls.

## Buy pressure: where new CFX goes

The Conflux buy ledger is empty, and that is the sharpest fact on this page. Buy #1, programmatic buyback, is **zero**: Conflux operates no buyback contract, publishes no buyback dashboard, and routes no protocol revenue back into CFX. Buy #3, Foundation buy, is **zero** — no treasury open-market purchase of CFX has been disclosed. Buy #4, new long-term lock, is **zero**: staked CFX did grow over the window, from **833.42M** to **882.37M**, but proof-of-stake positions unbond on a short timer and already sit inside circulating supply, so the framework does not treat them as a removal.

Buy #2, protocol fee burn, is the interesting zero. Conflux genuinely burns CFX in two ways — a share of every transaction's base fee is destroyed rather than paid to miners, and sponsored storage collateral converts into non-transferable storage credits, permanently removing the underlying CFX. Both ratios are set by the same governance vote that sets the block reward. The problem is throughput: Conflux settled about **11,256** core-space transactions on July 20 2026, which is a chain running well under one transaction per second. At that volume the base-fee burn is orders of magnitude below the ledger's smallest reporting unit. The permanent burn address confirms it directly — it held **572,964,410.53 CFX** at both ends of the window, unchanged to the decimal. That balance is Conflux's accumulated historical burn, including the 76M CFX ecosystem burn executed in 2025, and it is now static. The mechanism is alive; the flow is not.

## Foundation and overhang

Conflux has less overhang than most chains of its age, because the vesting schedule genuinely finished. Two positions are tracked. The first is the **Conflux ecosystem fund's proof-of-stake position** — roughly **500M CFX** committed to staking under a 2025 community vote, and still staked, sitting inside a network-wide proof-of-stake total of **882.37M CFX**. It has no published release schedule and is refreshed against the chain on each rebuild. The second is the **non-circulating residual**, the difference between Conflux total supply of **5,225.31M CFX** and circulating supply of **5,213.89M CFX** — about **11.4M CFX**, a rounding-scale position with no release plan. Neither moved this window. If either balance falls between refreshes, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh. Worth noting what is deliberately excluded: the 572.96M CFX in the burn address is destroyed, not held, and the 931.81M CFX sitting in the governance deposit contract belongs to ordinary holders, not to the project.

## How CFX compares to other hybrid proof-of-work chains

Conflux belongs to a small class: chains that pay a proof-of-work block subsidy and a proof-of-stake yield at the same time, out of the same uncapped supply. Compared with a pure halving-model chain, Conflux gives up the thing that makes those assets legible — there is no cap, no halving calendar, and therefore no date at which issuance mechanically falls. What Conflux substitutes is governance: the block reward and the staking interest rate are both live parameters set by on-chain votes, which is why the reward could go from 0.8 to 0.4 CFX in a single round rather than waiting years for a scheduled event. That is faster and more flexible than a halving, and also less predictable, because the same mechanism can vote the reward back up.

Compared with uncapped continuous-emission proof-of-stake layer-1s, Conflux looks conservative on rate and weak on offset. An emission of **+0.43%** per 90 days annualises to under 2%, which is at the low end of that peer group and materially below the double-digit issuance some staking chains still run. But most of those peers pair emission with an EIP-1559-style fee burn that scales with usage, so their net reading improves when the chain gets busy. Conflux has the burn mechanism but not the usage, so its net is essentially its gross. Compared with exchange tokens that run quarterly buybacks, the contrast is starker still: those assets have a revenue-to-token pipe that can push net supply negative, while the Conflux buy ledger is simply empty. The honest summary is that CFX inflation is low, mechanically simple, and entirely one-directional.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

The one thing that can move this reading is a chain parameter vote. Conflux runs these in rounds, and the most recent published result — Round 20, closed **Feb 6 2026** — is what halved the block reward and held the interest rate at 3.26%; the next round's result is the single highest-impact watch item, because it sets both halves of Sell #1 at once. Watch the staking interest rate specifically: it is the larger of the two mint streams, so a cut there moves the framework reading more than a block-reward cut would. Watch the proof-of-stake total, which climbed from 833.42M to 882.37M CFX this window — the protocol's yield falls as more CFX stakes, so a rising stake base quietly reduces the mint rate without any vote at all. Watch whether the Conflux ecosystem fund's public-company treasury programme, authorised by governance in 2025 with a stated lock-up of no less than four years, actually deploys CFX; a deployment would land in Sell #3 and the lock-up would land in Buy #4. And watch transaction volume, because Conflux already has a working fee burn and needs only usage to make Buy #2 a real row.

## Summary

Conflux is an uncapped hybrid layer-1 whose supply grows about 0.43% every 90 days, and all of that growth is fresh mint: roughly 6.24M CFX of proof-of-work block rewards plus about 18M CFX of proof-of-stake interest, totalling 22.3M CFX. Nothing offsets it — Conflux runs no buyback, and while a base-fee burn and a storage burn are both live, the chain settles only around eleven thousand transactions a day, so the permanent burn address did not move at all across the window. The key structural strength is that vesting is genuinely finished, with the chain's own two-year and four-year unlock balances reading zero, so there is no unlock cliff left to fear. The key risk is the mirror image: with no cap and no halving, the mint rate is a governance parameter rather than a promise, and the same vote that halved the block reward in April 2026 could reverse it.
