## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **ADA at +1.0% net** on the forward view, driven entirely by reserve-funded staking and treasury emission with no offsetting burn. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+0.98%**, essentially identical to the framework, so there is **no monitor-gap chip**. ADA has a **45 billion hard cap** and is not minted above it — but it is far from fully circulating, so the protocol keeps moving coins out of reserve every epoch. ADA is therefore **mildly, structurally inflationary**, at a low-single-digit pace that gently declines over time.

## Sell pressure: where new ADA comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the entire story, at about **363M ADA** over the next 90 days. This needs a careful read, because ADA is capped at 45 billion and no new coins are minted above that ceiling. Instead, new circulating supply is **released from a fixed reserve** that was set aside at genesis. Every 5-day epoch, the protocol moves **0.3%** of whatever remains in the reserve into a reward pot. That pot is then split: **20%** goes to the treasury and **80%** is paid out as staking rewards. About 18 epochs fall in a 90-day window, and the reserve currently holds roughly **7.8 billion ADA** — so the window releases around 363M ADA, all of it new float.

Because the release is a fixed share of a shrinking reserve, the rate is **disinflationary** by design: each year the reserve is smaller, so each year the drip is smaller too. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero**: the 2017 public sale, the founding organisations and the foundation finished distributing their allocations years ago, so no cliff hits the market. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also zero; treasury spending runs through community governance, and a June 2026 proposal to fund an event fell short of the votes it needed and never executed. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where ADA goes

This is the short half of the ledger: **nothing**. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is zero, because Cardano runs no buyback program. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is also zero, and this is the detail people most often get wrong. Cardano does **not burn transaction fees**. Fees are collected into a pot and recycled to stakers and the treasury, so they circulate rather than disappear. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero. With no burn and no buyback anywhere on the network, there is no force pulling supply back, so the reserve drip lands net positive.

## Foundation and overhang

ADA has no classic unlock overhang — the early allocations are fully distributed. The one pool worth naming is the **treasury**, which receives 20% of every epoch's reward pot and now holds well over a billion ADA. That treasury is not a stockpile waiting to dump: it can only be spent through community governance votes, each proposal ratified by delegate representatives. The framework books no discretionary treasury outflow beyond the reserve emission already counted in Sell #1, and re-checks the governance queue on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if an approved proposal pushes a large ADA sum to market, that outflow would enter Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How ADA compares to other capped proof-of-stake chains

ADA sits in an unusual middle ground: it is **hard-capped like Bitcoin** but emits like a **proof-of-stake reward chain**. The cap means there is a hard ceiling and no open-ended mint, which feels like a fixed-supply token. But because most of the supply was reserved rather than pre-circulated, the protocol still releases coins every epoch — so for an inflation lens, ADA reads as mildly inflationary today, not flat. The key difference from an uncapped staking chain is direction: ADA's issuance only goes down, because it is a shrinking percentage of a shrinking reserve.

The contrast worth drawing is with chains that pair issuance with a fee burn. Some proof-of-stake networks burn enough base fees to offset or even reverse their staking emission, which can flip them net-deflationary in busy periods. ADA has no such brake — fees recycle instead of burning — so every coin the reserve releases lands as net new float. That makes ADA cleaner to read than a burn-and-mint chain: there is one force, the reserve drip, and it is always positive and always slowly fading.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the reserve release rate — at 0.3% of the remaining reserve per epoch, the single number that matters is how fast the reserve depletes, and it only eases from here. Watch the treasury governance queue: an approved, large ADA proposal that actually pushes coins to market would be the one thing that lifts sell pressure beyond the reserve drip, and it would enter Sell #3. Watch staking participation, since the more ADA is staked and re-staked, the slower fresh rewards reach the open float. And note there is still no burn or buyback proposal in play — for an inflation read, that absence is what keeps ADA mildly positive rather than flat.

ADA is a hard-capped proof-of-stake token that still drips new supply from a fixed reserve. The reserve releases about 363M ADA over 90 days into staking rewards and the treasury, while nothing on the network burns ADA back out, leaving the framework at about +1.0% net. Our supply monitor reads +0.98% realized — effectively the same number, because the reserve drip is exactly what it measures, so there is no monitor gap. ADA stays mildly inflationary, with issuance gently fading year over year as the reserve shrinks toward the 45 billion cap.
