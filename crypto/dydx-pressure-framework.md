## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **DYDX at about 0.00% net** on the forward view — vesting is finished and there is no booked buy or sell flow ahead. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+1.74%**, versus the framework's **+1.48%** read for the same trailing window, which still contained the final **~12.5M** of investor unlocks — a gap of about **0.26 percentage points**, inside tolerance, so no ⚠ chip ships. The one nuance is the buyback: dYdX buys DYDX and **stakes** it rather than burning it, and staked coins still count as circulating, so the purchase removes float without showing up as a drop in reported supply. The supply story is turning **from headwind to neutral-to-tailwind**.

## Sell pressure: where new DYDX comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**. dYdX Chain mints no new DYDX; staking and trading rewards are paid in a stablecoin out of real protocol fees, so emission simply does not exist for this token. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero going forward**: the original five-year distribution released its **final monthly tranche on June 1 2026**, at roughly **4.16M a month**, with about **12.5M** falling across the trailing 90-day window. After that final tranche, nothing is scheduled. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — and Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — are both zero.

## Buy pressure: where new DYDX goes

Buy #1 — the programmatic buyback — is the live offset. Since **November 13 2025** (after a three-month experimental phase that used 100% of fees), dYdX directs about **75%** of net protocol fees to buy DYDX on the open market and **stake** it — an accumulation of roughly **25M over 90 days**. The key point for an inflation lens: staked tokens still count as circulating, so this buy **is tracked but not booked as supply removal**. Booking a deflation number off a soft estimate of staked accumulation would either double-count or fabricate a supply drop the chain data does not show, so the framework keeps it in the row note instead. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero, because there is no burn; fees fund the stablecoin rewards and the buyback, not destruction. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero.

## Foundation and overhang

DYDX no longer has a classic unlock overhang: the five-year vesting pool was **fully released after June 1 2026**, so the years-long stream of monthly tranches that pressured the token is done. The community and rewards treasuries remain, but they are governance-controlled with no dated release schedule, so the framework books no flow from them until governance acts. The buyback accumulation is a growing pile of **staked DYDX** — an on-chain balance, though surfaced through a JS-gated community tracker rather than a clean public feed. If any of these balances starts to fall between refreshes, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How DYDX compares to other fee-buyback exchange tokens

DYDX belongs to the class of **perpetual-DEX and exchange tokens that route trading fees into buybacks**. The distinction that matters for inflation is what happens to the bought coins. Some exchange tokens run **buyback-and-burn**, destroying the purchased supply, which directly shrinks the reported circulating number and can push a token net-deflationary. dYdX runs **buyback-and-stake**: the coins are bought and locked into staking, which removes them from active float but keeps them counted as circulating supply.

That difference is exactly why DYDX reads flat rather than deflationary on this metric. The float-reducing pressure is real — fewer coins are freely trading — but the supply count does not move, so an inflation lens that measures circulating supply registers no shrink. With vesting finished and emission at zero, the headline reading is neutral, with the staked buyback acting as a quiet tailwind the supply count doesn't capture.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the buyback rate — currently about 75% of net fees — and protocol fee revenue, since together they set how fast staked DYDX accumulates. Watch for any governance move to **burn rather than stake** the bought DYDX; that single change would flip this reading from neutral to outright deflationary, because burned coins leave the circulating count. And confirm that no new unlocks remain on the schedule — the final tranche was June 1 2026, and the framework treats the overhang as closed until any future distribution is announced.
