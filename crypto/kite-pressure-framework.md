Every tracker quotes a vesting schedule of **109.3M KITE** a month, or **327.8M** across a quarter; the four Kite vesting contracts that actually hold those tokens released only **69.4M** over the same period, and nothing at all before **Jun 5 2026**. With no KITE buyback and no KITE burn on the other side, the Pressure Framework reads **+2.90% net** against our supply monitor at **+32.83%** — a gap that is a classification and base-effect artifact on a token that only listed on **Nov 3 2025**, not real sell pressure.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending **Jul 20 2026**, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **KITE at +2.90% net**. Sell pressure totals **69.4M KITE** and buy pressure is **zero**, against a circulating base of **2,391.2M KITE**. Our supply monitor reads the same window at **+32.83%**, a gap of about **29.93 percentage points**, which is far outside tolerance and ships a monitor-gap chip on the Kite overview page. The deep walk closed most of that gap onto a single mechanism: the monitor books the published Kite vesting calendar, and its series steps by about **472M** in one day on **May 7 2026** — the six-month cliff date — while the Kite vesting contracts did not release a single token until **Jun 5 2026**. It then measures that step against a launch-era float of roughly **1.80B**, which magnifies everything. KITE is best characterized as **a hard-capped token whose unlock calendar runs far ahead of its real float**.

## Sell pressure: where new KITE comes from

Sell #1, protocol inflation, is **zero**, and unusually for a layer-1 that is structural rather than temporary. KITE has a hard cap of **10B** tokens and all **10B** were minted at genesis on **Nov 3 2025**. Kite runs no block subsidy and no staking mint. The KITE token contract confirms it: total supply read **9,758.10M** on Ethereum at the open of this window and **9,758.72M** at the close, with the balance of the cap sitting as **240.74M** on BNB Chain and **0.18M** on Avalanche — the same capped ten billion split across bridges, not new issuance. Kite's continuous reward stream, the "piggy bank" model in which participants accrue rewards and permanently forfeit future emissions the moment they claim, pays out of the already-minted Ecosystem and Modules buckets. That makes every reward a distribution of existing KITE, which is why it belongs in the vesting row and never in protocol inflation.

Sell #2, vesting unlocks, is **69.4M KITE** and is the entire Kite sell ledger. This is where the framework parts company with every unlock tracker covering KITE. The published Kite schedule releases **44.05M** from the Modules allocation and **65.22M** from Ecosystem & Community on the first of each month, so three tranches inside a quarter would be **327.8M KITE**. But the four Kite vesting contracts are readable on-chain, and read at three separate block heights they held **3,196.0M** on **Apr 21 2026**, still **3,196.0M** on **Jun 5 2026**, and **3,126.6M** on **Jul 20 2026**. The realised outflow is **69.4M**, and the entire drain fell in the final 45 days. The framework measures supply that reaches the market, so a KITE that vested on paper and never left the lock contract is not sell pressure yet. The same picture holds cumulatively: those contracts held **3,200.0M** on launch day and have released only **73.4M** in the eight and a half months since, against roughly **628M** of calendar entitlement over the same stretch.

Sell #3, Foundation and unscheduled unlocks, is **zero** for the window: outside the four draining Kite vesting contracts, every large non-exchange holding was identical to the token at both ends of the window, so nothing was released at anyone's discretion. The overhang behind those zeros is another matter and is covered below. Sell #4, long-term locked or bankruptcy, is **zero** — Kite is a 2025 launch with no estate, no trustee and no court-ordered distribution of KITE.

## Buy pressure: where new KITE goes

The Kite buy ledger is empty, and all four rows are **zero**. Buy #1, programmatic buyback, is the one with a real mechanism behind it: the Kite token model states that the protocol takes a small commission from each AI-service transaction and can swap that commission for KITE on the open market before distributing it, which is designed to tie KITE demand directly to agent-payment volume. That is a designed mechanism, not an observed one. This session found no executed purchase, no disclosed buying wallet, no buyback dashboard and no dated quantum anywhere on the Kite surfaces or in the news, so the framework carries it at zero and watches it.

Buy #2, protocol fee burn, is **zero** and is unlikely ever to be otherwise, because of a deliberate Kite design choice: gas on the Kite chain is charged in whitelisted stablecoins rather than in KITE, so that agents face predictable costs. A chain whose fees are not denominated in its own token has no fee-burn path at all — ordinary use of Kite never destroys a KITE. Buy #3, Foundation buy, is **zero**: no open-market KITE purchase has been disclosed. Buy #4, new long-term lock, is **zero** for the window, though the mechanism exists — Kite module owners must lock KITE into permanent liquidity to activate a module, and a cluster of roughly twenty holdings of exactly **30.0M KITE** each is consistent with exactly that. Every one of those holdings was unchanged at both ends of the window, so no new lock was added and there is no quantum to book.

## Foundation and overhang

KITE's overhang is the largest single fact about the token, and almost none of it is moving. The investor allocation sits in one contract holding exactly **1,200.0M KITE**, which did not move by a single token across all three block heights — an independent confirmation that the tracker line reading "investors: zero of 1,200M unlocked" is literally true on-chain. Three further pools of **800.0M**, **500.0M** and **500.0M** covering the Kite team allocation are equally untouched, as are operating wallets of **263.2M**, **227.3M** and **168.2M**. The four draining vesting contracts still hold **3,126.6M** between them. On top of all of that sits the quiet one: roughly **555M KITE** that has vested on the published Kite calendar since launch but was never actually withdrawn from the lock, sitting inside those contracts as claimable-but-unclaimed supply. Every one of these balances is read on-chain at each rebuild, and if any of their balances falls between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How KITE compares to other hard-capped launch-era tokens

KITE belongs to the cohort of hard-capped, fully-pre-minted application tokens that listed in the last year and are still working through a multi-year vesting calendar — a very different structure from a proof-of-work chain with a halving or an uncapped proof-of-stake chain minting validator rewards forever. In that cohort the supply question is never "how much is being created", because the answer is always none; it is "how fast is the locked share becoming tradable". KITE has **2,391.2M** of **10B** circulating, so roughly three quarters of the cap is still locked, and the calendar says it unlocks over 36 to 45 months by bucket.

What separates KITE from its peers is the gap between the calendar and reality. Most launch-era tokens with a linear vest see the schedule and the float move together, because the recipients claim promptly; that is precisely the assumption every unlock tracker is built on. Kite does not behave that way — only about **21%** of the calendar entitlement has actually been drawn out of the vesting contracts, and the remainder has quietly accumulated inside them. That matters in both directions. It means the realistic near-term sell pressure is far lower than the headline unlock figures suggest, and it means the backlog is a coiled overhang that could be drawn at any time without a new unlock event. The second structural difference is the buy side: exchange tokens in a similar life stage typically run a quarterly buyback or a fee burn to offset their vest, and layer-1s with native-token gas burn a slice of every transaction. Kite prices its gas in stablecoins by design, so it has neither. There is no mechanism on the Kite chain that removes a KITE from supply — only the module liquidity lock, which parks tokens rather than destroying them.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

First, watch the three scheduled Kite vesting tranches on **Aug 1 2026**, **Sep 1 2026** and **Oct 1 2026**, each about **109.3M KITE** on the published calendar. The number that matters is not the tranche but the draw rate against it — the framework re-reads the four vesting contracts at every rebuild, and a jump toward full drawdown would multiply this reading. Second, watch the vested-but-undrawn backlog of roughly **555M KITE**; a decision to sweep it out would land as a Sell #3 event with no unlock announcement attached. Third, the first Kite team and investor cliff of about **800M KITE** opens around **Nov 3 2026**, just past this window, and it is the largest single dated supply event on the KITE calendar. Fourth, watch for the first executed KITE buyback: the commission-to-KITE swap is written into the Kite token model, and the first disclosed purchase would open Buy #1 for the first time. Fifth, watch whether Kite ever moves gas pricing from stablecoins to KITE, which is the only change that would create a burn path.

## Summary

The MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads Kite at **+2.90% net** over the last 90 days and the same projected forward, against our supply monitor at **+32.83%**. The structural mechanism is a hard **10B** cap fully minted at genesis, which means KITE creates no new supply at all — every token reaching the market is an unlock, and the Kite vesting contracts released only **69.4M** of the **327.8M** the calendar entitled them to. The key risk is that this is a draw-rate story rather than a schedule story: about **555M KITE** is already vested and simply has not been claimed, and there is nothing on the buy side — no KITE buyback executed, no burn possible while Kite charges gas in stablecoins — to absorb it if it is. The ceiling is genuine and it is **10B**, but roughly three quarters of it is still ahead of the market, with the **800M** team and investor cliff of **Nov 3 2026** the next dated step.
