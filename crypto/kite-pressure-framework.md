## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window opening in July 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **KITE at +10.9% net** — supply clearly growing, driven entirely by the monthly vesting unlocks of a token less than a year past its November 3 2025 launch. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+32.69%**, a gap of about **21.8 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. That gap is structural, not a hidden unlock: KITE's **10B** supply is fully pre-minted, so the extra float is the large ecosystem and module pool being deployed after Kite's mainnet went live **April 30 2026**, faster than the vesting schedule alone. KITE is best characterised as a **young L1 that is structurally inflationary on its active float** — coins entering circulation far faster than anything removes them, with the large insider cliff still ahead.

## Sell pressure: where new KITE comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**, and that is the key structural fact. KITE has a fixed **10B** cap and every token is already minted, so the chain issues no new supply. The validator, module and delegator rewards Kite pays are drawn from the ecosystem and module allocations — the same coins that appear as vesting unlocks — not from fresh issuance. Counting them as a separate inflation stream would double-count, so the framework books them once, under vesting.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the real engine, at about **260M KITE** over the next 90 days. The ecosystem and module buckets release roughly **87M KITE** a month, and three monthly tranches fall in the window — about **August 1**, **September 1** and **October 1 2026**. The much larger team and investor allocations stay locked under a one-year cliff that only opens about **November 3 2026**, after this window.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is carried at zero as a quantified flow but is the live overhang. Since mainnet launched April 30 2026, the ecosystem and module pool has been deploying into the float as rewards, grants and incentives faster than the vesting calendar alone; no per-quarter figure is published, so the framework tracks it rather than plugging a number. The locked team allocation (**2,000M**) and investor allocation (**1,200M**) are the large scheduled overhang. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate applies to KITE.

## Buy pressure: where new KITE goes

There is effectively no buy pressure. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: Kite is designed to take a commission on AI-service transactions and route value to KITE, but no executed open-market buyback has been disclosed, and on a young chain the underlying service revenue is still small. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is **zero**, because KITE has no burn mechanism. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is **zero**, with no discretionary open-market buying observed. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is zero as a booked figure, though module owners must permanently lock KITE into liquidity pools to activate their modules, a real removal from circulation with no announced forward quantum. With nothing quantified on the buy side, all of the unlock reaches the float net.

## Foundation and overhang

The overhang on KITE is dominated by three pools. The largest active one is the **ecosystem and community** allocation at **48%** of the 10B supply — partly unlocked at launch and deploying into the float through reward emissions, with the remainder vesting linearly over roughly four years. The **modules** allocation at **20%** vests the same way and funds the reward system. Then sit the locked allocations: **20%** to the team and advisors and **12%** to investors, both held under a one-year cliff and released over the following years starting about **November 3 2026**. The framework books no scheduled team or investor release in this window; if the Foundation's ecosystem balance falls faster than tracked between refreshes, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How KITE compares to other early-unlock Layer-1 chains

KITE belongs to the class of **hard-capped, early-unlock Layer-1s** — a fixed 10B supply, like a capped coin, but with most of that supply still locked and vesting on a multi-year calendar, like a freshly launched token. That is a different shape from an uncapped, continuous-emission chain that mints new coins to stakers every block: KITE cannot issue beyond 10B, so its inflation is entirely a distribution question, not a minting question. It is also different from a mature capped coin whose float is essentially fully unlocked; KITE has only about **2.39B** of its **10B** in circulation, so the dilution ahead is large relative to today's float.

The sharper contrast is with exchange or fee-driven tokens that run active buybacks or burns. KITE has neither live yet — the commission-to-KITE design and the permanent module liquidity locks could become a real counterweight as network usage grows, but at current activity they remove little. So on an inflation lens KITE reads as structurally inflationary on the active float, its supply set to keep climbing as the ecosystem, module, team and investor tranches unlock over the next several years.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the three monthly ecosystem and module unlocks landing about **August 1**, **September 1** and **October 1 2026** — roughly 87M KITE each and the firm supply events in the window. Watch the Foundation's ecosystem-pool deployment, since post-mainnet reward emissions are the main reason realized float is growing faster than the schedule. Watch whether the designed commission buyback ever prints an executed on-chain amount, which would put the first real number on the buy side. And mark **November 3 2026**: the one-year cliff for the team and investor allocations opens then, just past this window but the single largest forward supply event.
