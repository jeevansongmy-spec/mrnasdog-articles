## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window beginning July 13 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **ENA at about +6.7% net** — about **637.5M ENA** of dated cliff vesting reaching the float, against nothing on the buy side. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+9.15%**, versus the framework's **+6.67%** read for the same window — a gap of about **2.5 percentage points** that **does raise a monitor-gap chip**. The published cliff schedule explains about **637.5M** of the roughly **800M** the float actually grew; the remaining **~160M** is Ethena's ecosystem and airdrop pool distributing ENA on no fixed calendar, which the monitor's data source counts as circulating. We book only the dated schedule rather than the monitor figure, so the primary read stays. ENA is **structurally inflationary on the active float**, a capped token working through the heaviest stretch of its multi-year unlock.

## Sell pressure: where new ENA comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**. ENA is fixed at **15B**, every token was minted at the token generation event, and there is no mint function and no staking emission, so the protocol issues nothing new. Every ENA that will ever exist already exists; the only question is how fast the locked **~5.44B** reaches the market.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is about **637.5M ENA** over the next 90 days, and it is the entire story on the sell side. The unlock runs on two recurring monthly cliff tranches: about **40.6M ENA** to the Ethena foundation on the 2nd of each month, a schedule that has run since February 2025, and about **171.9M ENA** to core contributors and investors on the 5th — the post-cliff linear vest, which works out to 75% of the combined 55% core-and-investor allocation over 36 months, or 171.9M a month. That is roughly **212.5M ENA** a month, and three cycles fall inside the window across the August, September and October dates. The vesting continues into 2028, so this is a steady, heavy overhang rather than a one-off cliff.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is booked at **zero** in the ledger, but it is the reason the monitor reads more growth than the calendar. The ecosystem development pool, about **28%** of supply or **~4.2B ENA**, hands out airdrop and reward tokens on no fixed calendar; that flow is real, but because it has no dated schedule to project forward, the framework records it at zero and watches it as an overhang. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is **zero**, because no bankruptcy estate or court-ordered distribution applies to ENA.

## Buy pressure: where new ENA goes

Every buy row is **zero**, and that is the defining feature of ENA's inflation profile right now. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is zero on a live basis: Ethena ran two large one-time buybacks in 2025, about **$360M** in July and **$530M** in September, but those treasury programmes are complete and sit outside the window. A recurring fee-switch buyback that would route protocol revenue to staked-ENA (sENA) holders is approved in principle, but it is not confirmed executing on-chain with a documented quantum, so the framework carries it at zero until a real flow appears.

Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero: ENA has no fee-burn mechanism, and none of the protocol's USDe revenue destroys tokens. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is zero, with no treasury or foundation open-market buying of ENA observed in the window. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is zero: staked ENA exists and earns a share of protocol economics, but sENA is liquid and unlocks on request rather than escrowing supply for years, and no new multi-year lock was announced. With no burn, no live buyback and no new lock, there is nothing structural to absorb the vesting, so it flows straight through to net supply growth. The fee switch is the one mechanism that could change this — if it turns on and directs revenue into open-market ENA buybacks, it would become the first real force pushing against ENA's supply growth.

## Foundation and overhang

Roughly **5.44B ENA** — about **36%** of the 15B supply — is still locked, and almost all of it is team-, investor- or foundation-controlled. The largest buckets are the **core contributors** at 30% of supply and **investors** at 25%, which together drive the 171.9M monthly tranche; the **foundation** at 15%, source of the 40.6M monthly tranche; and the **ecosystem development** pool at about 28%, which distributes airdrop and reward ENA off-calendar and is the reason the monitor reads faster growth than the schedule. These are the overhangs the framework watches on a roughly bi-weekly walk. If any of these balances falls between refreshes beyond the dated cliff schedule, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How ENA compares to other capped governance tokens

ENA belongs to the class of **capped governance tokens whose supply growth is a distribution schedule, not an emission rate**. Where a continuous-emission Layer 1 mints new coins forever, ENA can only release what already exists inside the 15B cap; the inflation is finite, but for now it is running fast because the core-contributor, investor and foundation tranches are all still vesting at once. In that respect it resembles the early unlock years of tokens like ARB or OP — a large team-and-investor share released over several years while circulating supply climbs long after launch.

The sharper contrast is with a capped token that runs a **live buyback or burn**. An exchange token with a quarterly burn, or a DeFi token that has finished vesting and uses revenue to buy itself back, reads roughly flat to deflationary because there is a structural buyer. ENA is at the opposite point of the cycle: its vesting is still live and heavy, and its fee-switch buyback — the mechanism that would give it that structural buyer — is approved but not yet running. Ethena earns real revenue from USDe, tens of millions a month, so the raw material for a buyback exists; the question is purely whether governance turns that revenue into open-market ENA purchases. Until it does, ENA stays structurally inflationary while its capped peers with live buybacks stay roughly steady — same category, opposite ends of the supply-pressure spectrum.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the fee switch, the single biggest swing factor: a governance decision to activate it and route USDe revenue into open-market ENA buybacks would be the first real force offsetting the unlocks. Watch the two monthly cliff dates — about 40.6M ENA to the foundation on the 2nd and 171.9M to core contributors and investors on the 5th of August, September and October 2026 — which together set the dated supply growth. Watch the ecosystem and airdrop pool, whose off-calendar distributions are the reason realized growth runs above the schedule. Watch whether any large 2025-style one-time buyback is repeated, which would temporarily flip the net. And watch the staked-ENA (sENA) balance, since a durable lock there would remove float that the schedule keeps adding.
