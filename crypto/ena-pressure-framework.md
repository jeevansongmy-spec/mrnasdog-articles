## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window opening June 22 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **ENA at +1.3% net** on the forward view, driven entirely by vesting unlocks against a buy side of **zero**. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+9.35%**, versus the framework's **+4.54%** dated read for the same trailing window — a gap of about **4.81 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The gap is the Ethena Foundation's ecosystem-development and airdrop emissions: roughly **372M ENA** entered the float in the last quarter (on top of dated vesting and the Season 5 airdrop) with no published date or quantum we can carry forward. ENA is **structurally inflationary on a fixed cap** — the supply ceiling is fixed at 15B, but the path to it dilutes holders quarter after quarter.

## Sell pressure: where new ENA comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**. ENA has no on-chain mint, no staking emission and no inflation function; the token is capped at **15B** and the protocol never creates new units. Every supply change is a pre-allocated bucket moving from locked to circulating.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the whole forward story, at about **121.89M ENA** over the next 90 days. Core contributors, investors and the foundation vest on a linear monthly schedule of roughly **40.63M ENA** on the 2nd of each month, and three of those tranches land inside the window: Jul 2 2026, Aug 2 2026 and Sep 2 2026. These are dated, deterministic cliffs, which is why they count in full. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is **zero as a booked flow**, but it is the source of the monitor gap: the Season 5 airdrop of about **300M ENA** opened around May 5 2026 just before the window, and Season 6 runs through about late September 2026 with its pool and distribution date still unannounced. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate applies to ENA.

## Buy pressure: where new ENA goes

Every buy row is **zero**, which is the defining feature of ENA's current structure. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the one to watch: a fee-switch programme to route protocol revenue from USDe into open-market ENA buybacks was approved around **Jun 17 2026**, but it is not yet executing on-chain with a confirmed amount, so the framework carries it at zero until tokens actually move. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero because ENA has no burn mechanism; the token is fixed-supply with no fee-burn function. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is zero, with no disclosed discretionary open-market buying. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is zero as a quantum, though a new rule requires at least 50% of newly claimed airdrop ENA to be locked.

## Foundation and overhang

The team-controlled overhang on ENA is large and structural. About **5.7B ENA** of the 15B cap is still unvested across core contributors, investors, the foundation and ecosystem-development buckets, releasing on multi-year schedules into 2028. The dated portion — core-contributor, investor and foundation linear vesting — is captured in Sell #2 at about 40.63M a month. The undated portion — ecosystem-development incentives, liquidity programmes and airdrop seasons run at the foundation's discretion — is what makes realized float grow faster than the dated schedule, and it is tracked as the Sell #3 overhang on a roughly bi-weekly walk. If that overhang's balance falls between refreshes through a new airdrop claim or incentive release, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How ENA compares to other fixed-cap unlock-heavy tokens

ENA belongs to the class of **fixed-supply governance tokens that are still early in a multi-year vesting schedule** — closer to a recently launched layer-1 or DeFi governance token than to a fully distributed, flat-float coin. Unlike a capped coin that has finished vesting (where supply is genuinely flat), ENA still has the majority of its 15B cap to release, so the cap is a long-run ceiling rather than a near-term constraint. Unlike an uncapped, continuous-emission proof-of-stake chain, ENA mints nothing — its dilution comes entirely from cliffs and linear unlocks, which makes the forward path more predictable but no less dilutive.

The contrast worth drawing is with exchange tokens or DeFi protocols that pair unlocks with an active revenue-funded buyback-and-burn, turning net supply flat or deflationary. ENA has the revenue — USDe generates substantial protocol income — but the fee switch that would convert that revenue into a buyback is approved and not yet live. Until it executes, ENA reads as a token whose supply is purely diluting: positive, low-single-digit net inflation on the dated view, with a much larger realized float increase whenever the foundation releases ecosystem and airdrop tokens.

And expect the framework to keep reading below our supply monitor for as long as undated ecosystem emissions reach the float — that gap is discretionary foundation activity, not a missed schedule.
