Cronos is releasing the **70B CRO** it re-minted in 2025 back into circulation: the Strategic Reserve vests about **3.5B CRO** over 90 days, plus roughly **243M** of staking emission, against no buyback and no burn. The Pressure Framework reads about **+8.1% net**, and our supply monitor reads **+8.90%** — both pointing the same way: supply is rising fast.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 30 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **CRO at +8.1% net** on the forward view, driven almost entirely by the Strategic Reserve vesting that returns the re-minted 70B CRO to circulation. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+8.90%**, versus the framework's **+8.32%** emission read for the same window — a gap of about **0.6 percentage points** that ships a **monitor-gap chip**. The gap is a gross-emission timing wedge, not a missed release: the reserve vesting accounts for the bulk of the growth, and the small extra is staking issuance that ran at the older, higher rate for part of the window before the V7 upgrade pinned it near 1% and set it to decay. CRO is **structurally inflationary** — and unusually so, because a reversed burn is now flowing back as supply.

## Sell pressure: where new CRO comes from

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the whole story, at about **3.5B CRO** over the next 90 days. In 2021 Crypto.com burned 70B CRO; in March 2025 a contested governance vote re-minted that 70B into an escrowed Strategic Reserve, and that reserve now vests on a five-year linear schedule via a Cosmos SDK vesting account, releasing roughly **1.17B CRO** every month. That single flow is by far the largest supply force on the chain, and it is dated, scheduled and on-chain — so it sits cleanly in the vesting row rather than the discretionary one.

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — adds about **243M CRO** over 90 days and is falling. Governance Proposal #33, live with the mainnet V7 upgrade on May 20 2026, pinned base staking emission near **1%** of total supply and layered a **6.8%** monthly compound decay on top, so the mint shrinks every month as Cronos pushes toward its 100B cap; last quarter ran nearer 330M because the older, higher rate still applied for part of the window. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a separate flow, because the reserve release is already counted once in the vesting row. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to CRO.

## Buy pressure: where new CRO goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is carried at **zero**: there is no scheduled, revenue-funded open-market buyback for the CRO token with a published, executed 90-day figure. The framework counts only executed flows, so nothing is booked here. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is also zero: network fees on the EVM chain can be burned, but the amount is small and not publicly quantified, and the 2026 tokenomics change moved the economics toward an emission-decay model rather than a fee-burn one. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero. The result is a buy side that is empty: nothing currently offsets the reserve release.

## Foundation and overhang

CRO's defining overhang is the Strategic Reserve itself: the re-minted 70B CRO held in an escrowed Cosmos SDK vesting account, releasing about 1.17B CRO per month for five years. With circulating near 46B against a 100B cap, well over half of the eventual supply is still locked in this reserve and other long-term allocations — a very large overhang that has years left to vest. Unlike a typical team or seed cliff, this overhang is not a stockpile that might dump unpredictably — it is a fixed, dated, linear release, which is exactly why it sits in the vesting row and drives the headline. The framework tracks the reserve balance on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if the release rate is adjusted by governance, or a discretionary outflow appears outside the scheduled vest, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How CRO compares to other capped-supply exchange-linked chains

CRO belongs to the class of **capped-supply, exchange-linked network tokens** — closer to BNB or a centrally-stewarded L1 than to a pure proof-of-stake chain with open emission. Like BNB it has a hard ceiling (100B CRO) and a custodial sponsor (Crypto.com) that steers tokenomics by governance; unlike BNB, which has spent years burning supply down toward a lower target, CRO is moving the opposite way right now — a reversed 2021 burn is being re-minted and vested back into circulation, so supply is climbing toward the cap rather than away from it.

That makes CRO an unusual case for an inflation lens. A halving-model coin dilutes on a fixed, decelerating curve; an uncapped proof-of-stake chain dilutes continuously with no ceiling; an aggressive exchange token shrinks via buyback-and-burn. CRO is doing none of those cleanly: it is filling a cap it had previously emptied, through a scheduled vesting release, with no buyback or burn currently offsetting the dilution. With the buy side empty, the reserve release is the dominant force and the chain reads firmly inflationary.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the monthly Strategic Reserve unlocks around the 17th — the next tranches land near Jul 17 2026, Aug 17 2026 and Sep 17 2026, each about 1.17B CRO, and they are the single number that sets the net read. Watch for any sign that a revenue-funded buyback or burn becomes a real, executed flow, since that is the only mechanism that could meaningfully brake the dilution; until then the buy side stays empty. Note the ongoing 6.8% monthly decay on staking emission, which quietly lowers the smaller Sell #1 each month. And expect the framework to keep reading just under our supply monitor for as long as the pre-V7 staking rate echoes through the realized number — that gap is timing, not a new release.

## Summary

CRO is a capped-supply, exchange-linked network token that is currently inflating, not deflating: a 70B burn from 2021 was re-minted in 2025 and is now vesting back into circulation at about 1.17B CRO a month. The framework reads about +8.1% net over 90 days — roughly 3.5B of reserve vesting plus ~243M of decaying staking emission, against no buyback and no burn. Our supply monitor reads +8.90% realized, with the small gap explained by the higher pre-V7 staking rate and continuous-vesting timing. The defining feature is the overhang: at ~46B circulating against a 100B ceiling, well over half of the eventual supply is still locked in the reserve, so the release has years to run — and only a real, executed buyback would change the direction.
