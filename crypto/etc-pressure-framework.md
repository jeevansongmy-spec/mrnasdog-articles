## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window opening July 13 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **ETC at +0.62% net** on the forward view, driven entirely by proof-of-work mining emission with nothing on the buy side. The framework's own trailing read is **+0.75%**, and our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+0.27%** — a gap of about **0.48 percentage points**, just inside the 0.5-point tolerance, so no monitor-gap chip ships. The residual is measurement-side: the framework's Sell #1 is the deterministic on-chain block subsidy, counted block by block, while the monitor's circulating-supply figure has under-tracked Ethereum Classic's realized mining issuance over the window. Ethereum Classic is a **quiet, structurally inflationary proof-of-work chain** whose new supply steps down 20% at each five-million-block era.

## Sell pressure: where new ETC comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the entire story, at about **1.18M ETC** mined over the last 90 days and about **0.97M ETC** projected over the next 90. Ethereum Classic is a pure proof-of-work chain: new ETC is created each block and paid directly to miners, with no dev tax and no allocation skimmed off the top. Emission follows **ECIP-1017** — the 5M20 schedule — which cuts the block reward 20% every 5,000,000 blocks. ETC currently sits in Era 5, paying a static **2.048 ETC** per block at roughly 6,400 blocks a day. Era 6 arrives at block 25,000,000, around **Jul 22 2026**, dropping the reward to **1.6384 ETC** — which is exactly why the forward 90-day mint sits below the trailing one.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero**: Ethereum Classic has no premine, no ICO tranche and no team vesting. It inherited the genesis chain, and that supply fully circulates, so no locked allocation can reach the market. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also **zero**, because there is no foundation or insider ETC treasury to release; development is funded by community donations, not a pre-allocated stash. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is **zero**, with no bankruptcy estate or court-ordered distribution attached to ETC. Every new ETC in the window is mined.

## Buy pressure: where new ETC goes

The buy ledger is **empty**. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: Ethereum Classic has no protocol buyback and no treasury or revenue routed to buy ETC back from the market. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is **zero**: ETC never adopted EIP-1559, so transaction fees are paid to miners rather than burned, and nothing is removed from supply there. The proposed **Olympia upgrade** would add an EIP-1559 base-fee burn late in 2026, but it is not live in this window, so the framework does not count it yet. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is **zero**, with no foundation or treasury to buy ETC on the open market. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is **zero**: Ethereum Classic is proof-of-work with no staking or lockups. With nothing on the buy side, net inflation equals gross mining emission.

## Foundation and overhang

Ethereum Classic has no overhang in the usual sense — no foundation wallet, no investor cliff and no unlock calendar — because the chain was never funded by a token sale. The ETC Cooperative and other contributors fund development through donations rather than holding a pre-allocated ETC reserve, so there is no identified balance the framework needs to watch for a sudden release. The only source of new ETC is the block reward itself, already captured in Sell #1. If a large identified treasury ever began distributing ETC — which the proof-of-work design does not create — that outflow would enter Sell #3 at the next refresh; today there is nothing to track.

## How ETC compares to other proof-of-work chains

ETC belongs to the class of **hard-capped proof-of-work coins** — the same monetary family as Bitcoin and Litecoin, where a fixed cap (about **210.7M ETC**) and a reward-halving schedule define supply. The difference is the step size. Bitcoin halves its reward every four years in a single sharp cut; Ethereum Classic's ECIP-1017 trims the reward 20% every 5,000,000 blocks, so the reductions are smaller and more frequent — a milder version of the same disinflation. Because ETC blocks come roughly every 13.5 seconds, an era lasts a little under two years, and the next cut is already days away rather than years.

Against uncapped proof-of-stake L1s, which mint perpetually to reward stakers, ETC is structurally tighter: issuance is bounded by the cap and steps down on schedule. But unlike exchange tokens that burn or buy back to go net-deflationary, Ethereum Classic has no offsetting mechanism today — the inflation lens is simply the mining curve, with no burn to read against it. That leaves ETC mildly inflationary for now. The one thing that could change the shape is the proposed Olympia upgrade's EIP-1559 burn; until it activates, ETC stays on the pure-emission profile.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the Era 6 block-reward cut, expected around **Jul 22 2026** at block 25,000,000, which drops the reward from 2.048 to 1.6384 ETC and quietly lowers forward issuance the moment it lands. Watch the **Olympia upgrade**: if its EIP-1559 base-fee burn and on-chain DAO treasury ship on schedule late in 2026, ETC would gain its first real buy-side mechanism and the framework read would shift toward neutral or deflationary. Watch realized block times — faster blocks pull the era boundary earlier and raise issuance slightly; slower blocks do the reverse. And expect the framework and our supply monitor to converge as the circulating figure catches up with realized mining.
