## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 30 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **MON at +4.2% net**, driven entirely by staking emission with no buy-side offset. Our supply monitor reads the realised last-90-day circulating change at **+9.35%**, versus the framework's **+4.17%** staking-emission read — a gap of about **5.2 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The gap is structural: most of the extra float is the Foundation's large ecosystem pool, unlocked at the November 2025 launch but only slowly deployed as grants, incentives and validator delegations. Monad is a **young, structurally inflationary chain** — new coins are minted and distributed faster than anything removes them.

## Sell pressure: where new MON comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the firm number, at about **493M MON** over the next 90 days. Monad mints **25 MON** every block to reward stakers, which annualises to roughly **2B MON**, or about **2%** of the 100B initial supply per year. Monad has no supply cap, so this issuance runs continuously; on-chain total supply has already passed 100B, confirming the mint is real new supply rather than a reclassification.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero** in this window: the team, investor and treasury allocations are all locked for the first year after launch, and the first cliff lands **Nov 24 2026**, after this window closes. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is carried at zero as a quantified flow but is the live overhang: a large ecosystem-development pool was unlocked at launch and enters the float as the Foundation spends it, with no per-quarter figure published. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where new MON goes

There is effectively no buy pressure. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: Monad runs no buyback programme. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is carried at zero: Monad burns the base component of every transaction fee, which can offset inflation as the network scales, but at current activity the amount is small and not publicly quantified. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero. With nothing on the buy side, all of the mint reaches the float net.

## Foundation and overhang

The overhang on MON is unusually large for a chain this young. The biggest piece is the **38.5B** ecosystem-development allocation — technically unlocked at launch, but with less than 2% committed so far; the rest funds grants, validator delegations and incentives over many years, entering the float gradually as deployed. On top of that sit the locked allocations: roughly **27%** to the team and **19.7%** to investors, both held for the first year then released over three to four years starting **Nov 24 2026**. The framework books no scheduled release in this window; if the Foundation's ecosystem balance falls faster than tracked between refreshes, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How MON compares to other uncapped Layer-1 chains

MON belongs to the class of **uncapped, continuous-emission Layer-1s** — closer to a young proof-of-stake chain paying staking rewards than to a hard-capped, halving-model coin. Unlike a fixed-supply token, MON has no ceiling; unlike a mature chain with a working fee burn, its base-fee burn is still too small to offset issuance. The 2%-a-year staking emission is modest by L1 standards, but it is gross issuance with no buyback brake, so on an inflation lens MON reads as steadily inflationary at the protocol level.

The sharper contrast is timing. A chain like Monad carries most of its dilution not in the daily mint but in the unlock schedule — the team, investor and treasury cliffs that begin in late 2026 and run for years. That makes MON's near-term inflation low but its forward overhang heavy, the opposite of a mature capped coin whose future supply is essentially fixed.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the Foundation's ecosystem-pool deployment — grants, incentives and validator delegations are the main reason realised float is growing faster than the mint. Watch whether the base-fee burn scales with network activity, since a busy chain could start to offset issuance meaningfully. Note the staking emission rate, holding near 2% a year unless governance changes it. And mark **Nov 24 2026**: the first major unlock cliff for team, investor and treasury tokens begins then — well after this window, but the single largest forward supply event.
