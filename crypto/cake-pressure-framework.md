PancakeSwap's CAKE still mints new coins — about **2.0M CAKE** to its liquidity pools over 90 days — but a revenue-funded buyback-and-burn destroys roughly **7.2M CAKE** in the same window, about **3.5x** more. The MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads CAKE at about **-1.61%** net over 90 days, in line with our supply monitor's **-1.66%** — a structurally deflationary exchange token under a hard **400M** cap.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window opening July 13 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **CAKE at -1.61% net**. PancakeSwap mints about **2.0M CAKE** to liquidity pools under its reduced Tokenomics 3.0 schedule, while its weekly buyback-and-burn removes about **7.2M CAKE** — so supply is falling, not rising. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **-1.66%** (a drop from about **328.4M** to **323.0M**), so the gap is only **0.05 percentage points** and **no monitor-gap chip** is needed. CAKE is best characterised as **deflationary by structural buyback** — an exchange token that spends its own trading revenue to shrink its float faster than emission can grow it.

## Sell pressure: where new CAKE comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is about **2.0M CAKE** over 90 days. PancakeSwap still mints CAKE as farming and liquidity rewards, but the **Tokenomics 3.0** overhaul of April 2025 retired the veCAKE lock model and cut daily emission from roughly **40,000** to about **22,250 CAKE** a day. That reduced mint is the only source of new CAKE, and it is deliberately small relative to the burn. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero**: CAKE was fair-launched in 2020 with no premine, no venture allocation and no vesting schedule, so there are no cliffs and nothing unlocks into the market on a calendar.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also **zero** in flow terms. The PancakeSwap team holds CAKE in a treasury multisig, but it sits on no published release schedule and no discretionary distribution was observed in the window, so it is tracked as an overhang rather than booked as supply. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is **zero**: no bankruptcy estate applies to CAKE, and the veCAKE lock that once held a large tranche off-market was retired in Tokenomics 3.0, so there is no locked balance unwinding into circulation.

## Buy pressure: where new CAKE goes

The buy side is where CAKE's deflation is manufactured. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is about **7.2M CAKE** over 90 days. PancakeSwap directs trading fees and product revenue (from its AMM, perpetuals, prediction and IFO products) into buying CAKE on the open market and sending it to a dead address; recent weeks removed between **405K** and **747K CAKE** each, and June 2026 marked the **34th** straight month in which more CAKE left circulation than entered it. Because the bought-back CAKE is destroyed rather than accumulated, this whole flow is a permanent burn — there is no treasury wallet building up on the sell side. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — reads **zero** only because there is no separate direct fee burn; all of PancakeSwap's burning runs through the Buy #1 buyback. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both **zero**, with no discretionary open-market buying beyond the revenue burn and no new lock mechanism after veCAKE was retired.

## Foundation and overhang

CAKE has a light overhang compared with most tokens. There is no investor or team vesting schedule to track, because none ever existed. The one team-controlled balance is the **PancakeSwap treasury multisig**, which holds CAKE for operations and grants but is on no fixed release calendar and showed no distribution into the market during the window. The framework tracks that balance on a roughly bi-weekly walk alongside the on-chain **burn address**, whose balance only ever grows as more CAKE is destroyed. If the treasury balance falls faster than its recent pace between refreshes, the extra outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh — but as of this build, the dominant on-chain flow by far is destruction, not release.

## How CAKE compares to other exchange-token burners

CAKE belongs to the class of **exchange tokens with a revenue-funded buyback-and-burn**, the same structural family as large centralized-exchange tokens that burn a slice of quarterly profits. The difference is that CAKE's burn is continuous and revenue-linked week by week rather than a discrete quarterly event, and it burns against a live emission — most burner tokens have no ongoing mint at all. Compared with an uncapped layer-1 that issues staking rewards forever, CAKE's **400M** hard cap and net-negative supply put it at the opposite end of the spectrum: issuance exists but is overwhelmed by destruction.

The contrast that matters is **burn versus mint**. On a token like CAKE the inflation question is not how many coins are created but whether trading revenue is large enough to keep buying and burning more than the pools emit. For 34 consecutive months it has been, which is why the framework reads CAKE as **structurally deflationary** — not because supply is capped, but because the protocol actively removes coins. That also makes the reading revenue-sensitive: a deep, sustained drop in PancakeSwap volume would shrink the burn and could narrow, though not obviously reverse, the net-negative print.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the weekly CAKE burn reports — the single most important number, since the burn is what drives the deflation; a sustained slide below roughly **400K** CAKE a week would materially cut the net-negative read. Watch PancakeSwap trading volume and product revenue, because the burn is funded directly from it. Watch the governance forum for any further change to the emission schedule or the **400M** cap, following the January 2026 vote that cut the cap from 450M. And track the on-chain burn address balance directly: it is the cleanest confirmation that destruction is still outpacing the roughly **2.0M**-per-90-day mint.

## Summary

CAKE is a decentralized-exchange token that still mints new coins but destroys far more of them. Over the next 90 days PancakeSwap emits about 2.0M CAKE to its liquidity pools while buying back and burning roughly 7.2M from trading revenue, leaving the framework at about -1.61% net — in line with our supply monitor's -1.66%, so no gap chip is needed. The key variable is revenue: the burn is deflationary only as long as trading volume funds it. As things stand, CAKE has shrunk its supply for 34 straight months against a hard 400M ceiling, making it one of the clearer examples of a structurally deflationary token in the framework.
