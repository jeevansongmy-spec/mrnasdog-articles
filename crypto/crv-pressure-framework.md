## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **CRV at about +1.8% net** on the forward view, driven entirely by gauge emissions with nothing on the buy side to offset them. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+2.5%**, versus the framework's gauge-emission read of about **+1.9%** for the same window — a gap of roughly **0.6 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The residual is the DAO community fund's slow grant vesting: roughly 24M CRV still to release on multi-year contracts, with no published 90-day rate, so it is tracked, not booked. Both the emissions and the grant overhang ease after the August 12 2026 emission cut. CRV reads as **predictable, slowly decelerating inflation.**

## Sell pressure: where new CRV comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the entire flow, at about **27M CRV** over the next 90 days. Curve mints CRV every block as liquidity-provider rewards on a fixed, permissionless schedule that decays roughly **15.9%** a year; the rate steps down again on the August 12 2026 anniversary. This is a hardcoded gauge emission, not a discretionary release — the schedule runs on its own, the same for everyone.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero**: team, investor and employee vesting fully completed in August 2024, so no scheduled cliff or linear founder unlock remains. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a flow, but the DAO community fund is a tracked overhang: roughly **24M CRV** still to release on multi-year grant contracts, with no published 90-day rate, so it is monitored rather than booked. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because the founder's past liquidations were personal leveraged positions, not a scheduled protocol or treasury sale.

## Buy pressure: where new CRV goes

Every buy-side row is **zero**. The crux is the fee design: Curve's **50%** trading-fee share pays veCRV lockers in a **stablecoin** (crvUSD), not by buying or burning CRV — so there is no buy-side offset at all. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is zero, since fee value goes to lockers, not open-market repurchase. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero, because that fee share is paid out as a stablecoin, not used to destroy CRV. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is zero, with no treasury purchase in the window. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is zero as a booked figure: locking CRV into veCRV removes it from float, but it is voluntary and continuous with no announced quantum, so the framework does not book it.

## Foundation and overhang

CRV has one live overhang and two structures worth naming. The **DAO Community Fund** holds roughly 24M CRV unallocated, released on linear grant contracts of a year or longer — this is the source of the monitor gap, but it carries no published 90-day quantum, so it stays tracked rather than booked. The **DAO Treasury**, established June 27 2025, accrues 10% of DAO revenue and is a holding structure, not a CRV-issuing one. The founder's personal holdings are not a protocol pool — past liquidations were leveraged personal positions, so they are excluded from the quantum. The framework books no discretionary release beyond gauge emissions; if a tracked balance falls between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How CRV compares to other DeFi emission tokens

CRV belongs to the class of **vote-escrow / ve(3,3)-style DeFi tokens** with scheduled, decaying emissions and no buyback. The contrast worth drawing is with fee-burn or buyback-and-burn tokens that turn protocol revenue into deflation: those pair issuance with an active sink, so their net supply can shrink. CRV does not. It directs its fee value to lockers as a stablecoin yield, which is good for veCRV holders but leaves the emission side without an offset.

This is a mechanism-based read, not a price-based one. On an inflation lens specifically, CRV reads as steadily, mildly inflationary: the gauge schedule is the dominant force, it decays year over year, and there is no buy-side brake — so dilution slows on its own schedule rather than being reversed by a burn.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the August 12 2026 annual emission cut — the gauge rate steps down about **15.9%**, from roughly 115M to about 97M CRV a year, which lowers the mint for every window after it. Watch community-fund grant disbursements, since those are the source of the monitor gap and the one tracked balance that can shift the realized number. And watch governance: any proposal that adds a CRV buyback or burn would introduce the missing offset and change the net read materially. For now there is no offset, so the emission schedule decides the pace.
