World Liberty Financial is a **fixed 100B-cap** Ethereum token with no minting. After a large May 2026 unlock, the April 2026 governance vote pushed every remaining team and early-supporter token behind a **2-year cliff**, so the framework sees **zero new vesting** over the next 90 days, while a fee-funded buyback burns about **40M WLFI**. Net reads about **−0.1%** — supply edging down. The last-90-day jump our supply monitor shows (**+14.89%**) was that earlier unlock, now done.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **WLFI at about −0.1% net** on the forward view: no new unlocks, and a small live buyback-burn. That is a sharp turn from the recent past — over the last 90 days our supply monitor recorded **+14.89%** growth as early supporters claimed tokens, against the framework's **+12.96%** read for the same window. The roughly **1.9 percentage point** gap ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**, but it is fully reconciled: both numbers describe the identical ~4,119M-token unlock — the difference is only which supply base the percentage divides by. WLFI is now **structurally flat-to-shrinking on the active float** until its cliff releases around May 2028.

## Sell pressure: where new WLFI comes from

For the next 90 days, every sell row is **zero**. Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is structurally zero: WLFI has a fixed 100B maximum supply and no minting, so the protocol never creates new coins. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is also zero, and this is the heart of the story. The April 2026 governance proposal, which passed with about 99.5% support, restructured roughly **62B locked tokens**: the remaining ~17B early-supporter allocation and the ~40.7B founder, team and partner allocation were placed behind a **2-year cliff** that does not begin releasing until around May 2028.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a flow: team and early-supporter buckets are locked under the same cliff, and the proposal's mandatory **10% insider burn** (about 4.5B WLFI) removes supply rather than adding it. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero. The large supply increase visible over the prior 90 days was the May 2026 second-phase claim activation, when early supporters signed Lockbox agreements and unlocked tokens before the new cliff took effect — a one-time event, not an ongoing flow.

## Buy pressure: where new WLFI goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the only active force on the supply, at about **40M WLFI** over 90 days. A governance-approved program routes **100%** of the treasury's liquidity-pool fees into buying WLFI on the open market and burning it; observed burns have run in the low millions of tokens per event across multiple chains through 2026. Because the destination is a burn address, those coins are destroyed for good. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is carried at zero, because the only burn IS this buyback; counting it again would double-count. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero: there is no discretionary open-market buying outside the fee program, and the 2-year cliff was already in force before this window opened.

## Foundation and overhang

WLFI's overhang is large but, for now, contractually frozen. The team, founder, advisor and partner allocation — about **40.7B WLFI** after the insider burn — sits in a Lockbox smart contract behind the 2-year cliff, with a 3-year linear tail running to roughly 2031. The early-supporter remaining tranche — about **17B WLFI** — is locked the same way, on a 2-year cliff then 2-year linear release. Neither can move in this window; both are tracked overhangs that the framework re-checks on a roughly bi-weekly walk. If any of these balances begins to fall before the scheduled cliff date — for example through an amended governance vote — the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How WLFI compares to other fixed-cap unlock-driven tokens

WLFI belongs to the class of **fixed-cap governance tokens whose supply is driven entirely by vesting unlocks**, not by emission. Unlike an uncapped proof-of-stake chain that mints new coins every block, WLFI can never exceed 100B and creates nothing — its inflation is purely the pace at which locked allocations convert to float. That makes the unlock calendar, not a block reward, the single variable that matters.

Against that class, WLFI is at an unusual point: it has just cleared a heavy unlock and then deliberately re-locked the rest behind a multi-year cliff, so its near-term dilution drops to zero while a fee-funded burn quietly trims the float. The forward read is therefore mildly deflationary — closer to an exchange token running a buyback-burn than to an early-stage token with a steep unlock ahead — with the important caveat that the deferred supply has not disappeared; it has been pushed to 2028 and beyond.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the on-chain buyback-burn pace — the fee program is the only thing moving supply now, so a busier treasury means a deeper burn. Watch for any new governance proposal that amends the unlock schedule; WLFI has already changed its vesting once in 2026, and another vote could pull the cliff forward. Track confirmation that the ~4.5B insider burn from the April proposal fully executes. And note the cliff date itself — around May 2028 — which is when the next real wave of vesting begins; until then, expect the framework to keep reading flat-to-slightly-negative.
