## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **ETHFI at about +12% net** on the forward view — strongly inflationary, driven entirely by vesting unlocks. ETHFI is fully minted, so there is no protocol inflation; supply grows only as locked team, treasury and community tokens release on a fixed monthly schedule. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+11.75%**, versus the framework's **+13.89%** read for the same window — a gap of about **2.1 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The gap is a window-edge effect: one ~45M monthly cliff lands right on the boundary, counted by the framework but only partly by the monitor. ETHFI is a **heavily unlock-driven token** for as long as the schedule runs into 2027.

## Sell pressure: where new ETHFI comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**, and structurally so. ETHFI was fully minted at its March 2024 launch and is capped at 1 billion forever; the protocol issues nothing new, so the only way supply reaches the market is vesting. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the whole story, at roughly **120M ETHFI** over the next 90 days. The schedule releases about **45M ETHFI a month** in discrete cliffs around the 17th–18th, and three of them fall inside this window. The cap is what holds the figure to ~120M rather than ~135M: only about **120M ETHFI remains locked**, so the schedule cannot release more than what is left.

The unlocking cohorts are the core team (a three-year vest running to March 2027), the DAO treasury, the community airdrop pool, and partnerships and liquidity — all releasing linearly after their one-year cliff cleared in March 2025. The early-investor allocation, the largest single slice, finished its two-year vest in March 2026, just before this window. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a separate flow, because the treasury's share is already inside the monthly cliff. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where ETHFI goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the only active offset, at roughly **12M ETHFI** over 90 days. The ether.fi DAO uses protocol revenue to buy ETHFI on the open market on a weekly and monthly rhythm. The important nuance: the bought-back coins are **redistributed to stakers** as rewards, not destroyed. So the buyback soaks up some open-market selling and rewards long-term holders, but it does not shrink the supply the way a burn would — the coins stay in circulation, just in different hands. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero, because ETHFI has no burn at all. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero.

## Foundation and overhang

ETHFI's overhang is its unvested supply, and it is large and scheduled. Of the fixed 1 billion, roughly **120M remains locked** today across the team, treasury, community and partnership allocations, and all of it is set to release through 2027 on the same monthly rhythm seen this year. That makes ETHFI's inflation unusually predictable — it is a calendar, not a discretion. The treasury slice is the one to watch: if the DAO accelerates or redirects treasury tokens beyond the scheduled cliff, that extra flow would enter Sell #3 at the next refresh. For now, everything sits inside the monthly unlock booked in Sell #2.

## How ETHFI compares to other unlock-driven tokens

ETHFI belongs to the class of **fixed-supply governance tokens still deep in their vesting schedule** — the opposite of a continuous-emission chain and also the opposite of a fully-distributed token. There is no mint and no halving; the entire inflation profile is a one-time distribution playing out over four years. That means the pressure is front-loaded and finite: it is heavy now, while monthly cliffs run, and it falls to near zero once the schedule completes in 2027.

The contrast worth drawing is with tokens that pair unlocks with an aggressive burn. ETHFI's buyback is real and revenue-funded, but because it redistributes rather than burns, it cannot turn the token net-deflationary the way a destroy-on-buyback model can. For an inflation lens, that leaves ETHFI reading as strongly inflationary today — the monthly unlock is roughly ten times the size of the buyback's open-market absorption, so the schedule dominates.
