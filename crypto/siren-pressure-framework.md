## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **SIREN at 0.00% net** on both the realized and forward views. There is no block reward, no vesting cliff and no recurring buyback or burn operating inside the window, so every one of the eight ledger rows is zero. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at about **−0.9%**, a gap of roughly **0.9 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. A deep walk found no burn or unlock transaction in the window; the large supply burn that defined SIREN's 2026 story happened **before** this period, so the small negative reading is the tracked float settling, not a live mechanism. SIREN is **structurally flat on supply** — a fixed token whose dilution risk is concentration, not issuance.

## Sell pressure: where new SIREN comes from

Nowhere — that is the headline. Sell #1, protocol inflation, is **zero**: SIREN is a fixed-supply token on BNB Chain with no block reward and no active mint, so no new coins are created. Sell #2, vesting unlocks, is also zero. The token was fair-launched, with no presale, no investor round and no team allocation on a cliff — the entire supply was distributed at launch and is already circulating, so there is no schedule of unlocks waiting to hit the market.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a flow. We found no public evidence of a discretionary treasury or foundation release in the window; if a project-controlled balance fell, it would enter here at the next walk, but none did. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero. The real overhang on this token is not a schedule; it is the documented concentration of supply in a small number of large-holder addresses, which is a market-structure risk rather than an inflation flow.

## Buy pressure: where SIREN goes

Also nowhere active in this window. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is zero: there is no published, recurring buyback-and-burn program funded by protocol revenue. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero, with no on-chain fee burn removing supply on a recurring basis. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero. The single large burn that shaped SIREN this year — roughly a quarter of the original mint, retired in a one-time donation-wallet burn early in 2026 — sits outside this 90-day window, so it does not count as a flow here even though it is the most important supply event in the token's history.

## Foundation and overhang

SIREN has no classic unlock overhang, because there was never a structured vesting schedule to overhang the price. What it has instead is concentration: on-chain analysis through 2026 flagged a large share of the circulating supply sitting in a small cluster of related addresses. That is not issuance and it does not show up as protocol inflation, but it is the dominant supply risk — a single large holder choosing to sell moves the float far more than any mint ever could. The framework books no discretionary release because none is scheduled or disclosed; if a known balance starts falling between walks, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh. For now, the honest read is flat supply with a heavy, unscheduled concentration risk that the inflation lens alone does not price.

## How SIREN compares to other fixed-supply tokens

SIREN belongs to the class of **fixed-supply, fully-circulating tokens** — closer to a fair-launched memecoin than to a scheduled-emission protocol coin. Unlike an inflationary proof-of-stake chain, it has no block reward diluting holders every day; unlike a heavily-vested venture token, it has no multi-year cliff waiting to unlock. On the inflation axis alone, that makes it cleaner than most: there is simply nothing structurally adding supply.

The contrast worth drawing is with tokens that run an active buyback-burn to go net-deflationary. SIREN does not — its supply contracted only once, in a one-time burn, and there is no recurring program pulling coins off the market month after month. So while the inflation reading is a clean flat, it should not be mistaken for a deflationary engine. The supply is fixed and quiet; the volatility comes from who holds it, not from how it is issued.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the large-holder addresses — concentration, not issuance, is the thing that moves SIREN, so a big wallet starting to distribute would matter far more than any mint. Watch for any new, recurring buyback-burn or fee-burn the project might actually ship, since that would be the first mechanism to turn the flat read negative. Watch for a one-time burn repeat, which would land as a buy-side event the moment it executes on-chain. And expect the framework to keep reading 0.00% for as long as the supply stays fixed and no scheduled flow appears — with the monitor gap closing as the tracked float finishes settling from the earlier burn.
