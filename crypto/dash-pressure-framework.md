Dash is a fair-launch proof-of-work masternode coin with **no premine** and **no vesting**; new supply comes only from mining, about **0.1M DASH** over 90 days, with no buyback or burn. The Pressure Framework reads about **+0.8% net**, in line with our supply monitor's **+0.77%**.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **DASH at about +0.8% net** on the forward view, driven entirely by decaying mining issuance with nothing on the buy side to offset it. Our supply monitor reads the realized change at **+0.77%**, a gap of only about **0.03 percentage points** — well inside tolerance, so no ⚠ monitor-gap flag ships. The protocol's fixed block-reward emission matches realized supply growth almost exactly, because there are no unlocks, treasury releases or discretionary flows to create a wedge. DASH reads as **clean, predictable, low sell-side pressure**.

## Sell pressure: where new DASH comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the only flow, at about **0.1M DASH** over the next 90 days. Dash produces roughly **576 blocks a day**, each currently paying about **1.99 DASH**, and that per-block reward steps down by about **7%** around August 15 2026 as the coin grinds toward its **18.9M** cap. The development treasury and the masternode payout are slices of that same block reward — they are not a separate mint — so they count once, here.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero**: Dash launched fair, with no premine and no team or investor allocation, so nothing unlocks on a schedule. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also zero, because there is no foundation token allocation at all; the treasury is funded out of the block reward, not from a pre-allocated stash. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where new DASH goes

Every buy-side row is **zero**. There is no programmatic buyback, no systematic fee burn — transaction and mixing fees are negligible and not destroyed in any meaningful amount — and no treasury or foundation open-market buying. The one structural lock is the masternode collateral: each masternode bonds **1,000 DASH**, which holds supply off the active float. But that lock is voluntary and continuous, and the active masternode count showed no measurable change in the window, so no new lock is booked on the buy side.

## Foundation and overhang

DASH has no token-allocation overhang to worry about. The reward-funded treasury holds no pre-allocated stockpile — it receives a slice of each block's reward and spends it through the governance proposal cycle, so there is no stash sitting ready to dump. The masternode collateral pool, roughly **3.8M DASH** bonded across active nodes, is a structural voluntary lock, not an overhang waiting to be sold. The remaining **~6.16M DASH** still to be mined to the cap is released only as decaying block rewards, which are already captured in Sell #1. If a treasury balance were to fall faster than the schedule, the outflow would enter Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How DASH compares to other proof-of-work coins with a hard cap

DASH belongs to the class of **capped proof-of-work coins with a halving-style decay**. The closest comparison is Bitcoin: both have a fixed maximum supply, both issue new coins only through mining, and neither runs a buyback or a burn. The difference is in the shape of the decay. Bitcoin cuts its reward in half every four years in one sharp step; Dash instead reduces its block reward by about **7.14% a year** in a smoother, more frequent grind. Either way, the result is the same kind of dilution: slow, mechanical and fully predictable from the emission schedule alone.

That puts DASH at the low-pressure end of the inflation spectrum. With issuance well under **1% a quarter** and falling, and with no unlock cliffs or discretionary treasury releases to surprise the market, the only variable is the mining schedule itself — and that schedule is public and decaying.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the block-reward reduction expected around **August 15 2026**, which lowers the per-block mint by about 7% and slows issuance further. Watch the masternode count: more active nodes means more collateral bonded off the float, which tightens effective supply, while a falling count would loosen it. And watch the Dash treasury proposal cycle, since that is where the reward-funded development budget is spent — a sharp draw-down there would be the only path to a new Sell #3 entry.

## Summary

DASH is a fair-launch proof-of-work masternode coin whose new supply comes purely from mining. The chain mints about 0.1M DASH over 90 days — well under 1% a quarter — with no premine, no vesting, no foundation overhang and nothing on the buy side. A built-in reward cut around August 2026 slows issuance further. The framework reads about +0.8% net, and our supply monitor agrees at +0.77%, leaving DASH a clean, predictable, low-pressure inflation read.
