# LAB Inflation Analysis · July 2026 · Supply growing, projected to keep growing

LAB is a young trading-terminal token whose float quadrupled last quarter — about **236M LAB** vested in — and roughly **309M more** releases over the next 90 days, dominated by the **Aug 14 2026** cliff that unlocks a **~282M** locked investor and team cohort, some **28%** of total supply. A buyback-and-burn is active but small — about **22.6M LAB** for **~$3.4M** since June — so the buy side is carried at zero. The framework reads about **+99% net** forward: sharply inflationary as the float roughly doubles again.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

On the forward view, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **LAB at about +99% net** over the next 90 days, driven entirely by vesting that releases new LAB far faster than anything absorbs it. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at about **+309%** — the float grew from roughly 76M to 313M LAB — because the monitor divides by the supply from 90 days ago, when LAB was a brand-new launch. This page divides by today's larger float, so the same vesting reads as about **+76%** for the trailing window; that denominator difference — a gap of roughly **234 percentage points** — ships a **monitor-gap flag**, not a data conflict. Notably, the monitor shows supply **flat over the last 30 days**: the heavy launch ramp is past, and the next wave is scheduled rather than trickling. LAB is **sharply inflationary on a young, insider-heavy supply**, with most of the 1B hard cap still locked.

## Sell pressure: where new LAB comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**: LAB is a trading-terminal token, not a base chain, so there is no block reward and no protocol mint. Every new coin comes from the vesting schedule, which is the entire story here. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is about **27M LAB** over the next 90 days, led by a **26.97M** linear tranche across July with continuing ecosystem and liquidity streams behind it. That is a fraction of last quarter's roughly **236M**, because the heavily front-loaded early ramp that quadrupled the float is now past — the monitor shows the LAB float essentially flat over the last 30 days.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is the dominant force at about **282M LAB**, booked as a dated event: **Aug 14 2026** unlocks the large locked cohort of investor, team and reserved tokens — roughly **28%** of all LAB. Multiple unlock trackers put the full 282M on that date, and disclosed insider deals split some tranches across **Aug 14** and **Sep 15 2026**, both inside this window, so the framework books the full quantum as an in-window cliff. On-chain analysts have flagged that insiders control the vast majority of the current float, concentrating the sell decision in few hands. A further **~508M LAB** sits locked with no published schedule — a monitored overhang, not yet booked. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero: the locked supply is a normal vesting overhang, not an insolvency estate or court-ordered distribution.

## Buy pressure: where new LAB goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is carried at **zero** even though a buyback-and-burn is now confirmed active, funded by part of the platform's 0.5% trading fee and designed to repurchase and burn LAB from the open market. The project's dashboard shows about **22.6M LAB** bought back for roughly **$3.4M** since it launched around **Jun 1 2026**. That flow is small against a 309M vesting wave and its forward pace is not reliably published, so the framework carries it at zero rather than estimate a figure it cannot project — this is monitored and will be booked once a confirmed, sustained on-chain rate exists. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero, because there is no separate automatic base-fee burn on the token; fee revenue feeds the buyback above, not a continuous burn. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero, with no discretionary open-market buying and no fresh multi-year lock announced. If anything, locked supply is starting to release, not extend.

## Foundation and overhang

LAB's overhang is large and mostly undistributed: of the 1B hard cap, only about **312.54M** is circulating, leaving roughly **687M** locked across investors, team and advisors, marketing and the unreleased balance. The headline overhang is the **282M locked cohort** whose cliff falls on **Aug 14 2026**; behind it sits about **508M LAB** of "to-be-determined" locked supply with no published release schedule. On-chain analysts have flagged that a very high share of the current float sits with insiders — one investigator estimated insider control above 90% — which concentrates the sell decision in few hands. The framework books the dated August tranche under Sell #3 and watches the rest as a scheduled or unscheduled overhang; if a locked balance falls faster than its schedule between refreshes, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How LAB compares to other young vesting-driven tokens

LAB belongs to the class of **young, low-float application tokens** — products with real trading revenue but most of their supply still locked, where the dominant supply force is the vesting calendar, not protocol issuance. Unlike an uncapped proof-of-stake chain that mints a steady percentage every year, LAB has a fixed 1B cap and zero protocol inflation; its dilution is front-loaded into the unlock schedule and arrives in discrete cliffs. That makes the inflation read lumpy — quiet for weeks, then a single date like **Aug 14 2026** can nearly double the float — the opposite shape to a continuous-emission L1 that dilutes smoothly.

The contrast worth drawing is with mature exchange or fee tokens that buy back and burn aggressively enough to go net-deflationary. LAB has launched a buyback-and-burn and it is genuinely running, but at roughly **$3.4M** of purchases so far it is orders of magnitude too small to offset a 282M cliff. Until the buyback is large, sustained and verifiable, LAB reads as one of the most inflationary young tokens on the board — a working product wrapped around a supply that is still deep in its dilution phase.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the **Aug 14 2026** cliff — the ~282M locked-cohort unlock is the single biggest supply event in the window, and how much actually hits the market depends on whether insiders sell or hold. Watch the **Sep 15 2026** follow-on tranche disclosed in insider OTC deals, also inside the window. Watch the July **26.97M** linear vesting finish by **Aug 1 2026**. Watch for a sustained on-chain buyback rate — a verified, repeatable burn amount is the only thing that would put a real number on the buy side. And watch the circulating-supply trend itself — if unlocked insiders route tokens through OTC rather than the open market, realized float growth could lag the scheduled unlock.

## Summary

LAB is a young, capped trading-terminal token whose supply is still flooding in from vesting: about 236M LAB entered the float last quarter, roughly 309M more releases over the next 90 days, and the Aug 14 2026 cliff alone unlocks a ~282M locked cohort worth 28% of total supply. There is no protocol inflation, and a Jun 1 2026 buyback-and-burn is active but tiny — about 22.6M LAB for ~$3.4M — so the framework carries the buy side at zero and reads about +99% net. The key risk is the unlock overhang against a concentrated, insider-heavy float; the ceiling is the fixed 1B cap, most of which is still locked.
