## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 22 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **SKY at +1.55% net** on the forward view, driven by Sky Token Rewards emission that out-issues a deliberately reduced surplus buyback. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+0.98%**, versus the framework's **+1.55%** emission read for the same window — a gap of about **0.6 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The gap is structural: roughly two-thirds of SKY is staked, so a large share of newly minted reward SKY accrues to staked positions and the staking engine and reaches the freely-tradable float more slowly than it is emitted. SKY is **structurally inflationary on the active float**, at a low-single-digit pace, with the buyback running below its full strength.

## Sell pressure: where new SKY comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the whole story, at about **419M SKY** over the next 90 days. Sky emits SKY as rewards through its Sky Token Rewards program to USDS and sUSDS savers and to stakers; the late-February 2026 governance vote, executed in early March, set this emission to **838.18M SKY over 180 days** — a reduction of about 161.82M versus the prior schedule, which works out to roughly **4.7M SKY a day**. Half of that 180-day schedule falls in this 90-day window, hence the 419M figure. These are newly issued reward coins, so they count once, here.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero**. SKY is a redenomination of MKR at 1 MKR to 24,000 SKY, and MKR was already fully circulating with no team or seed lockups, so SKY inherits no vesting cliffs. The remaining MKR-to-SKY upgrades — roughly a fifth of MKR still unconverted — are one-to-one swaps of already-counted value, not new supply. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also zero as a flow: SKY held in the treasury is fed back into the buyback rather than sold, and there is no dated discretionary release pending. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to SKY.

## Buy pressure: where new SKY goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the only active offset, at about **57M SKY** over 90 days. Sky's Smart Burn Engine uses protocol surplus to buy SKY on the open market and remove it from the float, now pairing the purchased SKY into protocol-owned liquidity. The critical recent change: a governance vote on March 14 2026 cut the daily buyback **87%**, from $300,000 to about **37,600 USDS a day**, for three months, to rebuild USDS and DAI reserves against macro risk. At a SKY price near **$0.059**, that throttled spend removes roughly 0.64M SKY a day — well below the engine's full pace. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero because there is no separate fee burn; the surplus buyback is the sole removal mechanism, so it is all booked under buyback. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero, with no discretionary open-market buying or new escrow announced in the window.

## Foundation and overhang

SKY has no classic unlock overhang — the token inherited a fully distributed supply from MKR. The one balance worth watching is the SKY held by the protocol and accumulated through the buyback, which the Smart Burn Engine pairs into SKY/USDS liquidity rather than selling. The unconverted MKR pool is a conversion queue, not a sell-side stockpile, since every upgrade is a one-to-one swap of value already counted. The framework books no discretionary release beyond reward emission and re-checks the governance buyback rate and on-chain emission on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if a treasury or buyback balance falls between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How SKY compares to other DeFi governance tokens

SKY belongs to the class of **uncapped DeFi governance tokens with revenue-funded buybacks** — closer to an exchange token that buys back from fees than to a hard-capped, halving-model coin. Unlike a fixed-supply token, SKY has no ceiling and emits continuously as staking and savings rewards; unlike a pure emission token, it pairs that emission with a surplus buyback that can, at low enough prices, remove more than it issues. The protocol itself frames a balance point near $0.17, where the buyback roughly offsets the reward emission; below that the buyback removes more SKY per dollar, and above it emission wins.

The contrast worth drawing is with the buyback being throttled right now. At full strength the Smart Burn Engine has, in past windows, removed enough SKY to flirt with net deflation; under the 87% cut it removes only a fraction of the reward emission. So for an inflation lens specifically, SKY currently reads as steadily, mildly inflationary — the reward stream is the dominant force, and the throttled buyback is a partial brake rather than an offset. Restore the buyback and the same protocol can flip net deflationary; that switch is a governance decision, not a code constant.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the buyback rate — the three-month throttle that began March 14 2026 is due for review around **Jun 14 2026**, and a restoration toward the prior 300,000 USDS-a-day pace would multiply the offset and could flip net direction. Watch the Sky Token Rewards schedule, since the current 838.18M-over-180-days pace runs out around **Aug 26 2026** and the next governance vote could raise or cut it again. Watch SKY's price against the ~$0.17 balance point, because a lower price makes a given dollar of buyback remove more coins. And expect the framework to keep reading above our supply monitor for as long as reward coins accrue to staked positions rather than reach the float — that gap is structural, not a new unlock.

## Summary

SKY is an uncapped DeFi governance token — the MakerDAO rebrand — whose supply grows through Sky Token Rewards and shrinks through a surplus-funded buyback. The protocol emits about 419M SKY over 90 days at the current reward pace, while the throttled Smart Burn Engine buyback removes roughly 57M back out, leaving the framework at about +1.6% net. Our supply monitor reads +0.98% realized, with the gap explained by reward coins accruing to staked positions instead of reaching the open market. SKY stays mildly inflationary on the active float while the buyback runs at 13% of its prior strength — a balance that can flip the moment governance restores the buyback or cuts the reward rate.
