For the 90-day window ending June 30 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **AVAX at about 0.00% net** — flat — because the staking mint and the network's fee burn run at roughly the same pace. The framework books about **2.5M AVAX** of staking emission against about **2.5M AVAX** of fee burn over 90 days, leaving net supply change near zero. AVAX is best read as **structurally at equilibrium on the active float**: a capped proof-of-stake token where the burn has caught up with the mint.

## Sell pressure: where new AVAX comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the whole sell story, at about **2.5M AVAX** over the next 90 days. Avalanche mints new AVAX as staking rewards paid to validators and delegators, on a curve that runs toward the **720M hard cap**; with roughly 201.9M AVAX staked (about 55% to 58% of circulating), the realized issuance reaching the float is about 2.5M over 90 days, and most of the gross reward re-stakes rather than selling. Every coin is newly issued supply, so the reward counts once, here.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero** for this window: AVAX is roughly 60% unlocked, and its team, seed and investor allocations vest on staggered schedules running into 2030, but no team or investor cliff reaches the market inside the last 90 days. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also carried at **zero** as a market flow: a scheduled Foundation cliff of about **1.67M AVAX** lands around **August 10 2026**, but it releases into Foundation custody for ecosystem use rather than onto the open market, and there is no dated evidence of a sale in the window. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where new AVAX goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: Avalanche runs no revenue-funded buyback. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is the active offset, and it is now large enough to cancel the mint, at about **2.5M AVAX** over 90 days. Every transaction fee on the network is burned on-chain — both the base fee and the priority fee are destroyed, unlike Ethereum where the priority fee is paid to validators — and the pace climbed through 2025 and 2026 as network activity rose. Because the destination is a burn, those coins are gone for good, and at 2026 activity levels the burn has caught up with the staking mint. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero.

## Foundation and overhang

AVAX's overhang is the unvested remainder of supply — roughly 40% of the 720M cap is still locked, split across a Foundation allocation (about 9.26% of supply), a team allocation (about 10%), and strategic and investor buckets, all releasing on staggered cliffs through 2030. The Foundation tranche is the live one in this window: about 1.67M AVAX unlocks roughly quarterly into a Foundation wallet, with the next cliff around August 10 2026. These are not a market dump — they accrue in custody to fund ecosystem incentives, and the framework re-checks the Foundation wallet and the unlock calendar on a roughly bi-weekly walk. If the Foundation balance falls between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How AVAX compares to other capped proof-of-stake chains

AVAX belongs to the class of **hard-capped proof-of-stake L1s that pair a staking mint with a fee burn** — structurally between a pure inflation chain and a deflationary exchange token. Unlike an uncapped continuous-emission chain, AVAX has a fixed 720M ceiling, so emission cannot run forever; unlike a halving-model coin such as Bitcoin, its new supply is staking rewards rather than block subsidies on a fixed schedule. And unlike a token with no sink, every fee is burned, so heavy network use can pull AVAX to net-flat — which is exactly where it sits in mid-2026.

The contrast worth drawing is with Ethereum, the closest structural analogue: both burn the base fee, but Avalanche also burns the priority fee, and AVAX carries a hard cap that ETH does not. The result is that AVAX's burn is a real counterweight to the staking mint — and at 2026 fee levels it has grown to match it, so the asset reads as neither inflationary nor deflationary on the active float. What makes AVAX distinctive for an inflation lens is that this equilibrium is dynamic: a busier network burns more and can tip AVAX net-deflationary, while a quieter one lets the mint edge ahead.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the daily burn rate — it is the single number that decides whether AVAX stays flat, tips deflationary on a busy network, or drifts mildly inflationary if activity cools. Watch the Foundation cliff around August 10 2026 (about 1.67M AVAX): the framework carries it at zero market flow today, but a move out of Foundation custody would push it into Sell #3. Watch the staking ratio, since a higher share staked raises the gross reward mint. And watch network activity broadly, because both sides of the balance — the burn and the realized staking issuance — move with it.
