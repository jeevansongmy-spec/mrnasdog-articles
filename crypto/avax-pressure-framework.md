## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **AVAX at about +0.7% net** on the gross staking mint, which out-issues the network's fee burn. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change in circulating AVAX at about **0.00%** — dead flat — versus the framework's **+0.74%** gross-emission read for the same window, a gap of about **0.7 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The gap is structural: under Avalanche's continuous-staking design, the bulk of newly minted AVAX is paid to validators and delegators that auto-restake it rather than sell. AVAX is best read as **structurally inflationary on paper but near equilibrium on the active float**, under a hard cap.

## Sell pressure: where new AVAX comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the whole gross story, at about **3.4M AVAX** over the next 90 days. Avalanche mints new AVAX as staking rewards paid to validators and delegators, on a curve that runs toward the **720M hard cap**; at the current roughly **7%** reward rate on about 190M staked AVAX, that works out to ~3.4M over 90 days. Every coin is newly issued supply, so the reward counts once, here.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero** for this window: AVAX is roughly 60% unlocked, and its team, seed and investor allocations vest on staggered schedules running into 2030, but no team or investor cliff lands inside the last 90 days. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also carried at **zero** as a market flow: two scheduled Foundation cliffs of about **1.67M AVAX** each land in the next 90 days (June 21 and August 15 2026), but they release into Foundation custody for ecosystem use rather than onto the open market, and there is no dated evidence of a sale in the window. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where new AVAX goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: Avalanche runs no revenue-funded buyback. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is the only active offset, at about **0.2M AVAX** over 90 days. Every transaction fee on the network is burned on-chain — both the base fee and the priority fee are destroyed, unlike Ethereum where the priority fee is paid to validators — at roughly 1,500 AVAX a day through 2026, lifting with network activity. Because the destination is a burn, those coins are gone for good, but the pace is well below the gross mint, so the burn slows dilution rather than reversing it. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero.

## Foundation and overhang

AVAX's overhang is the unvested remainder of supply — roughly 40% of the 720M cap is still locked, split across a Foundation allocation (about 9.26% of supply), a team allocation (about 10%), and strategic and investor buckets, all releasing on staggered cliffs through 2030. The Foundation tranche is the live one in this window: about 1.67M AVAX unlocks roughly quarterly into a Foundation wallet, with cliffs on June 21 and August 15 2026. These are not a market dump — they accrue in custody to fund ecosystem incentives, and the framework re-checks the Foundation wallet and the unlock calendar on a roughly bi-weekly walk. If the Foundation balance falls between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How AVAX compares to other capped proof-of-stake chains

AVAX belongs to the class of **hard-capped proof-of-stake L1s that pair a staking mint with a fee burn** — structurally between a pure inflation chain and a deflationary exchange token. Unlike an uncapped continuous-emission chain, AVAX has a fixed 720M ceiling, so emission cannot run forever; unlike a halving-model coin such as Bitcoin, its new supply is staking rewards rather than block subsidies on a fixed schedule. And unlike a token with no sink, every fee is burned, so heavy network use can push AVAX toward net-flat or even net-deflationary in busy periods.

The contrast worth drawing is with Ethereum, the closest structural analogue: both burn the base fee, but Avalanche also burns the priority fee, and AVAX carries a hard cap that ETH does not. The result is that AVAX's burn is a real brake on dilution but, at 2026 fee levels, only a fraction of the gross staking mint — so the asset reads as mildly inflationary on paper. What makes AVAX distinctive for an inflation lens is the auto-restaking design: because rewards compound back into stake rather than hitting exchanges, the realized tradeable float can stay flat even while the protocol mints, which is exactly the gap our monitor surfaces.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the daily burn rate — at roughly 1,500 AVAX a day it is the single number that decides whether the network drifts toward net-flat; a sustained activity surge, like the mid-2026 real-world-asset transfer spike, lifts the burn toward the staking mint. Watch the two Foundation cliffs on June 21 and August 15 2026 (about 1.67M AVAX each): the framework carries them at zero market flow today, but a move out of Foundation custody would push them into Sell #3. Watch the staking ratio, since a higher share staked raises the gross reward mint. And expect the framework to keep reading slightly above our supply monitor for as long as rewards auto-restake rather than reach the float — that gap is structural, not a new unlock.

## Summary

AVAX is a hard-capped proof-of-stake gas token whose supply grows as staking rewards minted toward a 720M ceiling. The network mints about 3.4M AVAX over 90 days at the current ~7% reward rate, while a 100%-fee burn destroys roughly 0.2M back out, leaving the framework at about +0.7% net on the gross mint. Our supply monitor reads the tradeable float as flat, with the gap explained by rewards auto-restaking instead of selling. The key risk is the unvested 40% still releasing through 2030; the key constraint is the hard cap, which means AVAX can never inflate without limit — and can tip net-deflationary when the burn outruns the mint.
