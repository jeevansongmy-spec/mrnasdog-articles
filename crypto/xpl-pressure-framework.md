# XPL Inflation Analysis · July 2026 · Supply growing, projected to keep growing

Plasma added about **226.7M XPL** to the market over the last 90 days, a net of **+8.72%** against a circulating base of **2,600.0M XPL** — and our supply monitor agrees, reading **+9.55%** on the same window off an almost identical **226.6M** numerator. The structural story is that Plasma creates no new XPL at all: validator inflation is written into the tokenomics at **5%** a year but has never been switched on, so every token reaching the market is an unlock from a pool minted on launch day. The forward reading is the one that matters. The team and investor one-year cliff releases roughly **1,666.7M XPL** on **Sep 25 2026**, inside the next 90 days, taking the projection to **+72.82%** with nothing on the buy side to absorb it.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending **Jul 20 2026**, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **XPL at +8.72% net**. Sell pressure totals **226.7M XPL** and buy pressure is **zero**, against a circulating base of **2,600.0M XPL**. Our supply monitor reads the same window at **+9.55%**, a gap of **0.83 percentage points**. A full deep walk closed that gap outright: the monitor's implied supply delta is **226.6M XPL** and the framework's independently derived on-chain release is **226.7M XPL**, so both sides measure the same flow and the residual is nothing more than which end of the window supplies the denominator. No monitor-gap chip ships on the Plasma overview page. XPL is best characterised as **a non-inflationary chain sitting directly in front of a cliff**.

## Sell pressure: where new XPL comes from

Sell #1, protocol inflation, is **zero**, and that is the fact most readers get wrong about Plasma. The published Plasma tokenomics do describe validator inflation — rewards beginning at **5%** a year, decaying by **0.5%** a year to a **3%** floor — but the same document is explicit that it activates only once external validators and stake delegation go live, and Plasma still runs its validator set in-house. The chain confirms the switch is off. Recent Plasma blocks carry an empty withdrawals list, none of the twelve system addresses behaves like a reward distributor, and the fee-collector address moved from **638,151 XPL** to **746,072 XPL** across the entire window — roughly **108,000 XPL** of priority tips over three months, which is fee accrual and not issuance. Until delegation ships, Plasma mints nothing.

Sell #2, vesting unlocks, is **226.7M XPL** and is the whole of the realised Plasma sell ledger for this window. The published Plasma calendar releases about **88.9M XPL** from the Ecosystem & Growth pool on the 25th of each month, so three tranches inside a quarter would be **266.7M**. The framework reads the escrow instead of the calendar. The four wallets that hold Plasma's entire non-circulating allocation held **7,633.6M XPL** on **Apr 21 2026** and **7,257.1M XPL** on **Jul 20 2026**, a gross outflow of **376.5M**. Of that, **149.8M** moved into a cross-chain lock contract whose balance rose from **115.2M** to **265.0M** over the same period — XPL relocated to another chain but still team-held, not XPL sold. Netting that relocation off leaves **226.7M XPL** genuinely reaching the market, which is the figure the framework ships and the figure the monitor independently arrives at.

Sell #3, Foundation and unscheduled unlocks, is **zero**: nothing was released at anyone's discretion beyond the vesting flow already counted, and the overhang behind that zero is covered below. Sell #4, long-term locked or bankruptcy, is **zero** — Plasma launched in September 2025 with no estate, no trustee and no court-ordered distribution of XPL. Sell #5 is the row the whole page turns on: the Plasma team and investor one-year cliff of roughly **1,666.7M XPL** on **Sep 25 2026**. One third of the **2,500M** team allocation and one third of the **2,500M** investor allocation unlock together on that date, one year to the day after mainnet beta, with the remaining two thirds vesting monthly to **Sep 25 2028**. It is booked as a dated event rather than folded into the continuous vesting row, and the escrow evidence that the supply is really there is direct: one of those two pools sits in a wallet holding exactly **2,500.0M XPL** that has not moved a single token at either end of the window.

## Buy pressure: where new XPL goes

The Plasma buy ledger is empty, and all four rows are **zero**. Buy #1, programmatic buyback, is zero because no Plasma buyback exists — there is no buying wallet, no published quantum and no repurchase programme anywhere in the Plasma token model. Buy #3, Foundation buy, is zero for the same reason: no open-market XPL purchase has ever been disclosed. Buy #4, new long-term lock, is zero as a direct consequence of Sell #1 being zero. Staking and delegation are the mechanism that would let XPL holders lock tokens away, and because that mechanism has not launched, there is simply no contract on Plasma into which XPL can be committed.

Buy #2, protocol fee burn, is the interesting zero. Plasma genuinely does run an EIP-1559 base-fee burn, and the Plasma tokenomics present it as the counterweight that will offset validator inflation once inflation begins. Arithmetically it is nil today. The base fee on Plasma reads **10 wei**, the floor the chain permits, and a busy block consumes on the order of **10.9M** gas — which means the roughly **7,776,000** blocks in a 90-day window destroy well under a single XPL between them. The deeper reason is by design: Plasma sponsors its flagship zero-fee stablecoin transfers through a paymaster, so the dominant activity on the chain pays no base fee at all. A burn that scales with fee revenue cannot bite on a chain built to make its most common transaction free.

## Foundation and overhang

Plasma's overhang is the dominant fact about XPL, and it is unusually legible because the entire locked allocation resolves to four readable wallets. Together they held **7,257.1M XPL** on **Jul 20 2026** — nearly three times the tradable float. The largest holds **2,674.5M**, down from **2,847.5M** at the window open. A second holds exactly **2,500.0M** and has never moved a token, which is one of the two cliff pools sitting intact. A third holds **1,527.0M**, down from **1,730.5M**, and a fourth holds **555.6M** unchanged. Alongside them, a cross-chain lock contract has grown from **115.2M** to **265.0M** — Plasma supply moved to another chain rather than released to buyers, and therefore still an overhang rather than a completed sale. Cross-checking against the published Plasma split lands in the same place: **4,000M** ecosystem less the **1,600M** the calendar has released to date, plus **2,500M** team and **2,500M** investors, gives **7,400M** that should be locked, against **7,257.1M** actually held — so release is running slightly ahead of the calendar, not behind it. Every one of these balances is read on-chain at each rebuild, and if any of them falls between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How XPL compares to other stablecoin-settlement layer-1 chains

Plasma belongs to a small and distinctive class: layer-1 chains built to settle stablecoins rather than to host general computation, where the native token is collateral and governance rather than the unit users actually transact in. That inverts the usual supply arithmetic. On a general-purpose chain with native-token gas, heavy usage burns the token and the burn genuinely offsets staking emissions — the busier the chain, the tighter the supply. On Plasma the busiest possible day burns almost nothing, because the transfers driving that volume are sponsored and denominated in stablecoins. The Plasma burn is real in code and immaterial in practice, and no realistic level of adoption changes that while the paymaster model stands.

Against uncapped proof-of-stake layer-1s that mint validator rewards continuously, Plasma looks better on paper right now and worse on schedule. Those chains typically run a few percent of annual issuance forever, but they issue it smoothly and their float has usually finished unlocking. Plasma issues nothing at all today — a genuinely rare position for a layer-1 — while carrying roughly **7,400M XPL** of locked supply against a **2,600.0M** float, which is the profile of a token whose entire supply risk is calendar risk rather than emission risk. Against other 2025-vintage tokens working through a multi-year vest, what separates XPL is concentration. Most of that cohort releases on a smooth monthly line; Plasma has put **1,666.7M XPL** — about **64%** of everything currently circulating — on a single date. And unlike the exchange tokens that pair a large vest with a quarterly buyback or fee burn, Plasma has no absorbing mechanism on the other side at all.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

First and above everything, watch **Sep 25 2026**: the Plasma team and investor one-year cliff of roughly **1,666.7M XPL**. The framework books the full entitlement, but the number that will actually matter is how much leaves the escrow — that wallet is read on-chain at every rebuild, and a cliff that unlocks on paper while the balance sits still is a very different outcome from one that drains. Second, watch the monthly Plasma ecosystem tranches of about **88.9M XPL** on **Jul 25 2026** and **Aug 25 2026**, and the draw rate against them, which has been running near the calendar rather than far behind it. Third, treat the US public-sale lockup expiry of **Jul 28 2026** as a non-event for supply: Plasma's debut float of **1,800M** already contained the full **1,000M** public sale, so the expiry lifts a transfer restriction on tokens that have been counted as circulating since day one. Fourth, watch for the launch of Plasma validator staking and delegation, which is the single switch that turns Sell #1 from zero to roughly **5%** a year and simultaneously opens Buy #4. Fifth, watch the cross-chain lock balance — a fall there would mean relocated Plasma supply arriving somewhere it can be sold.

## Summary

The MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads Plasma at **+8.72% net** over the last 90 days and **+72.82%** projected for the next 90, against our supply monitor at **+9.55%** for the completed window — a **0.83pp** gap that the deep walk closed entirely to denominator convention. The structural mechanism is that Plasma creates no XPL: validator inflation is designed but switched off, so all **226.7M XPL** that reached the market came out of pools minted on launch day, and the EIP-1559 burn that is supposed to offset future issuance destroys under one XPL a quarter because the chain sponsors its own core transfers. The key risk is concentration rather than issuance: **1,666.7M XPL**, roughly **64%** of the current float, unlocks to the team and early investors on **Sep 25 2026**, with no buyback, no meaningful burn and no staking lock anywhere on the buy side to absorb it. There is no protocol cap on XPL, but the practical ceiling is the **10,000M** minted at genesis — and **7,257.1M** of it is still sitting in four wallets, ahead of the market.
