## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window from Jul 14 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **OP at +4.33% net**, driven almost entirely by scheduled vesting cliffs against a collapsed buyback. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day float change at **+1.04%**, a gap of about **3.29 percentage points** — well outside the 0.5-point tolerance, so a **⚠ monitor-gap chip** ships. The gap is honest, not a rounding artifact: OP unlocks about **31.34M** a month on a monthly cliff (the **Jul 31 2026** cliff is confirmed at **31,340,000 OP**), for roughly **94M OP** a window, but much of each release goes to core contributors and investors who have not moved it to market, and Retro Funding and ecosystem OP stays inside the Collective treasury — so on-chain float rose far more slowly than the gross unlock. OP is **structurally inflationary by vesting**, at a mid-single-digit quarterly pace on the schedule, with a much softer realized read on the float.

## Sell pressure: where new OP comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**. Optimism is an Ethereum layer-2 where gas is paid in ETH, not OP, so the network never mints new OP; the fixed initial supply of **4,294,967,296 OP** is unchanged, and total supply still equals the cap. A dormant provision lets the Token House switch on up to **2%** annual inflation, but it has never been activated, so protocol emission contributes nothing.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the engine, at about **94M OP** over the next 90 days. OP's core-contributor and early-investor allocations unlock on a monthly cliff on the last day of each month, releasing roughly **31.34M OP** each time; the **Jul 31 2026** cliff alone is **31,340,000 OP**, about **1.79%** of circulating supply. Three of these cliffs fall inside the window — **Jul 31 2026**, **Aug 31 2026** and **Sep 30 2026** — and the schedule runs to about **2029**. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — carries a projected value of **zero** even though the overhang is enormous: the Collective treasury and Retro Funding reserve reach the market only when governance votes to spend, on no fixed schedule, so the framework enumerates and monitors that overhang rather than projecting a forward figure. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to OP.

## Buy pressure: where new OP goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the only non-zero buy-side row, and it is small at about **0.5M OP** over 90 days. Optimism governance approved a buyback on **Jan 28 2026** that spends half of Superchain sequencer revenue on open-market OP each month, and its first month bought **1.57M OP** with 95.8 ETH. But on **Feb 18 2026** Coinbase's Base — about **96.5%** of the Collective's gas fees — announced it was rotating off the OP Stack, collapsing the revenue the buyback captures from roughly **$8.75M** a year to a few hundred thousand dollars. The bought-back OP settles to a treasury wallet on-chain, where it may later be burned or used as staking rewards, but for now it is simply held. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is **zero**: gas is paid in ETH, so OP is never burned. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are also **zero**, with no separate discretionary OP buying and no new escrow announced in the window. That near-absence of any offset is the point of OP's inflation profile: cliffs add supply, and almost nothing takes it away.

## Foundation and overhang

OP's dominant overhang is the **Optimism Collective treasury** — the roughly **2.14B OP** that remains non-circulating, about half of the fixed supply, spread across the ecosystem fund, governance fund, partner and seed funds, and the Retro Funding reserve (the Collective earmarked **850M OP** for public goods alone). This treasury reaches the market only when governance votes to spend it, which is why Sell #3 projects its discretionary portion at a monitored zero rather than a scheduled cliff. A second overhang is the still-vesting core-contributor and investor balance, which drains predictably each month at about **31.34M OP** until the schedule completes around **2029**. A third is the buyback accumulation wallet — bought-back OP held at treasury address **0x36c4…33DA**, readable on-chain each refresh. The framework re-checks the unlock schedule, Retro Funding rounds and treasury movements on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if any of these balances falls between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How OP compares to other Ethereum layer-2 tokens

OP belongs to the class of **fixed-supply layer-2 governance tokens** — like Arbitrum's ARB, it is capped, mints nothing, and charges gas in ETH so there is no base-fee burn of the token itself. That makes OP fundamentally different from Ethereum, where the base-fee burn can push net supply negative, and from an exchange token that buys back and burns from revenue. Both OP and ARB are still mid-vest, so both dilute from monthly unlocks with no emission and no burn to complicate the picture — their inflation reads cleanly off the unlock calendar rather than off network activity.

What briefly set OP apart was its 2026 revenue-linked buyback, a buy-side lever ARB has never had. But the buyback's scale depends entirely on Superchain revenue, and Base's exit gutted that revenue almost overnight, so in practice OP today looks much like ARB: monthly cliffs push supply up with only a token offset. Against capped tokens whose unlocks have already finished and settled to flat supply, OP still dilutes at roughly four to five percent a quarter on the schedule. The one structural nuance worth noting is the wedge between schedule and float: because so much OP unlocks to insiders and to a treasury that holds rather than sells, the realized on-chain float grows far slower than the gross cliffs — which is exactly why our monitor reads **+1.04%** against the framework's **+4.33%**. For an inflation lens specifically, OP reads as **structurally inflationary by vesting**, softened on the float by treasury retention.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the three monthly vesting cliffs on **Jul 31 2026**, **Aug 31 2026** and **Sep 30 2026** — each unlocks about **31.34M OP** and together they are essentially all of the supply growth. Watch how much of each cliff actually reaches the market versus staying in insider and treasury wallets, since that is what drives the gap between the framework and the monitor. Watch the monthly buyback execution reports for whether Superchain revenue, and therefore OP demand, recovers from Base's departure, or whether governance moves to pause or repurpose the program. And watch any Token House proposal that would activate the dormant 2% inflation provision, which would add a protocol-emission row that is zero today.
