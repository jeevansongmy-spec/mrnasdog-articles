## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 30 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **OP at +2.7% net** on the forward view, driven by monthly vesting unlocks that out-issue the revenue-funded buyback. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+1.99%**, versus the framework's **+4.14%** gross-vesting read for the same window — a gap of about **2.15 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The gap is structural: the monthly cliffs unlock about **94M OP** a window on paper, but much of each release — RetroPGF, ecosystem and Foundation buckets — is retained inside the Optimism Collective treasury rather than reaching the open float, and the buyback pulls more OP back into that same treasury. OP is **structurally inflationary on the active float**, at a low-single-digit pace, with a large locked overhang still ahead.

## Sell pressure: where new OP comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero** for OP, because OP is a governance token, not a chain fuel coin: there are no block rewards or staking emissions minting new OP. Every new circulating OP comes from the vesting schedule instead. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the whole story, at about **62.7M OP** over the next 90 days. A scheduled cliff of roughly **31.34M OP** releases on the last day of each month — about **16.5M** to core contributors and **14.8M** to investors — and two of those cliffs, on **Jul 31 2026** and **Aug 31 2026**, land inside the window. The schedule runs in shrinking amounts to 2029.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a flow: beyond the monthly cliff schedule there is no dated discretionary release in the window. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to OP. The standout fact is the overhang: with circulating OP near **2,159.5M** against a **4,294,967,296** hard cap, roughly **2,135M OP** — about half the total — is still locked across investor, contributor, RetroPGF, ecosystem, partner, seed and governance buckets.

## Buy pressure: where new OP goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the only active offset, at about **4.7M OP** over 90 days. Optimism governance approved, with **84.4%** support, directing **50%** of net Superchain sequencer revenue into a monthly open-market OP buyback for a 12-month pilot that began in February 2026; the first execution converted **1,574,817 OP** through an OTC desk, so the run-rate is roughly 1.6M OP a month. The crucial detail is the destination: the bought-back OP is **held in the Collective treasury**, not burned, so it leaves the open float but stays a tracked overhang that governance could later burn, stake or redeploy. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero, because Superchain transaction fees are paid in ETH, not OP. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero.

## Foundation and overhang

OP carries one of the larger team-controlled overhangs in the framework. About **2,135M OP** remains locked — investor and core-contributor allocations on the monthly cliff schedule, plus RetroPGF, ecosystem, partner, seed and governance funds that are largely held inside the Optimism Collective treasury. The buyback accumulation wallet, settling around **1.6M OP** a month, is a second tracked balance on top of that. None of these is booked as a discretionary sell today, because each release follows either the published cliff schedule or governance direction. The framework re-checks the unlock calendar and the buyback execution thread on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if a treasury balance falls between refreshes outside the published schedule, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How OP compares to other governance-token L2s

OP belongs to the class of **capped governance tokens on Ethereum Layer-2s** whose supply growth comes from **vesting unlocks, not from protocol inflation** — the same shape as other large L2 governance tokens with multi-year investor and contributor cliffs. Unlike a proof-of-stake L1, OP has no staking emission and no block-reward mint; unlike a fee-burn token, its network fees are paid in ETH, so transaction activity removes no OP. That makes the unlock calendar, not chain usage, the dominant supply force.

What sets OP apart within that class is the revenue-funded buyback: most unlock-driven L2 tokens have no structural buy-side offset at all, whereas Optimism routes half of Superchain sequencer revenue into monthly OP purchases. For an inflation lens, the buyback is real but small next to the unlock schedule — roughly a twentieth of the gross monthly cliff — so it slows dilution rather than reversing it. OP reads as mildly but persistently inflationary on the active float until the vesting schedule winds down toward 2029.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the monthly vesting cliffs on **Jul 31 2026** and **Aug 31 2026**, each releasing about 31.34M OP to core contributors and investors — the single biggest supply event in the window. Watch the buyback execution thread, since the program's monthly OP fills depend on Superchain sequencer revenue. Watch any governance decision on what to do with the treasury-held buyback OP — a vote to burn it would turn the buyback from accumulation into real deflation. And expect the framework to keep reading above our supply monitor for as long as unlocked OP is retained in the Collective treasury rather than reaching the open float.
