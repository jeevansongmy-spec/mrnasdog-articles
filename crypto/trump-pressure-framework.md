Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**. Official Trump is a fixed-supply SPL token on Solana with a hard cap of **1B coins** and no on-chain mint, so the network creates no new TRUMP. Every coin that will ever exist already exists; the only question is when it moves from locked to circulating.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the whole story, at about **4.95M TRUMP** over the next 90 days. Insiders — the Fight Fight Fight and CIC Digital entities behind six "Creators & CIC Digital" groups — hold roughly **80%** of supply and release it on a schedule that runs into 2028. Of the gross schedule, the framework books only what actually reached the open market: circulating supply grew about **4.95M** over the last 90 days, and a similar pace is due over the next 90.

Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is **zero** as a flow: there is no public evidence of a discretionary insider release outside the daily vesting in the window. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is **zero**, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to TRUMP.

## Buy pressure: where new TRUMP goes

## Foundation and overhang

The defining feature of TRUMP is its overhang. About **80%** of the 1B cap is held by insider entities — the six "Creators & CIC Digital" groups plus team, treasury and liquidity allocations — and only roughly **237.4M** coins, about 24% of supply, are circulating today. The daily linear drip releases on the order of **0.049%** of total supply each day from April 20 2026 for 1,096 days, but most of those tokens move into insider custody wallets rather than directly onto exchanges, which is why realized float grows far slower than the gross schedule. The framework re-checks the vesting calendar and the circulating read on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if a custody balance falls onto the market faster than the schedule implies, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How TRUMP compares to other memecoins with insider vesting

TRUMP belongs to the class of **fixed-supply memecoins with heavy insider vesting** — closer to a celebrity or founder token with a long unlock tail than to a hard-capped proof-of-work coin or an uncapped emission chain. Unlike a halving-model coin, TRUMP has no block reward and no miner issuance; unlike an exchange token, it runs no fee burn or buyback to claw supply back. The supply curve is therefore one-directional: locked coins convert to circulating on a calendar, and nothing converts back.

The contrast worth drawing is with tokens that pair issuance with a burn or buyback and can go net-deflationary. TRUMP has neither brake, so its inflation is set entirely by the unlock pace against a still-small float. That makes it structurally and steadily inflationary for as long as the vesting schedule runs — a profile driven by tokenomics design, not by network usage or protocol revenue.
