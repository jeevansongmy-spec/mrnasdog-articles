NEAR Protocol mints about **7.97M NEAR** every 90 days in validator rewards — a **2.5%** annual emission that was cut in half from 5% and is now live in the protocol itself. Against that, the NEAR Intents buyback absorbs roughly **0.77M NEAR** and the gas burn destroys another **0.06M**, leaving the MrNasdog Pressure Framework at about **+0.55% net** for the window, against our supply monitor's **+0.28%**. NEAR is an uncapped, continuously-emitting layer-1 whose buy side is real but still an order of magnitude too small to cancel the mint.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending **July 19 2026**, the framework reads **NEAR at about +0.55% net** — a sell side of **7.97M NEAR** against a buy side of **0.83M NEAR**, over a circulating base of about **1.30B NEAR**. Our supply monitor reads the realised change over the same window at **+0.28%**, a gap of roughly **0.27 percentage points** — inside tolerance, so no monitor-gap chip ships and the read stands clean. The honest label for NEAR is **a halved emission the burn is still too small to cancel**: the inflation rate is genuinely half what it was, the buyback is genuinely funded by real fee revenue, and both facts together still leave supply drifting up rather than down.

## Sell pressure: where new NEAR comes from

Almost all of NEAR's sell pressure is one row. Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **7.97M NEAR** over the window, and it is not an estimate: NEAR Protocol writes the emission straight into the chain's own total-supply field, which steps up once per epoch by the validator reward and decays every block by the gas burn. Reading that field at both ends of the window and adding the measured burn back gives the gross mint. Each epoch — roughly every seven hours — pays out about **26.7K NEAR**, which annualises to about **32M NEAR**, or **2.5%** of total supply. That 2.5% is the halved rate: the tokenomics upgrade that took the emission down from 5% went live on **Oct 30 2025**, and the protocol's own live configuration confirms the lower setting today. Roughly a tenth of each epoch reward is routed to the protocol treasury rather than to validators, but that share is minted supply either way and is already counted inside this row.

The other three sell rows are all zero, and for a structural reason worth stating plainly. Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero** because NEAR is fully unlocked: total supply and circulating supply are the same number, and the original five-year distribution schedule for core contributors, backers, the community sale and the early ecosystem physically expired in 2025. There is no cliff left to fire. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also **zero**, because there is no non-circulating reserve to draw down; every NEAR in existence already sits inside the float that the percentage is measured against. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, with no estate and no court-ordered distribution touching NEAR.

## Buy pressure: where new NEAR goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **0.77M NEAR** and it is the most interesting row on the page. Since **Feb 23 2026**, fees captured by NEAR Intents, the chain's cross-chain swap layer, are converted into open-market NEAR purchases. Three on-chain accounts receive those purchases, and reading their balances at both ends of the window shows a net gain of about **0.77M NEAR** — including one dedicated buyback multisig that went from effectively empty to roughly **584K NEAR** inside this window alone. Cross-checking against the protocol revenue that funded it — about **$1.63M** over the same 90 days — lands on the same figure at the window's average clearing price. One nuance matters for anyone modelling this: the widely-quoted Intents fee number is far larger than the revenue number, because most of the taker fee is kept by solvers and market makers; only the protocol's own slice funds the buyback. Dividing the headline fee figure by price would overstate the buy row roughly sixfold.

Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is **0.06M NEAR**. NEAR burns 70% of the gas paid on every transaction, and because the burn shows up directly as decay in the chain's total supply, it can be measured rather than inferred. Measured, it is small: gas on NEAR sits at its floor price, so 90 days of network activity destroyed only about **59K NEAR**. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — is zero, with no discretionary open-market buying evidenced in the window. Buy #4 — new long-term lock — is also zero: NEAR holders can lock coins for governance voting power and more than **5M NEAR** sits locked, but each lock lives in its own sub-account, so the net change over the window cannot be measured cleanly and the framework leaves it at zero rather than estimating it.

## Foundation and overhang

NEAR has no classic team overhang — there is no locked allocation, no vesting contract and no non-circulating reserve, because distribution finished in 2025. Two things are still tracked. The first is the **protocol treasury account**, which takes roughly a tenth of every epoch reward and paid out about **20.4M NEAR** in a single sequence on **May 13 2026**, falling from about 20.2M to under 0.6M before beginning to refill from subsequent epochs. Where that payout landed could not be confirmed from primary sources this session, so it is surfaced as an overhang rather than booked as market supply — and because every NEAR is already counted as circulating, a treasury-to-elsewhere transfer adds no new circulating supply in any case. The second is the **buyback accumulation wallets**, which now hold a growing balance of bought-back NEAR that is held and staked rather than burned. If either the treasury balance or the buyback wallets fall between refreshes, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How NEAR compares to other uncapped proof-of-stake chains

NEAR sits in the same structural family as the large uncapped, continuously-emitting layer-1s: supply is created by a staking reward with no ceiling, and the only question is how much of it a fee mechanism claws back. On that axis NEAR now looks better than most of its peers on the emission side and worse on the offset side. Its 2.5% annual issuance is genuinely low for an uncapped proof-of-stake chain — governance halved it, and the change is encoded in the protocol rather than promised — but its fee burn is a rounding error, because NEAR's gas is cheap by design and sits at the floor.

The comparison that flatters NEAR is with a chain whose issuance funds a similar validator set but has no fee-funded buyback at all: NEAR at least converts real product revenue into real market bids, which is a demand channel most emitting layer-1s do not have. The comparison that does not flatter NEAR is with a fee-burning chain where activity alone can flip the coin net-deflationary; NEAR's burn cannot do that at current gas prices, so the buyback would have to grow several times over before the net turns negative. Against hard-capped, halving-model chains the difference is categorical rather than a matter of degree: NEAR has no maximum supply, so its inflation is a policy setting that governance can move in either direction, not a schedule fixed in code.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

The clearest dated item is the governance decision passed on **Jul 8 2026** to remove NEAR's developer gas rebate, which today returns 30% of contract gas to the contract owner. Once it ships — expected around **Aug 2026** with the next network release — **100%** of contract gas burns instead of 70%, lifting Buy #2 by roughly **43%**. That is real but small at current gas levels; it matters more as a signal that governance keeps tightening the supply side. Watch NEAR Intents volume, because the buyback scales directly with it and is by far the largest lever on the buy side. Watch the protocol treasury account for another payout on the scale of the **May 13 2026** one, and watch whether a destination is disclosed for the last one. Watch the governance forum for any follow-up to the recurring proposal to give NEAR a fixed maximum supply, which would change the category the coin sits in. And watch the growth of governance locking, which would become a genuine Buy #4 the moment it can be measured as a net flow.

## Summary

NEAR Protocol is an uncapped, continuously-emitting proof-of-stake layer-1 that mints about 7.97M NEAR every 90 days at a 2.5% annual rate — half what it was before the Oct 30 2025 halving. A fee-funded buyback returns about 0.77M NEAR and the gas burn destroys about 0.06M, leaving the framework at roughly +0.55% net against our supply monitor's +0.28%, a clean match with no gap chip. The key risk is that both offsets are structurally small: the burn is capped by NEAR's deliberately cheap gas, and the buyback is capped by Intents revenue, so supply keeps drifting up unless usage grows several times over. The key point in NEAR's favour is that there is no ceiling to fear on the other side either — no locked reserve, no vesting cliff and no treasury overhang waiting behind the float, because every NEAR that exists is already circulating.
