## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 24 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **GT at −2.4% net** — supply shrinking, with the forward view holding at roughly **−2.4%** as the next quarterly burn lands. There is no sell pressure to weigh against it: GateToken has no protocol inflation, no mint function, and no scheduled unlock inside the window. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at about **−38%** — a gap of roughly **36 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. That gap is not a 60M-coin burn; it is a one-time correction to how circulating supply is counted, where long-locked team, treasury and community coins stopped being tallied as in-circulation. The only real destruction in the window was the on-chain quarterly burn of about **2.56M GT**. GT is **structurally deflationary by design**: a fixed, declining exchange token.

## Sell pressure: where new GT comes from

The short answer is that no new GT comes from anywhere. Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**: GateToken has no mint and no emission schedule, so the supply can only stay flat or fall. The original cap was 300 million coins, and about **62%** of that has already been permanently destroyed through years of quarterly burns, leaving roughly 121M total and 106.5M circulating.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is also zero as a flow. GateToken does still carry locked allocations for team, treasury and community, but the next scheduled unlock is dated years out in 2030; nothing vests into the market inside this window. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero, with no dated discretionary release. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero. Every sell row on the GateToken ledger is empty.

## Buy pressure: where new GT goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the only active flow on the page, at about **2.5M GT** over 90 days. Each quarter the Gate exchange commits part of its profit — driven by net trading-fee income and on-chain gas — to buy GT and destroy it on-chain. The Q1 2026 burn removed **2,557,729 GT** (over $20M) on Apr 27 2026, and the Q2 2026 burn, which Gate typically announces in late July, should remove a similar amount on recent trend. Because the destination is a burn address, those coins are gone for good.

Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is zero as a separate line: the on-chain gas that would feed a fee burn already flows into the quarterly buyback-and-burn above, so counting it twice would double-book the same destruction. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero.

## Foundation and overhang

GateToken does carry a real locked overhang: team and shareholder, treasury, and community allocations that are not yet in circulation. The key fact for an inflation read is timing — the next scheduled release is dated 2030, so none of it reaches the market inside this window or the next. Those coins are excluded from circulating supply, which is exactly why the aggregator's recent recount of circulating GT looks like a huge drop even though no extra coins were burned. The framework books no discretionary release beyond the quarterly burn; if a locked balance were released early, that outflow would enter Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How GT compares to other exchange tokens

GT belongs to the class of **exchange tokens with a profit-funded quarterly buyback-and-burn** — the same structural model as the largest exchange coins. The defining trait of this class is that supply is fixed or capped and only ever shrinks: there is no block reward, no staking emission, no continuous inflation to fight against. That puts GT on the opposite end of the spectrum from an uncapped proof-of-stake L1, where new coins are minted every block and a burn only slows dilution rather than reversing it.

Within the exchange-token class, the variable that matters is how aggressively the burn runs relative to the float. GateToken has already destroyed about 62% of its genesis supply, so the remaining float is small and each quarterly burn of ~2.5M coins removes a meaningful 2%-plus of circulating supply. That makes GT one of the cleaner deflationary reads in the framework: no mint to offset the burn, and a burn pace tied directly to exchange revenue rather than to token emissions.
