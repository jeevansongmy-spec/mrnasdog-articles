## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **FET at about -0.04% net** on the forward view — roughly neutral, with a quarterly burn just edging out a shrinking unlock drip on a hard-capped supply. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **-0.07%**, versus the framework's **-0.04%** for the same window — a gap of about **0.03 percentage points**, well inside tolerance, so no monitor-gap chip ships. Both reads tell the same story: FET is structurally capped, vesting is nearly finished, and the Earn & Burn is small but real. FET is a **quiet, hard-capped AI token** sitting on the flat line between mild deflation and zero growth.

## Sell pressure: where new FET comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**, and that is the structural heart of FET. The token is hard-capped at **2.71B FET** (total supply equals max supply), and the Artificial Superintelligence Alliance runs it as a non-inflationary token: staking rewards on the Fetch.ai chain are paid from a pre-allocated reserve that already sits inside the cap, not from new issuance. The early staking-reward phase was front-loaded and the model is moving to a fee-based design, so no new FET is minted above the ceiling — there is no continuous emission to book.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the only live sell-side flow, at about **4M FET** over the next 90 days. Migration vesting is roughly **97%** complete as of February 2025, so what remains is a thin tail of AGIX and Ocean migration drips that finish by August 2026; the next is on **Jun 28 2026** at about **2.59M FET**, with no large cliff left. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a flow: the Foundation reserve has been unlocked since 2023 but books no dated discretionary release, and the **286M FET** Ocean returned in the early-2026 settlement is held, not sold. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where new FET goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the only active offset, at about **5M FET** over 90 days. The Earn & Burn programme, launched **Jan 10 2026** with a first burn of **5M FET**, spends ecosystem service fees buying FET on the open market and burning it once a quarter, targeting roughly **35M FET** in its first year. Because the destination is a burn, those coins are gone for good. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is carried at zero: the Fetch.ai chain has no separately quantified base-fee burn. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero.

## Foundation and overhang

FET's overhang is held, not scheduled. The Foundation reserve plus the **286M FET** Ocean returned in the early-2026 lawsuit settlement together make up most of the gap between the **2.26B** circulating and the **2.71B** capped total. Neither is on a published release schedule, and neither has moved to market in the window, so both sit in Sell #3 at a flow of zero. The framework re-checks the Foundation wallets, the settlement holdings and the burn reports on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if any of those balances falls between refreshes, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How FET compares to other hard-capped tokens

FET belongs to the class of **hard-capped tokens with no protocol inflation** — closer to a fixed-supply asset than to an uncapped, continuous-emission L1. Unlike a staking chain that mints fresh rewards every block, FET pays staking yield from a reserve already counted inside its 2.71B cap, so its supply cannot grow past the ceiling. That puts it alongside exchange and utility tokens that pair a fixed cap with a fee-funded burn, rather than alongside inflationary proof-of-stake chains.

The contrast worth drawing is with tokens that burn aggressively enough to go firmly net-deflationary. FET's Earn & Burn is real but modest — about 5M FET a quarter against a 2.26B float — so it roughly cancels the last of the migration unlocks rather than driving meaningful scarcity. For an inflation lens, that means FET reads as flat: no new mint, a thin unlock tail, and a small burn that just offsets it.

Watch the next quarterly Earn & Burn, expected around **Jul 10 2026** — the size of each burn is the single number that decides whether FET tips mildly deflationary once the unlock tail ends. Watch the final migration unlocks finishing by **August 2026**, after which the sell side goes to near zero. And watch for the long-promised FET-to-ASI rebrand, which would not change supply but could shift how the merged tokenomics are reported.
