ORCA is a **fixed-supply** Solana exchange token — the protocol mints nothing, all vesting ended in 2024, and a treasury buyback spends **40%** of fees pulling ORCA off the market. That leaves the Pressure Framework reading about **−0.3%** net, with supply edging down. Our supply monitor reads **−0.29%** over the same window — the two line up.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 20 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **ORCA at about −0.3% net** — mildly deflationary. There is essentially no sell pressure: ORCA is a fixed-supply governance token, so the protocol issues no new coins, and every original team, investor and treasury allocation finished vesting back in 2024. The only active force is on the buy side — a 24-month treasury buyback that spends **40%** of protocol fees buying ORCA on the open market. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **−0.29%**, almost exactly matching the framework, so this ships **clean — no monitor-gap chip**. ORCA is **structurally non-inflationary**, with a buyback that slowly trims the float.

## Sell pressure: where new ORCA comes from

The short answer is that it does not. Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**. ORCA is a fixed-supply token with a 100M ceiling; there is no block emission, no staking-reward mint, no continuous issuance. In fact the supply has moved the other way: **25M ORCA** were permanently burned from the community treasury in April 2025, cutting total supply to roughly 75M.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is also **zero**: the team, private-sale and advisory allocations followed a three-year vesting with a one-year cliff, and that schedule finished in 2024. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a flow. About **39M ORCA** still sits in the DAO treasury, but that is not a vesting overhang; any movement is a discretionary governance grant, and no dated treasury release falls inside the window. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where ORCA goes

Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is the only active force on the page, at roughly **0.2M ORCA** of net float removed over 90 days. The Orca DAO runs a 24-month treasury program that spends a share of protocol fees buying ORCA on the open market; in January 2026 the council **raised that share from 20% to 40%** of fee revenue. The bought-back ORCA is deposited as rewards for stakers rather than sent to a burn address, so the permanent float reduction is smaller than the gross buy — coins re-enter circulation as stakers claim them.

Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is **zero**: because the buyback currently routes to stakers, no ORCA is permanently destroyed today (the 25M burn was a one-time treasury action). Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero.

## Treasury and overhang

ORCA has no vesting overhang left — the schedule is done. What it has instead is a large, governed treasury: roughly **39M ORCA** held by the DAO, which the community has already shown it will **burn** when it wants to (the 25M reduction in 2025). This makes the treasury a two-way lever: it can add float through grants, or remove it through burns and buybacks. None of those moves is on a fixed calendar, so the framework books no discretionary release beyond the buyback and re-checks the quarterly buyback reports and on-chain treasury balance on a roughly bi-weekly walk. If the treasury balance falls faster than a known grant, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How ORCA compares to other DEX governance tokens

ORCA belongs to the class of **fixed-supply DEX governance tokens** — closer to a capped, no-emission asset than to an inflationary chain token. Unlike a proof-of-stake L1 that mints rewards every block, ORCA issues nothing; unlike a token still bleeding through a multi-year unlock, its vesting is complete. That puts the whole inflation question on the buy side.

Against peers, ORCA reads cleaner than most. Many exchange tokens pair real fee revenue with a buyback but still carry an unlock tail that offsets it; ORCA has the buyback without the tail. The contrast worth drawing is with tokens whose buyback is a true burn — those go more firmly net-deflationary. ORCA's buyback routes to stakers, so its supply effect is gentler: it trims the open-market float rather than destroying it. For an inflation lens, that means ORCA reads as flat-to-slightly-deflationary — no dilution to fight, a modest buyback nudging supply down.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the quarterly buyback reports — the share of protocol fees going to buybacks (now 40%) and Orca's trading volume together decide how much ORCA leaves the market each month. Watch for any governance vote that turns the buyback into a burn, which would tip ORCA from mildly to firmly deflationary. Watch the DAO treasury, the one source that could add float through a grant. And expect the framework to keep tracking our supply monitor closely, because with no mint and no unlocks, the realized and modeled numbers move together.
