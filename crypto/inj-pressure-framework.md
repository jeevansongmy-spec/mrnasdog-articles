## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **INJ at +1.0% net** on the forward view, driven by staking inflation that out-issues the recurring burn. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+0.22%**, versus the framework's **+1.04%** staking-emission read for the same window — a gap of about **0.8 percentage points** that ships a **⚠ monitor-gap chip**. The gap is structural: Injective is a proof-of-stake chain with roughly **57% of supply bonded**, and the freshly minted INJ paid to stakers is largely re-staked rather than sold, so on-chain float grows far more slowly than the gross mint. INJ is **structurally inflationary on the active float**, at a low-single-digit pace that the burn only partly offsets.

## Sell pressure: where new INJ comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is the whole story, at about **1.19M INJ** over the next 90 days. Injective mints new INJ every block to reward stakers, on a dynamic rate that targets a **60%** bonded ratio: when less than that is staked, inflation rises toward a cap; when more is staked, it falls. Bonded supply currently sits around 51%, below the goal, so the chain's mint module reads the rate at its **4.4%** yearly cap, with the floor at 2.2%. At that rate the chain's annual provisions of about 4.84M INJ work out to roughly 1.19M over a 90-day window — all of it newly issued supply, counted once, here.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is **zero**: every original team, seed, private-sale and ecosystem allocation finished vesting by January 2024, so INJ is fully unlocked and no cliff hits the market. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is also zero as a flow; the token is fully circulating with no remaining scheduled unlock pool. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where new INJ goes

Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is the only active offset, at about **0.15M INJ** over 90 days. Injective channels a share of ecosystem revenue into a recurring buy-and-burn: a monthly Community BuyBack round, alongside the long-running weekly burn auction, where INJ is bought and permanently destroyed on-chain. Recent monthly rounds grew from about **37K** to **55K INJ** burned, and governance proposal IIP-617 (the Supply Squeeze) doubled the deflation rate. Because the destination is a burn, those coins are gone for good. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is zero as a separate line: Injective's buying is paired directly with the burn, not accumulated in a treasury, so it is booked once under the fee burn. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero.

## Foundation and overhang

INJ has no classic unlock overhang — the token has been fully distributed since early 2024, with no team, seed or ecosystem cliff left to release. The only structural source of new supply is the staking inflation itself, paid continuously to validators and delegators. That issuance is not a stockpile waiting to dump; the large majority of it re-bonds into staking, which is exactly why realized float grows slower than the gross mint. The framework books no discretionary release beyond protocol inflation and re-checks the burn rounds and chain emission on a roughly bi-weekly walk; if a treasury or staking balance falls faster than the schedule, the outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How INJ compares to other uncapped proof-of-stake chains

INJ belongs to the class of **uncapped Cosmos-SDK proof-of-stake L1s with dynamic staking inflation** — closer to a continuous-emission chain than to a hard-capped, halving-model coin. Unlike a fixed-supply token, INJ has no ceiling; unlike a pure inflation chain, it pairs the staking mint with a revenue-funded burn that scales with on-chain finance activity. The dynamic rate is the distinctive feature: most Cosmos chains let inflation float between a floor and a cap based on the bonded ratio, and INJ does the same, with its Supply Squeeze upgrade pulling those bounds down over time.

The contrast worth drawing is with exchange tokens that burn aggressively enough to go net-deflationary. INJ's burn is real but modest relative to the staking mint — about an eighth of the gross issuance over this window — so it slows dilution rather than reversing it. For an inflation lens, that means INJ reads as steadily, mildly inflationary on the active float: the staking mint is the dominant force, and the burn is a partial brake.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the monthly Community BuyBack rounds — the next ones land around **Jul 13 2026**, **Aug 13 2026** and **Sep 13 2026**, and their growing size is the single number that decides whether the burn keeps pace with the mint. Watch the bonded ratio: because inflation is dynamic, more staking pushes the rate down toward the 2.2% floor and less staking lifts it toward the 4.4% cap. Note any governance vote that lowers the inflation bounds further under the Supply Squeeze schedule. And expect the framework to keep reading above our supply monitor for as long as staking rewards re-bond rather than reach the float — that gap is structural, not a new unlock.
