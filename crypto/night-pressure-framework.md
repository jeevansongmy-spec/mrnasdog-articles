## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window running July 14 2026 to October 12 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **NIGHT at 0.00% net** — a genuinely flat supply. Every ledger row is zero: there is no protocol mint, no in-window vesting cliff, no buyback and no burn. Our supply monitor reads the realized last-90-day change at **+0.02%**, a difference of about **0.02 percentage points** — well inside tolerance, so **no monitor-gap chip** ships. The window sits entirely after Midnight's federated mainnet went live on March 31 2026, which confirms the block-reward reserve is not yet emitting to the market. NIGHT is best characterised as a **fully-minted, hard-capped token with a large dormant overhang** — steady today, with the direction of travel set by when that overhang begins to move.

## Sell pressure: where new NIGHT comes from

The honest answer is that no new NIGHT comes from anywhere right now. Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**. All 24B NIGHT were minted up front and the Midnight protocol has no mint function, so new coins can never be created above the cap. Block producers are rewarded from a fixed reserve on a disinflationary curve, but that reserve is not yet reaching the market in Midnight's early phase, so no fresh supply lands on the float.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is also **zero** in net terms, and this is the subtle part of NIGHT. The community airdrop — the Glacier Drop and the Scavenger Mine, roughly **4.5B** NIGHT claimed — thaws in four equal quarterly tranches through December 4 2026, and the third tranche does fall inside this window. But those coins are already counted in circulating supply and have been tradable since December 4 2025, so each thaw frees **already-circulating** NIGHT into a sellable form rather than adding new supply. It is a liquidity event, not an issuance event. Sell #3 — foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a flow, though it carries the whole overhang story below. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero, because no bankruptcy estate or court distribution applies to NIGHT.

## Buy pressure: where new NIGHT goes

There is no active buy pressure either, which is why the net reads flat rather than negative. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — is **zero**: no protocol or treasury mechanism buys NIGHT back off the open market. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is **zero** and always will be: NIGHT is never burned. Transaction fees on Midnight consume DUST, a separate non-transferable resource that holding NIGHT generates over time — the NIGHT supply itself has no burn path. Buy #3 — foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero, with no discretionary market buying and no new escrow of circulating NIGHT announced in the window. Staking NIGHT earns rewards and faster DUST generation, but it is not a supply lock. With every sell row and every buy row at zero, the framework's net is exactly flat.

## Foundation and overhang

This is where NIGHT's real supply question lives. Roughly **7.4B** NIGHT — the gap between the 24B cap and the ~16.6B circulating — sits outside the float as a loom. It breaks into a few parts: a roughly **6B** protocol reserve and team-and-ecosystem allocation on a multi-year vesting schedule, a smaller undistributed treasury, and a **~252M** Lost-and-Found pool holding unclaimed airdrop allocations on a five-year recovery window. None of these has a dated release inside the next 90 days — the first team-and-investor cliff is not due until around December 4 2026, outside this window — so each is booked at zero and monitored. The trigger is mechanical: if the reserve begins paying block rewards, or the treasury or Lost-and-Found pool deploys tokens between refreshes, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh — and NIGHT stops reading flat.

## How NIGHT compares to other fully-minted, hard-capped chains

NIGHT belongs to the class of **fully-minted, hard-capped tokens released through a community airdrop** — closer to a distributed governance token than to a continuous-emission proof-of-stake chain. Unlike an uncapped chain that mints new coins every block, NIGHT can never grow past 24B; unlike an exchange token with a fee-burn or a revenue buyback, it has no mechanism to shrink supply either, so it cannot go deflationary. Its supply can only travel in one direction over time — up, toward the cap — as the dormant reserve and treasury gradually enter the float.

The sharper contrast is with a token whose whole float is already liquid, like a fully-distributed fixed-supply coin. NIGHT looks flat today for the same reason those coins do — nothing is being minted, bought or burned — but it carries a much larger latent overhang: about **31%** of the cap is still off-market. So while the current reading matches a static-supply token, the forward risk is closer to a project mid-way through its vesting, just without a fully published cliff calendar. Because the reserve's reward curve is disinflationary, when emission does begin it starts higher and tapers, rather than arriving as one large unlock.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the mainnet decentralization milestone: the moment Midnight moves past its federated Kūkolu phase and the block-reward reserve starts paying public rewards is the single event that flips Sell #1 from zero to a live emission. Watch the quarterly Glacier Drop thaw running through December 4 2026 — it does not change the circulating count, but it governs how much claimed NIGHT becomes freely sellable each quarter, and the next tranche lands inside this window. Watch the on-chain treasury for any first deployment. And watch the ~252M Lost-and-Found pool, whose unclaimed allocations can drift into circulation as late claims are processed across the recovery window.

## Summary

NIGHT is a fully-minted, hard-capped community token with no mint, no buyback and no burn, so the Pressure Framework reads it at 0.00% net over the next 90 days — flat, and matching our monitor's +0.02% to within noise. The real question is not today's flow but the ~7.4B non-circulating overhang: a roughly 6B reserve and team-and-ecosystem allocation, an undistributed treasury, and a ~252M unclaimed pool. As long as those stay locked, NIGHT's supply is steady; the first sustained reserve emission or treasury deployment is what would turn this flat read into mild inflation.
