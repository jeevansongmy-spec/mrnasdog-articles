Midnight's NIGHT has a **fixed 24B cap** and mints nothing — it is non-inflationary by design. Yet the Pressure Framework reads about **+15% net** over 90 days, because the Glacier Drop airdrop is still thawing — an estimated **2.5B NIGHT** moves into circulation while **nothing** on the buy side removes it. The pressure is distribution, not issuance, and it ends when the thaw completes.

## The verdict, in one paragraph

For the 90-day window ending June 16 2026, the MrNasdog Pressure Framework reads **NIGHT at about +15% net** on the forward view, driven entirely by airdrop unlocks with no offsetting buy pressure. Our supply monitor has **no usable read** on NIGHT yet — the token only launched on Cardano in December 2025 — so the realized cross-check is deferred to a future refresh, and the framework leans on primary thaw-schedule data for now. The headline number looks extreme, but it is a one-time distribution effect against a fixed total, not a perpetual inflation rate: NIGHT is **structurally non-inflationary, temporarily flooding on its airdrop**.

## Sell pressure: where new NIGHT comes from

Sell #1 — protocol inflation — is **zero**, and that is the most important fact about NIGHT. The token has a hard 24B cap and the network mints nothing; transaction fees are paid in a separate, decaying resource that holding NIGHT generates, so NIGHT itself is never issued to validators or stakers. There is no emission curve, no halving, no tail inflation.

Sell #2 — vesting unlocks — is the entire story, at an estimated **2.5B NIGHT** over the next 90 days. The Glacier Drop airdrop, which assigned 80% of supply to holders across Cardano, Bitcoin and six other ecosystems, unlocks in four equal quarterly steps per allocation, with redemption running from December 2025 to December 4 2026. Claimed tokens thaw a quarter at a time and unclaimed tokens keep entering as people redeem, so circulation climbs steadily through the year. The exact 90-day figure is staggered — each address gets a randomized first-thaw date — so this is a reasoned estimate, not an exact count. Sell #3 — Foundation and unscheduled unlocks — is zero as a flow: roughly 20% of supply held by the reserve, team and ecosystem sits on an 18–24 month vesting that mostly extends past this window. Sell #4 — long-term locked or bankruptcy — is zero.

## Buy pressure: where new NIGHT goes

Every buy row is **zero**, and that is by design. Buy #1 — programmatic buyback — does not exist; the project runs no open-market purchase program. Buy #2 — protocol fee burn — is also zero, because fees are paid in the separate decaying resource, not in NIGHT, so network usage never destroys a single NIGHT. Buy #3 — Foundation buy — and Buy #4 — new long-term lock — are both zero. With nothing on the buy side, the thaw inflow translates almost one-for-one into net supply growth.

## Foundation and overhang

NIGHT's overhang is the airdrop itself. About 16.6B of the 24B cap is already circulating, leaving roughly **7.4B** still frozen, unclaimed or held in the reserve and team allocations. The reserve, team and ecosystem share — close to 20% of supply — carries an 18–24 month vesting, while the bulk of the remaining airdrop thaws through the rest of 2026. None of this is a treasury that can dump at will; it is a published, scheduled release. If a reserve or team balance falls faster than its schedule, that outflow enters Sell #3 at the next refresh.

## How NIGHT compares to other fixed-cap distribution tokens

NIGHT belongs to the class of **hard-capped, non-inflationary tokens in their airdrop-distribution phase** — closer to a large fair-launch unlock than to a continuously-emitting proof-of-stake chain. Unlike an uncapped L1 that mints new coins to validators forever, NIGHT will stop growing entirely once the airdrop is fully thawed and claimed; its supply curve flattens permanently at 24B. Unlike an exchange token that burns supply back down, it has no buyback and no fee burn, so there is no mechanism pulling supply the other way during the unlock.

The right mental model is a one-time release, not a rate. A typical proof-of-stake L1 reads a low-single-digit net inflation every quarter, indefinitely; NIGHT reads a high double-digit number for a few quarters and then stops. The privacy design reinforces this — because fees burn a separate decaying resource rather than NIGHT, the token is engineered to be held, not spent, and its long-run supply is fully predetermined. The current pressure is the cost of getting tokens into the market, paid once.

## What to watch in the next 90 days

Watch the redemption pace — the share of the 4.5B-plus claimed tokens that actually thaw and reach the market is the single number that sets how heavy the next quarter is. Watch the Dec 4 2026 end of the thawing period and the Nov 29 2026 thaw-complete date, after which new unlock pressure drops sharply. Watch for the first reserve or team vesting steps, which would add a Sell #3 flow if they begin inside a future window. And watch for a usable supply-monitor read as exchange and on-chain data on NIGHT matures — until then the realized cross-check stays deferred and the framework leans on the published schedule.

## Summary

NIGHT is a fixed-cap, non-inflationary privacy-network token that mints nothing, yet reads about +15% net over 90 days because its Glacier Drop airdrop is still thawing an estimated 2.5B into circulation with no buyback or burn to offset it. The pressure is one-time distribution, not perpetual issuance, and it eases as the thaw completes through late 2026. The key risk is the unlock overhang — roughly 7.4B still to enter against 16.6B circulating — and the ceiling is hard: once the airdrop and the 18–24 month reserve vesting finish, supply stops at 24B for good.
